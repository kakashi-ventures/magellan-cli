The Experiment

What if the next scientific breakthrough is already published — just scattered across papers that no single researcher reads together?

MAGELLAN is an experiment in whether modern agentic AI systems can make autonomous scientific discoveries. The core hypothesis: a well-designed multi-agent architecture running frontier models (March 2026) can find real connections between existing bodies of knowledge that humans have not yet linked — operating in full autonomy, with zero domain input from the user.

It is not a tool for researchers. It is a capability test. The user runs /discover, leaves the room, and comes back to find testable scientific hypotheses. These are then cross-model validated by GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1, and ideally submitted to domain experts for evaluation.

Built for Claude Code with Opus 4.6 (deep reasoning) and Sonnet 4.6 (structured tasks), the system deploys 15 specialized AI agents that read across disciplinary boundaries, identify where established knowledge in one field connects to another through an unexplored bridge, and generate testable hypotheses about what that connection means. Then it attacks its own ideas — adversarially, rigorously — and presents only the survivors.

An honest framing

We don't know yet if it works. We believe multi-agent AI can find connections humans miss — but every hypothesis here needs testing by real scientists. We're not claiming discoveries. We're claiming a system that generates hypotheses worth investigating.

The confidence scores are intentionally moderate. The kill rate is intentionally high (70–86%). Every claim is tagged as GROUNDED, PARAMETRIC, or SPECULATIVE. We label our uncertainty because that's what makes this credible.

Conceptual Foundation: Swanson's Undiscovered Public Knowledge

At its core, MAGELLAN is a machine for finding Undiscovered Public Knowledge (UPK) — a concept formulated by Don R. Swanson in 1986.

The concept

Swanson observed that the scientific literature contains logical connections that no one has made yet — not because the information is secret, but because it resides in disjoint literatures: bodies of knowledge that never cite each other. The foundational example: the literature on magnesium (vasodilatory effects, platelet anti-aggregation) and the literature on migraine (vasoconstriction, platelet aggregation) contained all the premises to hypothesize that magnesium could treat migraine. No one had made the connection because the two fields did not talk to each other. Swanson made it in 1988, and subsequent clinical trials confirmed the hypothesis.

Swanson formalized this as the ABC model: Field A and Field C do not cite each other, but they share an intermediate concept B (the bridge term) that appears in both. Discovery consists of identifying B and constructing the inference A→B→C.

Today there are over 100 million published papers. The fragments are everywhere. The connections between them — the ones that lead to new mechanisms, new treatments, new understanding — go unnoticed for years, sometimes decades.

From bibliometrics to parametric knowledge

Swanson's original method was bibliometric: co-occurrence analysis of MeSH terms, citation graphs, frequency matrices. This approach is superseded by 2026 frontier models. The UPK problem exists because no human researcher can read both literatures. An LLM trained on millions of papers has already absorbed both. The problem is no longer detecting citation disjunction — it is eliciting the latent connections from the model's parametric knowledge.

The evidence confirms this: none of the AI-driven breakthroughs of 2025–2026 (GPT-5 + Ginkgo, Google AI Co-Scientist, Aletheia) used bibliometric analysis. All of them use parametric knowledge + multi-agent architecture + debate/evolution.

Self-review before output

Six agents have reflection loops before producing final output: the Generator verifies its own mechanism specificity (SELF-CRITIQUE), the Critic calibrates its kill rate (META-CRITIQUE), the Scout checks bridge specificity (TARGET QUALITY CHECK), the Quality Gate verifies confidence in its PASS verdicts (META-VALIDATION), and both the Literature Scout and Evolver verify output completeness.

Adaptive cycles

The pipeline adapts to output quality: if cycle 1 produces top-3 scores ≥ 7.0, it completes early (skip cycle 2). If survival drops below 30%, it extends to cycle 3. If top-3 ≥ 6.5 with good diversity after cycle 2, it skips the Evolver. Better models produce higher-quality output earlier — the system doesn't waste compute on unnecessary refinement.

The Hybrid Paradigm: Parametric Generation + Retrieval Validation

Frontier models (Opus 4.6, GPT-5.2, Gemini 3.1 Pro) score 91–94% on GPQA Diamond — PhD-level questions in biology, physics, and chemistry. This is a 55-point jump from GPT-4 (39%, 2023). Parametric knowledge has improved enormously. However: open factual recall reaches only 55%, hallucination rates range 22–48%, and open research scores only 25.3%. Peer-reviewed literature behind paywalls is largely absent from training data.

Parametric knowledge is the generative engine — it is where the non-obvious cross-disciplinary connections reside. But every factual claim is verified through retrieval. Neither approach alone is sufficient — parametric knowledge finds connections, retrieval keeps them honest.

The flow through the pipeline:

  1. Scout (parametric) — Identifies WHERE to look using 10 strategies. Produces mandatory bridge concepts. Generates 5–6 candidates. Consults meta-insights from prior sessions
  2. Literature Scout (verification) — Verifies disjointness for ALL candidates using MCP servers (Semantic Scholar, PubMed) as mandatory first step, WebSearch as fallback. Retrieves full text for top 5–10 papers
  3. Target Evaluator (adversarial) — Challenges 3 filtered targets on 4 axes: popularity bias, vagueness, structural impossibility, local-optima
  4. Computational Validator — KEGG pathway cross-check, STRING protein interactions, PubMed co-occurrence counts, back-of-envelope physics. Catches impossible mechanisms early
  5. Generator (parametric + context) — Builds an on-the-fly parametric Knowledge Graph for each field, then generates hypotheses using deep reasoning + full papers + computational signals
  6. Critic (adversarial + web) — 9 attack vectors: web-searches EVERY [GROUNDED] claim individually. Citation hallucination = automatic KILL
  7. Ranker — 6-dimension weighted scoring including Groundedness (20%), diversity check, Elo tournament sanity check
  8. Cross-Model Validator — GPT-5.4 Pro (web search + code interpreter) and Gemini 3.1 Pro (code execution + Google Search grounding) provide genuinely independent assessment

The Scout: 10 Strategies

How the Scout decides where to look. Each session must use at least 2 different strategies, with at least 1 from a strategy with fewer than 2 prior sessions of data (the exploration slot).

  1. Recent Breakthrough Radiation — Traces implications of recent discoveries into non-obvious distant fields. What does this finding change about something nobody has thought to connect?
  2. Anomaly Hunting — Targets reproducible but unexplained phenomena — reliable observations that resist explanation under current paradigms
  3. Converging Vocabularies — Finds fields developing similar mathematical frameworks or conceptual language independently, suggesting hidden shared structure
  4. Tool Transfer Opportunities — Identifies analytical tools, methods, or instruments from one field that could solve open problems in an unrelated domain
  5. Scale Bridging Gaps — Connects phenomena well-understood at one scale (molecular, cellular, organismal, ecological) to adjacent scales where the same logic hasn't been applied
  6. Failed Paradigm Recycling — Revisits ideas abandoned in their original field — they may work in a completely different context where the original failure conditions don't apply
  7. Swanson ABC Bridging — Systematic identification of literatures with shared intermediate concepts (B) but no direct citations between them (A and C). The classic discovery method, operationalized
  8. Contradiction Mining — Active search for contradictions between fields as sources of novel hypotheses. If two papers make mutually exclusive claims, resolving the contradiction often reveals a non-trivial connection. Inspired by FutureHouse's ContraCrow
  9. Structural Isomorphism Discovery — Fields sharing identical mathematical structure (equations, network topology, information-theoretic constraints) but completely different physical substrates. The bridge IS the math itself. Domain-agnostic. Example: percolation theory connects epidemiology and fracture mechanics
  10. Serendipity Through Random Encounter — Deliberate exposure to unexpected knowledge: pick a domain NEVER explored before, find its most surprising discovery, ask "which distant field would be most transformed?" Must cross 2+ disciplinary boundaries

Bridge concepts are mandatory for every target, not just for the Swanson strategy. Even for Anomaly Hunting or Scale Bridging, the Scout must articulate the concrete connection mechanism. A rotating creativity constraint (mod 5 per session) forces exploration of diverse territory: cross-discipline bridge, mathematical bridge, temporal gap >50 years, tool transfer, or unsolved problem.

Before each session, the Scout consults meta-insights from prior sessions to avoid re-exploration, reuse productive bridges, and prioritize strategies with higher survival rates. The system learns which approaches are most fruitful.

The Critic: 9 Attack Vectors

The Critic is genuinely adversarial. Its objective is to destroy weak hypotheses — killing 50–70% is normal and healthy. A 0% kill rate is a red flag.

  1. Novelty Kill — WebSearch to verify whether the connection is already published or well-studied
  2. Mechanism Kill — Physical/chemical/biological plausibility: energy scales, time constants, concentrations
  3. Logic Kill — Correlation passed off as causation, analogy confused with structural relationship, post-hoc reasoning
  4. Falsifiability Kill — If it cannot be proven false with a specific experiment → KILL
  5. Triviality Kill — Would a PhD student in each field say "obviously"?
  6. Counter-Evidence Search — Dedicated WebSearch for contradictions and mechanism failures in published literature
  7. Groundedness Attack — Distinguishes literature-grounded claims from parametric knowledge from pure speculation. If >50% of core claims are unverifiable → significant downgrade
  8. Hallucination-as-Novelty Check — "Does it appear new because genuinely unexplored, or because it is wrong in non-obvious ways?" Directly targets the finding that AI novelty scores collapse from 5.38 → 3.41 after experimental validation (Si et al., ICLR 2026)
  9. Claim-Level Fact Verification — Web-searches EVERY individual [GROUNDED] claim: author+year+journal, directionality, protein properties. Citation hallucination or fabricated data = automatic KILL

After all attacks, the Critic runs a META-CRITIQUE: (1) calibrates its own kill rate, (2) for each hypothesis that SURVIVES, writes the strongest reason it should have been killed, (3) verifies web search completeness. When a mechanism is too vague, the Critic writes specific questions that the Orchestrator forwards to the Generator in cycle 2 — creating an indirect feedback loop.

A +0.5 cross-domain creativity bonus is applied for hypotheses crossing 2+ disciplinary boundaries, compensating for the systematic penalization from bio-centric infrastructure. An Elo tournament (15 pairwise comparisons among top-6) cross-checks the linear ranking — inspired by Google AI Co-Scientist, which found superior correlation with expert evaluations. A diversity check prevents convergence: if 3+ of the top-5 share the same bridge mechanism, the duplicates are demoted.

Impact enters the pipeline as a parallel signal, never as a substitute for quality. The Impact Potential Score (IPS) combines the Scout's estimate (40%) with the Convergence Scanner's translational signals (60%).

Why Life Sciences as Primary Domain

MAGELLAN is structurally optimized for cross-disciplinary discovery in the life sciences. This is not an arbitrary choice — three factors converge:

  • Structural fragmentation — The life sciences have the highest density of latent "missing links." Sub-disciplines like the microbiome, chronobiology, epigenetics, immunology, and neuroscience operate with largely disjoint literatures despite shared molecular mechanisms. This is exactly the terrain of Swanson's UPK
  • Available retrieval infrastructure — PubMed (38M+ articles with structured abstracts), KEGG (metabolic and signaling pathways), STRING (protein interactions) provide structured grounding and programmatic queries. No comparable equivalent exists for theoretical physics or mathematics
  • Rapid falsifiability — Cross-disciplinary biomedical hypotheses are testable with wet-lab experiments or computational analyses on existing datasets (GEO, TCGA, UK Biobank). Physics or math hypotheses often require years of formal development

Score interpretation guide

Non-bio hypothesis scores are not directly comparable with bio scores. A theoretical physics hypothesis scoring 5.5 may be qualitatively equivalent to a biomedical hypothesis scoring 7.0 — the gap is primarily in Testability (no public datasets for rapid validation), Groundedness (no MCP server for structured retrieval), and Mechanistic Specificity (formal vs. molecular mechanisms). The cross-domain creativity bonus (+0.5) partially compensates.

Operational Robustness

The pipeline must operate without human supervision. Robustness operates on three levels:

1. Guard logic

Conditional blocks after every phase that verify output. The flow is always: verify → retry → fallback → abort. If the Scout produces 0 targets, it retries with lower thresholds, then falls back to hardcoded parametric targets. If the Generator produces too few hypotheses, a blocking gate forces re-execution. If the Critic kills everything, the system regenerates with different mechanisms and more conservative claims — failing the session explicitly if it happens twice.

2. Per-agent quality gates

Each agent has a dedicated quality gate that blocks insufficient output. The Ranker's gate requires at least 3KB of detailed scoring (preventing thin output without proper justification). The Literature Scout degrades gracefully if retrieval services are unavailable. The Critic's gate is warn-only — it can legitimately kill everything. A master gate prevents premature pipeline termination and verifies that all required agents were dispatched.

3. Session health classification

Every session terminates with an explicit status:

  • SUCCESS: ≥2 hypotheses passed Quality Gate with Groundedness ≥5
  • PARTIAL: 1 hypothesis passed, or all have low Groundedness
  • DEGRADED: Pipeline completed, 0 pass Quality Gate (cards presented with warning)
  • FAILED: Pipeline non-completable (0 targets, all killed, agent error)

State is maintained in structured JSON — a slim coordination index (~3KB) separate from per-session results. Each agent re-reads state at dispatch, making the pipeline resilient to context compaction in long runs.

Where MAGELLAN differs

  • Autonomous target selection — Nearly all other systems require a human to define the research question. MAGELLAN's Scout autonomously decides what to explore, using 10 formalized discovery strategies that no other system implements as an explicit repertoire
  • Cross-model validation, not self-review — Most systems use same-model critique. MAGELLAN sends hypotheses to competing LLMs from different providers for genuinely independent assessment
  • Formal Groundedness scoring (20%) — Not present as an explicit dimension in other systems. Prevents fluent hallucinations from scoring high
  • Open source and contributor-owned — Most competitors are proprietary or enterprise-only. Anyone with a Claude subscription can run discoveries, attributed to their profile

What others do better

  • Experimental validation: Google and FutureHouse have wet-lab confirmed discoveries. MAGELLAN generates testable hypotheses with protocols but has no pathway to physical validation
  • Proprietary data: BenevolentAI's 350M+ relationship KG finds things public APIs miss. MAGELLAN uses only public sources
  • Formal verification: DeepMind's systems can mathematically prove discoveries correct. MAGELLAN's quality rubric is rigorous but not formally verifiable

Philosophy of autonomy

/discover with no arguments is the primary mode. The entire project exists to answer: "Can an agentic AI system autonomously find real scientific connections?"

This differentiates MAGELLAN from Google AI Co-Scientist (scientist-in-the-loop) and FutureHouse Kosmos (human-defined objectives). MAGELLAN is more ambitious: the Scout must autonomously decide what is interesting — a capability Demis Hassabis believes is still 5–10 years away for AI (Lex Fridman Podcast #475, July 2025: "in the next maybe five to ten years we'll have systems capable of not only solving an important problem in science but coming up with it in the first place").

Modes for domain scientists

The --context, --papers, and --interactive flags are designed for scientists with domain expertise who want to direct discovery without controlling it. A ferroptosis researcher can write --context "I study ferroptosis in hepatocytes" and the Scout uses this expertise to inform target selection — without restricting itself exclusively to that domain. The value of serendipity is preserved: the Scout might find connections the scientist would never have sought.

What Makes Full Autonomy Possible (March 2026)

Running a fully autonomous scientific discovery pipeline would not have been feasible 18 months ago. Seven capabilities converge in March 2026:

  1. Scout with 10 formalized strategies — It does not choose randomly. Structural isomorphism, bisociation, serendipity, and 7 other strategies are elicitation mechanisms designed to surface where unlinked knowledge is most likely hiding
  2. Parallel Literature Scout — Real-time verification that targets are not already explored, providing literature context to enrich generation. MCP servers (Semantic Scholar, PubMed) enable structured retrieval independent of generic web search
  3. Opus 4.6 time horizon: 14h30m (METR) — Sustained autonomy at this level is a new capability. No pre-2026 model could operate coherently over these durations
  4. GPT-5.4 as external validator — 33% fewer factual errors vs GPT-5.2, with web search and code interpreter for exhaustive literature grounding. Reduces the risk of Claude validating its own hallucinations
  5. Auto Mode — Eliminates permission interruptions, allowing the pipeline to flow without intervention across 15–45 minute sessions
  6. JSON-based state management — Survives context compaction, maintaining coherence over long runs. Each agent re-reads state at dispatch
  7. Growing with AI — The architecture is designed so that as models improve, every stage becomes more powerful: deeper reasoning → deeper connections, fewer hallucinations → higher groundedness, broader knowledge → more diverse targets. Every model improvement is a MAGELLAN improvement

Design Principles

  • Parametric + Retrieval — Frontier LLMs generate cross-domain connections from internal knowledge. PubMed, KEGG, STRING, and Semantic Scholar validate every factual claim. Neither approach alone is sufficient
  • Groundedness scoring (20% weight) — Prevents fluent hallucinations from scoring high. Every [GROUNDED] claim is verified against real papers. The 22–48% hallucination rate in frontier models makes this essential
  • Mandatory agent dispatch — The Orchestrator cannot execute phases inline. Each phase is dispatched to a specialized agent with defined tool access and fresh context. This prevents monolithic LLM behavior
  • Cross-model validation — Survivors are independently assessed by competing LLMs from different providers. Cross-model consensus increases confidence; disagreement flags areas for investigation
  • Session-to-session meta-learning — Strategy performance, kill patterns, and bridge survival rates are tracked across sessions. The Scout reads this before each session to focus on what works
  • Radical transparency — Every claim is tagged GROUNDED, PARAMETRIC, or SPECULATIVE. We publish kill rates, confidence scores, counter-evidence, and cross-model assessments. We show what the system rejects. If it can't survive scrutiny, it shouldn't be published

Empirical Validation

The validation vs. effectiveness conflict

Formally validating that MAGELLAN works would require "blinding" the system (no web search, parametric only), but maximum effectiveness requires every tool. The solution: completely separate the two objectives.

Track 1: Production pipeline enrichment

Two post-Quality-Gate agents consult sources never used by the main pipeline:

  • Convergence Scanner: Searches ClinicalTrials.gov (active trials), NIH Reporter (funded grants), and patents for independent convergence signals. Finding that someone else is already pursuing a related mechanism increases confidence
  • Dataset Evidence Miner: Verifies specific molecular claims against 5+ bioinformatics databases — Human Protein Atlas (tissue expression), GWAS Catalog (genetic associations), ChEMBL (compound-target), UniProt (protein function), PDB/AlphaFold (structure)
EES = dataset_score × 0.55 + convergence_score × 0.45

The Empirical Evidence Score runs parallel to the composite — it measures independent empirical evidence, not reasoning quality.

Track 2: Holdout Validation Framework

The retrodiction paradox: The Quality Gate searches the web with no temporal filter. If a post-cutoff paper confirms the mechanism, the QG kills it as "not novel" — the opposite of the desired outcome for validation.

Solution: Curate cross-disciplinary discoveries published after May 2025 (Claude's training cutoff). Give MAGELLAN only [Field A] × [Field C] in targeted mode. The pipeline runs normally — no handicap. Afterward, the Holdout Evaluator checks:

  • Contamination: Was the "answer" paper found by any agent? (grep DOI/PMID/title across all outputs)
  • Mechanism similarity: How close did MAGELLAN's hypotheses get to the known mechanism?

Verdicts: GENUINE_REDISCOVERY (uncontaminated + strong match), PARTIAL_REDISCOVERY, ADJACENT_DISCOVERY, CONTAMINATED, MISSED. This is more rigorous than blinding because it tests the REAL system and objectively measures contamination.
