# Discovery Output Licensing

This document explains how MAGELLAN's discovery outputs (scientific hypotheses)
are licensed. The **software** is licensed under Apache 2.0 (see [LICENSE](LICENSE)).
Discovery outputs have a separate, dual-track license described below.

## Why a Separate License for Outputs?

MAGELLAN generates scientific hypotheses using AI agents. The legal status of
AI-generated content varies by jurisdiction and is evolving rapidly. Rather than
assert uncertain rights, we use a dual-track approach that is honest about
copyright status while encouraging proper attribution.

## Dual-Track Licensing

### Track 1: Autonomous Discoveries — CC0 1.0 (Public Domain)

**Applies when:** `/discover` is run with no arguments or flags (fully autonomous
Scout mode — the AI decides what to explore with zero human creative input).

**License:** [CC0 1.0 Universal (Public Domain Dedication)](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/)

No copyright is claimed. These outputs are dedicated to the public domain.
Anyone may use, share, modify, or build upon them for any purpose without
restriction.

**Voluntary citation request:** While not legally required, we ask that you
cite MAGELLAN when using autonomous discoveries, following standard scientific
attribution norms:

> Generated by MAGELLAN (https://magellan-discover.ai), a project by
> Alberto Trivero / Kakashi Venture Accelerator. Session: [session-id].

### Track 2: Guided Discoveries — CC-BY 4.0 (Attribution Required)

**Applies when:** The user provides creative direction through any of:
- `/discover [Field A] x [Field C]` — targeted field selection
- `/discover [topic]` — open exploration with topic direction
- `/discover solve: [problem]` — problem-driven framing
- `--context "..."` — domain expertise context
- `--papers DOI1,DOI2` — curated seed papers
- `--interactive` — target approval decisions

**License:** [Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC-BY 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)

You are free to share and adapt these outputs for any purpose, including
commercial use, provided you give appropriate credit.

**Required attribution format:**

> Hypothesis generated by [contributor name] using MAGELLAN
> (https://magellan-discover.ai), a project by Alberto Trivero /
> Kakashi Venture Accelerator. Session: [session-id].

If running without a contributor connection (`/connect`), the contributor
name defaults to "Anonymous".

## How the License is Determined

The license is determined automatically at session initialization based on
the mode and flags used. The `output_license` field in session metadata
records the determination:

| Mode | Flags | License | Contributor Role |
|------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Scout (no args) | None | CC0-1.0 | runner |
| Scout | `--context` | CC-BY-4.0 | domain_expert |
| Scout | `--papers` | CC-BY-4.0 | director |
| Scout | `--interactive` | CC-BY-4.0 | director |
| Targeted | Any | CC-BY-4.0 | director |
| Open | Any | CC-BY-4.0 | director |
| Problem | Any | CC-BY-4.0 | director |

## Legal Context

### AI-Generated Content and Copyright

As of 2026, most jurisdictions (including the United States, European Union,
and United Kingdom) have not established clear copyright protection for
content generated autonomously by AI systems. The U.S. Copyright Office
requires human authorship for copyright registration.

Our dual-track approach reflects this reality:
- **Autonomous outputs (CC0):** We do not assert copyright over content
  where no human creative input was provided. CC0 is the honest choice.
- **Guided outputs (CC-BY 4.0):** When a human provides creative direction
  (field selection, domain context, paper curation, target approval), there
  is a stronger basis for copyright. CC-BY 4.0 recognizes this contribution
  while keeping outputs freely usable.

### Scientific Ideas vs. Expression

Scientific hypotheses as **ideas** are not copyrightable in any jurisdiction.
What can be protected is the **specific expression**: the particular wording
of the mechanism, the test protocol, the falsifiable predictions. CC-BY 4.0
applies to this expression in guided mode.

Researchers who independently arrive at similar hypotheses through their own
work are not constrained by this license.

### Disclaimer

MAGELLAN hypotheses are **scientific propositions, not verified findings**.
They have not been experimentally validated. Users are responsible for:
- Independent verification before acting on any hypothesis
- Checking the patent landscape before commercial development
- Conducting proper ethical review for any experimental validation
- Citing primary literature referenced in the hypotheses
