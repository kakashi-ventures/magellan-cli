# Matrix Stiffness and Stress Relaxation Regulate Osteogenesis Through Histone Demethylases KDM4B and KDM6B

## Key Findings

### Experimental System
- Mesenchymal stem cells on hydrogels of varying stiffness and stress relaxation profiles
- Epigenetic analysis of histone demethylase activity

### ECM Mechanics → Epigenetic Enzyme
- ECM **stiffness** and **stress relaxation** directly regulate activity of histone demethylases **KDM4B** and **KDM6B**
- KDM4B: H3K9me3/H3K9me2 demethylase → removes repressive marks
- KDM6B: H3K27me3 demethylase → removes Polycomb repressive marks (opens chromatin for activation)
- **Stress relaxation** (viscoelastic property) is a key driver — not just stiffness magnitude

### Mechanistic Link
- Stiffer ECM and faster stress relaxation → increased KDM6B activity → H3K27me3 removal → osteogenic gene activation
- This provides a direct link: ECM physical properties → specific histone demethylase → specific histone mark change → gene expression

### Significance
- Identifies specific epigenetic enzymes as mechanosensors
- KDM6B demethylates H3K27me3 — which is the mark that must be removed for enhancers to become active (H3K27me3 = poised/inactive enhancers; removal enables H3K27ac deposition)
- This creates a direct pipeline: stiff ECM → KDM6B → H3K27me3 removal at enhancers → H3K27ac deposition by p300 → enhancer activation
