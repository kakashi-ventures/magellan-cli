# Mechanosensitive Genomic Enhancers Potentiate the Cellular Response to Matrix Stiffness

**Authors**: Cosgrove BD, Bounds LR, Taylor CK, Su AL, Rizzo AJ, Barrera A, Sun T, Safi A, Song L, Whitlow T, Tata A, Iglesias N, Diao Y, Tata PR, Hoffman BD, Crawford GE, Gersbach CA
**Year**: 2025 (preprint Jan 2024)
**Journal**: Science
**PMID**: 40997217
**DOI**: 10.1126/science.adl1988
**PMC**: 13005951
**Status**: Peer-reviewed

## Abstract

Epigenetic control of gene expression and cellular phenotype is influenced by changes in the local microenvironment, yet how mechanical cues precisely influence epigenetic state to regulate transcription remains largely unmapped. In this study, we combined genome-wide epigenome profiling, epigenome editing, and phenotypic and single-cell RNA sequencing CRISPR screening to identify a class of genomic enhancers that responds to the mechanical microenvironment. These "mechanoenhancers" can be preferentially activated on either soft or stiff extracellular matrix contexts and regulate transcription to influence critical cell functions including apoptosis, adhesion, proliferation, and migration. Epigenetic editing of mechanoenhancers reprograms the cellular response to the mechanical microenvironment and modulates the activation of disease-related genes in lung fibroblasts from healthy and fibrotic donors. Epigenetic editing of mechanoenhancers holds potential for precise targeting of mechanically driven diseases.

## Key Findings

### Experimental System
- Human lung fibroblasts and A549 lung adenocarcinoma cells
- Polyacrylamide hydrogels: soft (1 kPa) vs stiff (50 kPa) substrates
- Methods: ATAC-seq, RNA-seq, CRISPRi screening, scRNA-seq CRISPR screening, Micro-C chromatin contact analysis, luciferase reporters, Cas9 saturation screening

### Mechanoenhancer Discovery
- First genome-wide identification of "mechanoenhancers" — a distinct class of genomic enhancers responsive to ECM stiffness
- Stiffness-dependent chromatin accessibility changes occur **within 1 hour** of contractile changes
- Contractility (actomyosin tension) is required to maintain stiffness-induced chromatin accessibility

### Stiff ECM Mechanoenhancers
- Enriched TF motifs: **TEAD** (YAP/TAZ), **AP-1**, **SP1**, **SRF/CaRG**
- Named enhancers: MYH9 intron 3 (myosin/contractility), CYR61 intergenic (YAP/TAZ target), CTGF (YAP/TAZ target), SKP2, RAD18, ANKRD1, RANGAP1

### Soft ECM Mechanoenhancers
- Enriched TF motifs: **AP-1** (distinct sequence variants from stiff), **RUNX1**, **FOXF1**, **CEBPE**
- Named enhancers: BMF intron 4 (anoikis/apoptosis), MMP14, PPARG

### Critical Gap Identified
- **Only 13.8% of mechanoenhancer-gene connections show annotated chromatin looping** (Micro-C)
- The dominant mechanism linking mechanoenhancers to gene expression is NOT canonical enhancer-promoter looping
- Alternative mechanisms (phase separation, RNA-mediated, TF scanning) are unexplored

### Disease Relevance
- Mechanoenhancer-connected genes are upregulated in pathogenic fibroblast populations from IPF (idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis) patients
- Epigenetic editing (CRISPRi/CRISPRa) of mechanoenhancers reprograms fibrosis gene expression even in presence of stiff mechanical input
- Proposed therapeutic target for mechanically-driven diseases: fibrosis, cancer, atherosclerosis
