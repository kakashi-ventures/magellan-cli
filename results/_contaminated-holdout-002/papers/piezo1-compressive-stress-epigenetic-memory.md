## Key Findings

### Piezo1 as Epigenetic Mechanosensor
- **Piezo1** is the primary mechanosensory channel activated by compressive stress
- Compressive stress → Piezo1 → Ca²⁺ influx → **Rho-ROCK activation**
- Rho-ROCK → **histone acetylation at H3K residues** → open chromatin
- Open chromatin at cancer-related genes → transcriptional activation

### Epigenetic Mechanical Memory
- Cells **retain the epigenetic imprint** after mechanical force removal
- H3K acetylation established by compression persists beyond the mechanical stimulus
- This constitutes "epigenetic mechanical memory" — mechanical force permanently alters chromatin state
- Memory is erased only by HDAC activity or cell division

### Cancer Relevance
- Piezo1-mediated epigenetic memory promotes malignant characteristics
- Tumor progression driven by compressive stress from tumor growth
- Compressive force → Piezo1 → H3K acetylation → cancer stem cell gene program

### Also from Search Results: Piezo1 → YAP pathway
- Piezo1 activation promotes YAP nuclear localization via actin remodeling
- Piezo1 → F-actin → YAP nuclear → osteogenic differentiation (related study, ACS Appl Mater Interfaces 2025)
- PIEZO1 calcium influx → nuclear mechanical properties change via actin remodeling (Biochim Biophys Res Commun 2024)
