# Pulsatile Flow Induces Chromatin Interaction with Lamin-Associated Proteins to Enrich H3K9 Methylation in Endothelial Cells

**Authors**: Chen LJ, Li JY, Nguyen P, Norwich G, Wang Y, Teng D, Shiu YT, Shyy JYJ, Chien S
**Year**: 2025
**Journal**: PNAS
**PMID**: 40117319
**PMC**: 11962468
**DOI**: 10.1073/pnas.2424566122
**Status**: Peer-reviewed

---

## Key Findings

### Experimental System
- Human endothelial cells under pulsatile vs oscillatory shear flow
- ChIP-seq for H3K9me3
- Lamin-associated domain (LAD) analysis

### Mechanical Signal → Histone Modification
- Pulsatile mechanical flow → **chromatin interaction with lamin-associated proteins**
- Result: enrichment of **H3K9me3** (repressive histone methylation mark) at lamin-associated chromatin regions
- This is a direct mechanosensitive histone mark — first clear mechanosensitive ChIP-seq histone mark mapping

### Mechanistic Route
- Shear force → lamin A/C reorganization → lamin-chromatin interactions → H3K9 methyltransferase recruitment → H3K9me3 deposition
- Repressive mark: genes in these regions are silenced by mechanical loading

### Significance for Mechanoepigenomics
- Demonstrates that mechanical forces can DIRECTLY deposit specific histone marks
- The mark is REPRESSIVE (H3K9me3) — not activating — at lamin-associated heterochromatin
- Parallel to: stiff ECM → open chromatin at mechanoenhancers (Cosgrove 2025, activating)
- Together: bimodal epigenomic response to force — some regions open (TEAD/YAP targets), others compact (lamin-associated heterochromatin)

## Gap for Generator

- H3K9me3 (repressive) mapped by mechanical force; H3K27ac (activating enhancer mark) at mechanoenhancers NOT yet mapped
- The mirror question: does stiff ECM deposit H3K27ac at mechanoenhancers via p300/CBP?
- Could the same mechanical signal simultaneously enrich H3K9me3 at lamin-associated domains AND H3K27ac at distal mechanoenhancers (repositioning heterochromatin to free up euchromatin for enhancer activation)?
