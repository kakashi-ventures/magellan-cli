# The Force is Strong with This Epigenome: Chromatin Structure and Mechanobiology

**Authors**: Hsia CR, Melters DP, Dalal Y
**Year**: 2023
**Journal**: Journal of Molecular Biology, Volume 435, Issue 11
**DOI**: 10.1016/j.jmb.2023.168057
**PMC**: 10567996
**Status**: Peer-reviewed review, open access, 193 references

---

## Key Findings (Review)

### Bidirectional Mechanical Feedback
- **Outside-in pathway**: ECM → cytoskeleton → nucleus → chromatin structure alteration → gene expression
- **Inside-out pathway**: chromatin structure → nuclear stiffness → cytoskeletal tension → ECM remodeling
- Both pathways create mechanical feedback loops that regulate cell behavior

### Mechanosensing Mechanisms
- Nuclear pore complex dilation → increased nuclear import of YAP/TAZ and mechanosensitive TFs
- Lamin A/C: determines nuclear stiffness, transmits force to chromatin
- LINC complex (nesprin/SUN proteins): physical connection cytoskeleton ↔ nucleoskeleton
- Chromatin compaction state (heterochromatin vs euchromatin) affects nuclear stiffness

### Chromatin as Mechanical Element
- Chromatin is a key structural AND mechanoresponsive element
- Condensed chromatin = core nuclear mechanical protection element
- Chromatin adapts its organization in response to mechanical stress
- Heterochromatin accumulation at nuclear periphery provides structural integrity

### Applications
- Cancer: tumor-stroma mechanical interactions alter chromatin and gene expression
- Development: mechanobiological responses conserved across evolution
- Mitosis: centromeric chromatin regulation by mechanical forces

### Research Gaps Identified (2023)
- "How mechanotransduction mechanisms alter chromatin structure for SPECIFIC cellular functions" — still unclear
- Remains unclear which chromatin modifiers respond to which mechanical inputs
- Tissue-specific vs cell-type-specific mechanosensing programs not mapped
