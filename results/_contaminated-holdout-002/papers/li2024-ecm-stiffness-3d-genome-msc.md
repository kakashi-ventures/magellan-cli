# Stiff Extracellular Matrix Drives MSC Differentiation Toward Osteogenesis by Multiscale 3D Genome Reorganization

**Authors**: Li et al.
**Year**: 2024
**PMID**: 39094522
**Status**: Peer-reviewed

---

## Abstract

Extracellular matrix (ECM) stiffness is a major driver of stem cell fate. However, the involvement of the three-dimensional (3D) genomic reorganization in response to ECM stiffness remains unclear. Here, we generated comprehensive 3D chromatin landscapes of mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) exposed to various ECM stiffness. We found that there were more long-range chromatin interactions, but less compartment A in MSCs cultured on stiff ECM than those cultured on soft ECM. However, the switch from compartment B in MSCs cultured on soft ECM to compartment A in MSCs cultured on stiff ECM included genes encoding proteins primarily enriched in cytoskeleton organization. At the topologically associating domains (TADs) level, stiff ECM tends to have merged TADs on soft ECM. These merged TADs on stiff ECM include upregulated genes encoding proteins enriched in osteogenesis, such as SP1, ETS1, and DCHS1. Knockdown of SP1 or ETS1 led to the downregulation of osteogenic marker genes, including COL1A1, RUNX2, ALP, and OCN in MSCs cultured on stiff ECM.

---

## Key Findings

### Experimental System
- Mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) on hydrogels of various stiffness
- Hi-C genome-wide 3D chromatin mapping
- Validation by qRT-PCR and alkaline phosphatase staining

### 3D Genome Changes with ECM Stiffness
- **More long-range chromatin interactions** on stiff ECM vs soft ECM
- **Less compartment A** overall on stiff ECM (paradoxically, despite more active transcription at specific loci)
- **Compartment B → A switching** at cytoskeletal organization gene loci under stiff ECM
- **TAD merging** on stiff ECM: adjacent TADs fuse, creating larger regulatory domains

### Functional Consequences of TAD Merging
- Merged TADs on stiff ECM include osteogenic genes: SP1, ETS1, DCHS1
- SP1 knockdown → downregulation of COL1A1, RUNX2, ALP, OCN
- ETS1 knockdown → same phenotype
- TAD merging = co-regulation of previously separated gene clusters

### Stiffness-Driven 3D Genome Model
- Stiff ECM → cytoskeletal tension → nuclear deformation → chromatin architecture change
- B→A switching at mechanosensitive gene loci enables transcriptional activation
- TAD merging allows co-expression of osteogenic gene networks

## Key Gap for Generator

- **TAD-level only**: no enhancer-promoter loop resolution data; we do not know which specific enhancers drive the stiffness-responsive gene expression within these merged TADs
- No data on H3K27ac/H3K4me1 at these stiffness-responsive genomic regions
- SP1 and ETS1 are known transcription factors for enhancer activation — are they binding new enhancers in the merged TADs?
- Critical connection: do merged TADs enable new enhancer-promoter contacts at osteogenic gene loci?
