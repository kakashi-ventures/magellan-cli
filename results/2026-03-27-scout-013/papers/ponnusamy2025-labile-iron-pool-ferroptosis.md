# Labile Iron Pool Dynamics Do Not Drive Ferroptosis Potentiation in Colorectal Cancer Cells

**Authors:** Ponnusamy V, Randall DR, Lee ZH, Das NK, Zhao L, Buscher K, Solanki S, Renslo AR, Hsu PP, Shah YM
**Year:** 2025
**Journal:** bioRxiv / published 2025
**DOI:** 10.1101/2025.07.01.662602
**PMID:** 40631145
**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12236665/

## Abstract
The study examines iron's role in ferroptosis in colorectal cancer cells. Colorectal cancer cells exhibit elevated iron accumulation. Using the TRX-PURO reactivity-based probe of labile iron, the authors find that the labile iron pool (LIP) does NOT measurably increase during ferroptosis induction with GPX4 inhibition or inhibition of the SLC7A11 cysteine/glutamate antiporter.

## Key Findings Relevant to Candidate 5 (CNT × Ferroptosis Iron Pool Dynamics)

1. **Central paradox (key anomaly confirmed):** The LIP does NOT expand during ferroptosis, despite ferritinophagy releasing ferritin iron. Exogenous iron *potentiates* ferroptosis but endogenous LIP expansion does NOT occur.
2. **Iron homeostasis genes:** Minimal transcriptional changes in iron homeostasis genes during ferroptosis induction.
3. **The unexplained gap:** If ferritinophagy releases iron from ferritin during ferroptosis, where does the iron go? Why doesn't the LIP expand? This is precisely the mechanistic gap CNT could address — the iron may nucleate into transient nano-clusters that evade probe detection.
4. **Current framework limitation:** The field lacks a quantitative kinetic framework for iron speciation during ferroptosis. Existing models assume LIP expansion as the mechanism; this paper disproves that assumption.
