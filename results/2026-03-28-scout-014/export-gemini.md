### C2-H1: A* State Population Is the Protein Mpemba Overlap Coefficient

**Mathematical claims to verify**:

**Claim 1 — D_KL as Mpemba monotone**:
The Kullback-Leibler divergence D_KL(P_A* ‖ P_eq) is claimed to be a "resource-theoretic
Mpemba monotone" — a quantity that must decrease monotonically in any system exhibiting
Mpemba-type behavior. The hypothesis cites Avanzini et al. 2026 PRX 16:011065 for this claim.

Verification task:
1. Use Google Search to find Avanzini et al. 2026 in PRX (Physical Review X)
2. Independently, verify from first principles: is D_KL monotonically decreasing under
   detailed-balance dynamics (standard result from H-theorem)? If yes, does this make it
   a "Mpemba monotone" in the sense of Lu & Raz 2017?

Note: The Lu & Raz (2017) definition of Mpemba effect requires that the initial state with
HIGHER temperature reaches equilibrium FASTER. A monotone for this effect would need to
capture which initial state is farther from equilibrium in the relevant sense. D_KL from
P_0 to P_eq measures total divergence; is this the right quantity, or should it be the
projection onto the slowest eigenmode?

**Claim 2 — A* population = Mpemba overlap coefficient |c₂|**:
The Mpemba overlap coefficient (Lu & Raz 2017) is defined as:
c₂ = <left eigenvector φ₂ | P_0 - P_eq>

where P_0 is the initial distribution and φ₂ is the left eigenvector corresponding to
the second eigenvalue (slowest relaxation mode) of the transition matrix.

The A* excited-state population (Chakraborty 2020) is defined as:
P_A* = fraction of equilibrium Boltzmann weight in A* conformations
(approximately 3% for Abeta42, 0.3% for Abeta40 from SOP-IDP simulations)

Verification task: Is P_A* formally identical to |c₂|? Write out the two definitions
mathematically. What are the conditions under which P_A* ≈ |c₂|?

**Claim 3 — D_KL = δF/kT**:
The hypothesis claims D_KL(P_A* ‖ P_eq) = δF/kT where δF is the free energy difference.

Verification task: This is the Gibbs-Shannon identity, valid at equilibrium. Is it valid
for the A* distribution? Run code to verify: generate a simple 3-state system, compute
D_KL directly and compare to δF/kT. Does the identity hold when P_A* ≠ P_eq_3state?

**Structural mapping question**:
The core logical tension: Mpemba effect = a system at higher temperature reaches thermal
equilibrium FASTER than a system at lower temperature (faster relaxation TO equilibrium).
Amyloid aggregation = kinetic TRAPPING away from equilibrium (slower escape from
aggregation-prone states = more disease-relevant).

Is there a mathematical formulation where these two processes are connected, or is this
an irreducible logical tension?

### C2-H3: Cooling-Rate-Dependent Fibril Polymorph Selection via Eigenmode Branching

**Mathematical claims to verify**:

**Claim 1 — T_cross existence and value**:
The hypothesis predicts a crossover temperature T_cross (45–55°C for insulin at pH 2)
where eigenmode overlap coefficients satisfy |c₂(T)| = |c₃(T)|.

The temperature dependence of eigenmode overlap coefficients is:
c_k(T) = <φ_k^L | P_0(T) - P_eq(T)>

where P_0(T) is the initial distribution at temperature T and φ_k^L is the kth left
eigenvector of the (temperature-dependent) transition matrix.

Verification task: For a simple model — say a 3-state system where transition rates

**Claim 2 — Eigenmode branching mechanism for polymorph selection**:
The hypothesis proposes that different cooling rates select different fibril polymorphs

Mathematical structure: The transition matrix M(T) changes with temperature. The
eigenmode populations evolve as the system cools. The cooling rate determines whether
the system tracks the instantaneous eigenstates (adiabatic/slow cooling) or freezes into
a non-equilibrium eigenstate (rapid quench).

Verification task: Is this an instance of the quantum adiabatic theorem applied to
classical stochastic dynamics? If so, what is the classical adiabatic criterion — i.e.,
how slow must cooling be to ensure adiabatic eigenmode tracking? Compute the criterion

**Claim 3 — Distinct polymorphs from different eigenmodes**:
The hypothesis requires that different eigenmodes of the protein MSM correspond to

Mathematical question: Is this claim about eigenmodes consistent with the definition of

more general framework?

Is this a formal isomorphism (adiabatic theorem applies literally to protein MSM
cooling), a structural analogy (similar mathematics, different validity conditions),
