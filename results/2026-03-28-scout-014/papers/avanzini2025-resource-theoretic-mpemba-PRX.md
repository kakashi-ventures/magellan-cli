**Authors:** Avanzini et al. (multiple authors)

## Summary (retrieved via WebSearch)
Unifies the thermal Mpemba effect (faster cooling from hot state) and the symmetry Mpemba effect (faster symmetry restoration) under a single resource-theoretic framework in both classical and quantum settings.

## Key Findings

**Resource-theoretic unification:**
- Both the thermal Mpemba effect and symmetry Mpemba effect arise from the same resource structure
- Thermal Mpemba: governed by slowest-decaying mode — characterized by quantum relative entropy divergence with respect to the STEADY STATE
- Symmetry Mpemba: governed by slowest symmetry-restoring mode — characterized by quantum relative entropy divergence with respect to an instantaneously DEPHASED state

**Common structure:**
- Both effects share the same monotone: quantum relative entropy (or classical KL-divergence)
- The difference is only the reference state: steady state (thermal) vs. dephased state (symmetry)
- This unification shows Mpemba physics is a universal property of open Markovian dynamics, independent of the specific physical observable being tracked

**Classical and quantum settings:**
- Framework applies to featureless baths (Markovian) and non-Markovian environments
- Classical limit recovers Lu & Raz 2017 result
- Quantum extension captures symmetry-breaking dynamics

## Relevance to Mpemba x Amyloid Bridge

**Unified framework for protein MSMs:**
- The resource-theoretic language (relative entropy, monotones) is applicable to protein conformational dynamics
- The Mpemba index of a protein MSM = the relative entropy of the initial conformational distribution relative to the stationary (equilibrium) distribution, weighted by the slowest eigenmode
- Proteins with higher relative entropy in their A* states relative to random coil ground state have larger "Mpemba resources" available

**Symmetry Mpemba analog in proteins:**
- Many amyloidogenic proteins have symmetry-broken fibril structures (β-sheet stacks with translational symmetry)
- Symmetry Mpemba effect analog: certain initial conformational states restore fibril-forming symmetry faster than others
- Could explain why some seed conformations (polymorphs) propagate faster than others

**Quantitative framework:**
- Provides a rigorous measure (relative entropy) for comparing Mpemba indices across different protein systems
- This is more rigorous than eigenmode overlap alone: accounts for both spectral and non-normal contributions
