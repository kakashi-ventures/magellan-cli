# Enzymatic Mpemba Effect: Slowing of Biochemical Reactions by Increasing Enzyme Concentration

**Authors:** Multiple authors
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** arXiv preprint
**arXiv:** 2411.08058
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.08058v1
**Submitted:** November 2024

## Summary (retrieved via WebSearch)
Demonstrates a biochemical analog of the Markovian Mpemba effect in enzymatic reaction systems with multisite modifications and allosteric proteins. Increasing enzyme concentration can paradoxically SLOW the relaxation to equilibrium — named the Enzymatic Mpemba effect.

## Key Findings

**Enzymatic Mpemba mechanism:**
- System: allosteric protein with multiple modification sites catalyzed by two enzymes with same catalytic activity but slightly different affinities
- When two enzymes are present, increasing the concentration of one enzyme increases the relaxation time to equilibrium
- The system remains in a long plateau before relaxing — the plateau with higher enzyme concentration is FARTHER from equilibrium than the plateau with lower concentration → takes longer to relax

**Mechanistic parallel to Markovian Mpemba:**
- Mechanism is similar to the Markovian Mpemba effect
- Higher enzyme concentration → changes overlap coefficients of the biochemical state with slow eigenmodes of the reaction network
- The reaction network (enzyme kinetics Markov chain) has spectral structure analogous to the thermal Mpemba systems
- This is the first demonstration of Mpemba physics in a biochemical (rather than purely physical) system

**Key distinction from our target:**
- Enzymatic Mpemba: concentration plays the role of "temperature" → concentration increase = "hotter" system
- Amyloid Mpemba: initial conformation plays the role of "temperature" → A* state population = eigenmode overlap coefficient
- Both involve Markovian networks, but: enzymatic systems are well-mixed, proteins involve conformational space MSMs

## Critical Assessment for Literature Disjointness

**IMPORTANT for DISJOINT status:**
- This paper applies Mpemba spectral theory to a biochemical (enzymatic) context for the FIRST TIME
- However, it does NOT apply to: protein folding, protein misfolding, amyloid aggregation, or conformational dynamics
- The enzymatic system is a chemical reaction network — fundamentally different from a protein conformational MSM
- Our hypothesis remains DISJOINT: no paper applies Mpemba spectral theory to protein conformational dynamics or amyloid aggregation selectivity

**Closest existing work:**
- This is the closest existing paper to our hypothesis in the biological/biochemical domain
- It confirms: biochemical systems CAN display Mpemba effects via Markovian spectral mechanisms
- It strengthens the plausibility argument: if enzymatic networks show Mpemba physics, protein conformational networks (which are also Markovian) plausibly do too
- But the specific claims about amyloid MSMs, conformational eigenmodes, and aggregation selectivity remain completely unexplored

## Relevance to Mpemba x Amyloid Bridge

**Plausibility support:**
- Demonstrates that biochemical Markovian networks follow Mpemba spectral theory
- Validates the conceptual leap from physical (thermal) Mpemba to biological (conformational) Mpemba

**Critical difference from our hypothesis:**
- Enzymatic systems: multi-enzyme competition affects relaxation timescale
- Protein conformational dynamics: initial ensemble overlap with aggregation eigenmode determines aggregation selectivity
- Our hypothesis is more fundamental: it operates at the level of the energy landscape and eigenmodes of the conformational MSM, not at the kinetics of chemical modification networks
