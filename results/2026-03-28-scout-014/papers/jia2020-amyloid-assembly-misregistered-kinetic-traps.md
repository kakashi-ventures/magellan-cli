# Amyloid Assembly Is Dominated by Misregistered Kinetic Traps on an Unbiased Energy Landscape

**Authors:** Zhiguang Jia, Jeremy D. Schmit, Jianing Chen
**Year:** 2020
**Journal:** PNAS
**DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.1911153117
**PMID:** 32345723; PMC: 7229673

## Abstract Summary
Combines atomistic simulations with Markov State Models (MSMs) to study fibril growth. The key finding: amyloid aggregation is dominated by kinetic traps arising from misregistered binding states, not by thermodynamic barriers. The ratio of misregistered (off-pathway) states is much higher than properly registered (on-pathway) states.

## Key Findings

**MSM structure:** MSM of fibril growth maps the search for in-register fully-bound state as a random walk on a 2D energy landscape defined by β-sheet alignment and hydrogen-bonding states.

**Kinetic trap dominance:** Without energetic bias, amyloids must exhaustively search the alignment space — slow because of relative simplicity of cross-β structure.

**Spectral relevance:** MSM eigenvalues correspond to relaxation timescales. The gap between the slowest and second-slowest eigenvalue governs whether aggregation is kinetically trapped or proceeds rapidly.

**Selectivity implication:** Different amyloid sequences have different MSM spectral gaps — some sequences form fast (many paths to fibril), others are selectively slow (high kinetic barriers from misregistration).
