# Studies on the Mechanism of General Anesthesia

**Authors:** Margaret A. Pavel, E. Nicholas Petersen, Hao Wang, Richard A. Lerner, Scott B. Hansen
**Year:** 2020
**Journal:** PNAS
**DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.2004259117
**PMID:** 32467161

## Abstract Summary
Establishes a membrane-mediated target of inhaled anesthesia. Inhaled anesthetics disrupt GM1 lipid domain structure, causing PLD2 to relocalize from GM1 rafts to PIP2-rich regions, generating phosphatidic acid (PA) that activates TREK-1 K+ channels, producing loss of consciousness.

## Key Findings

**Complete signaling cascade:**
1. Inhaled anesthetics (chloroform, isoflurane) intercalate into GM1-rich ordered lipid rafts
2. GM1 raft structure disrupted → domains expand, become disordered
3. PLD2 (palmitoylated via PH domain cysteines) dissociates from GM1 and translocates to PIP2-rich disordered regions
4. At PIP2 domains, PLD2 gains access to phosphatidylcholine substrate
5. PLD2 hydrolyzes PC → phosphatidic acid (PA)
6. PA binds gating helix of TREK-1 → channel activation → loss of consciousness

**In vivo validation:** PLDnull Drosophila flies resist anesthesia; whole-brain super-resolution imaging confirms raft disruption.

**Mechanosensation parallel:** TREK-1 activation by mechanical force uses a related but distinct mechanism — lipid raft degradation rather than expansion.
