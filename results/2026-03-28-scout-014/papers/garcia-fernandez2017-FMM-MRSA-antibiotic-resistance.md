# Membrane Microdomain Disassembly Inhibits MRSA Antibiotic Resistance

**Authors:** García-Fernández, Koch, Wagner, Fekete, Stengel, Schneider, Mielich-Süss, Geibel, Markert, Stigloher, Lopez
**Year:** 2017
**Journal:** Cell
**PMC:** 5720476

## Abstract Summary
FMMs in MRSA depend on carotenoid lipids (staphyloxanthin) and flotillin proteins. Disrupting FMM assembly (statin treatment or genetic deletion) prevents proper PBP2a organization, converting resistant MRSA to penicillin-susceptible bacteria.

## Key Findings

**FMM-resistance link:** FMMs are dedicated platforms for organizing antibiotic resistance proteins (PBP2a = β-lactam target).

**Pharmacological disruption:** Statins deplete carotenoid lipids needed for FMM integrity → FMMs disassemble → PBP2a mislocalizes → β-lactam sensitivity restored.

**In vivo proof:** Mouse infection model confirms statin+penicillin combination is effective against MRSA.

**Scope of FMM function:** FMMs support virulence, antibiotic resistance, biofilm formation, sporulation, motility, membrane fluidity, heme acquisition across diverse bacteria.
