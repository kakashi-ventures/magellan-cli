# Biphasic Cartilage Mechanics (Mow et al.) and Soil Liquefaction (Boulanger-Idriss)

## PAPER 1: Biphasic Cartilage Model
**Authors:** Van C. Mow, S.C. Kuei, W.M. Lai, C.G. Armstrong
**Year:** 1980
**Journal:** Journal of Biomechanical Engineering, 102(1):73-84
**DOI:** 10.1115/1.3138202

### Key Findings
- Articular cartilage modeled as biphasic medium: solid phase (collagen/proteoglycans) + fluid phase (interstitial water)
- Under load: fluid pressurizes rapidly (low permeability) then redistributes → biphasic relaxation
- Permeability parameter k governs drainage rate — low k = slow drainage = high pore pressure
- Load borne by fluid pressure (effective stress) + solid skeleton

### OA-relevant parameters
- k decreases in OA → slower drainage → elevated pore pressure under cyclic loading
- Proteoglycan depletion → reduced fixed charge density → altered fluid-solid coupling

## PAPER 2: Liquefaction Criteria (Representative)
**Authors:** R.W. Boulanger, I.M. Idriss
**Year:** 2004
**Report:** CGM-04/01, UC Davis

### Key Findings
**CSR (Cyclic Stress Ratio):** CSR = 0.65 × (σ_v/σ'_v) × (a_max/g) × r_d
- σ_v = total vertical stress; σ'_v = effective vertical stress; a_max = peak ground acceleration

**CRR (Cyclic Resistance Ratio):** Soil-specific resistance to liquefaction

**Factor of Safety:** FS = CRR/CSR; FS < 1.0 → liquefaction

**N_liq:** Number of cycles to trigger liquefaction at given CSR — varies as power law: N_liq ∝ CSR^{-b}

**Pore pressure ratio r_u = u_e/σ'_v:** r_u → 1.0 at liquefaction (effective stress → 0)
