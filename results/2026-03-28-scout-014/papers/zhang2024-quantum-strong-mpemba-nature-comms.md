# Observation of Quantum Strong Mpemba Effect

**Authors:** Zhang et al.
**Year:** 2024 (published January 2025)
**Journal:** Nature Communications
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-024-54303-0
**PMID:** 39762250

**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-54303-0

## Summary (retrieved via WebSearch)
First experimental observation of the quantum strong Mpemba effect in a single trapped ion (40Ca+) system with three energy levels. Demonstrates exponentially accelerated relaxation by preparing an initial state with zero excitation of the slowest decaying mode.

## Key Findings

**Experimental setup:**
- Single trapped Calcium-40 ion, three energy levels (ground + two excited states)
- Controlled Lindblad master equation dynamics (open quantum system)
- Precise preparation of initial quantum state

**Strong Mpemba effect observed:**
- By preparing the optimal initial state with no excitation of the slowest decaying Liouvillian mode (zero overlap with slowest eigenmode), exponentially accelerated relaxation was demonstrated
- The acceleration is NOT just marginally faster — it is exponential (orders of magnitude faster convergence)

**Connection to Liouvillian exceptional point:**
- The condition for realizing the strong Mpemba effect coincides with the Liouvillian exceptional point: coalescence of both eigenvalues AND eigenmodes of the Lindblad superoperator
- At exceptional points, both eigenvalues degenerate AND eigenvectors coalesce → anomalous non-normal dynamics
- This provides the link between Mpemba physics and non-Hermitian physics

**Engineering implications:**
- Efficient strategy to engineer open quantum system dynamics
- The paper demonstrates that eigenmode overlap engineering is experimentally achievable
- This is directly relevant to protein systems: if protein conformational dynamics can be engineered to suppress the misfolding eigenmode, Mpemba-like fibril suppression follows

## Relevance to Mpemba x Amyloid Bridge

**Proof of principle:**
- The experimental demonstration that eigenmode suppression (zero overlap with slow mode) produces exponentially accelerated relaxation validates the core mechanism
- For proteins: if an annealing protocol can be designed that puts the initial conformational ensemble in a state with low projection on the aggregation eigenmode (= low A* state population in Chakraborty's language), fibril formation would be exponentially suppressed

**Liouvillian exceptional point connection:**
- At exceptional points, the Liouvillian becomes non-normal (non-orthogonal eigenmodes)
- Protein MSMs near critical conformational transitions may exhibit non-normal transition matrices
- Non-normality in protein MSM → transient amplification of misfolding even from native-like initial states → explains why some proteins aggregate rapidly under mild stress despite starting from folded conformations

**Experimental analogy:**
- Trapped ion initial state preparation ≡ protein refolding protocol
- Liouvillian eigenmode suppression ≡ conformational ensemble preparation avoiding A* states
- Exponential relaxation speedup ≡ exponential suppression of fibril formation lag time
