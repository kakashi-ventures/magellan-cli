# In Vivo Characterization of the Scaffold Activity of Flotillin on the Membrane Kinase KinC of Bacillus subtilis

**Authors:** Johannes Schneider, Benjamin Mielich-Süss, Richard Böhme, Daniel Lopez
**Year:** 2015
**Journal:** Molecular Microbiology
**PMC:** 4591472

## Abstract Summary
Demonstrates that FloT (bacterial flotillin-like protein) physically binds to KinC (membrane-bound sensor kinase) within functional membrane microdomains (FMMs) of B. subtilis. FloT scaffolds KinC dimerization, enabling activation by surfactin (a membrane-active AMP-like molecule).

## Key Findings

**FloT as scaffold:** FloT promotes efficient self-interaction and dimerization of KinC at optimal concentrations, enabling the active dimeric form.

**Specificity:** FloT protects KinC from non-specific aggregation with KinB (dead-end intermediate), ensuring signaling fidelity within FMMs.

**Activation trigger:** KinC is activated by surfactin — a self-produced cyclic lipopeptide that causes specific membrane disruption. Surfactin activates KinC → phosphorylates Spo0A → biofilm formation.

**Localization:** KinC must be in FMMs (co-localized with FloT and FloA); detergent-resistant membrane fraction contains both FloT/FloA and KinC.
