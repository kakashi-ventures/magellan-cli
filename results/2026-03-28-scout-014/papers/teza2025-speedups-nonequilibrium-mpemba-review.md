# Speedups in Nonequilibrium Thermal Relaxation: Mpemba and Related Effects

**Authors:** Gianluca Teza, John Bechhoefer, Antonio Lasanta, Oren Raz, Marija Vucelja
**Year:** 2025 (submitted Feb 2025; published Physics Reports Vol. 1164, 2026)
**Journal:** Physics Reports
**arXiv:** 2502.01758

## Abstract Summary
Comprehensive review of anomalous cooling and thermal relaxation phenomena. Covers classical systems (water, granular gases, colloidal particles, spin systems, magnetic alloys, Brownian particles) and quantum systems (trapped ions, open quantum systems). Unifies mechanisms through stochastic thermodynamics and kinetic theory.

## Key Findings

**Common mechanism:** Non-monotonic dependence of relaxation time vs. initial distance from final state.

**Two distinct mechanisms identified:**
1. **Spectral-gap mechanism (strong ME):** Initial state with zero overlap on slowest-decaying eigenmode → relaxes via faster modes → exponential speedup
2. **Non-normal Liouvillian mechanism:** Non-orthogonal eigenmodes produce transient interference → anomalous early-time behavior even without spectral gap changes

**Systems covered:** Water, granular gases, colloidal particles, spin systems, Brownian particles, trapped ions.

**No biological/biophysical applications discussed.** Review covers through end of 2024; no protein, amyloid, or biophysical Mpemba effect papers exist in literature.
