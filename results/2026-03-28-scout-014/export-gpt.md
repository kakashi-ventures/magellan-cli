## Glossary (technical terms explained)

- **MSM (Markov State Model)**: A network model of protein conformational dynamics. Each
  node is a protein structure; edges are transition probabilities. The eigenvalues of
  this network's transition matrix describe how fast different motions equilibrate.
- **Eigenmode / eigenvalue**: In an MSM, the slowest-decaying eigenmode (second eigenvalue
  closest to 1) represents the longest relaxation timescale. Having a large gap between
  eigenvalue 2 and eigenvalue 3 means the slowest process is well-separated from faster ones.
- **Mpemba effect**: Experimentally observed phenomenon where a hotter system sometimes
  cools faster than a cooler system given identical conditions. In the spectral theory
  formulation (Lu & Raz 2017), this happens when the initial state has high overlap with
  the slowest eigenmode — meaning the slow relaxation timescale gets "short-circuited."
- **Mpemba overlap coefficient |c₂|**: A scalar measuring how much of the initial
  probability distribution projects onto the second eigenmode (slowest relaxation mode)
  of the system's dynamics. High |c₂| → Mpemba effect possible.
- **A* (excited) state**: A metastable conformational state of an amyloid-forming protein
  (Chakraborty et al. 2020) that has elevated aggregation propensity. These states are
  rare at equilibrium (~3% for Abeta42, ~0.3% for Abeta40) but disproportionately seed
  fibril formation.
- **Zwanzig roughness formula**: A 1988 formula by Robert Zwanzig showing that diffusion
  on a rough energy landscape is slowed by exp(epsilon^2/(kT)^2), where epsilon is the
  amplitude of surface roughness. Predicts D_rough = D_smooth × exp(-epsilon^2/(kT)^2).
- **D_misfold/D_fold ratio**: The ratio of diffusion coefficients on the misfolding
  energy landscape vs the folding energy landscape, measured by force spectroscopy.
  Yu et al. 2015 measured this for prion protein (PrP) and found D_misfold << D_fold.
- **Sarle's bimodality coefficient (BC)**: A statistical test for bimodality. BC > 0.555
  indicates a bimodal distribution (two distinct peaks rather than one). Applied here
  to the distribution of eigenvalue spacings in protein MSMs.
- **D_KL (Kullback-Leibler divergence)**: An information-theoretic measure of how
  different a probability distribution P is from a reference distribution Q. Here used
  to quantify how different the A* excited-state distribution is from the equilibrium
  Boltzmann distribution.
- **Fibril polymorph**: Different structural variants of the same amyloid fibril — same
  protein sequence but different 3D arrangements. Different polymorphs can have different
  pathological properties (e.g., different prion strains).
- **T_cross (crossover temperature)**: The temperature at which eigenmode overlap
  coefficients c₂ and c₃ cross — i.e., where the system transitions from being dominated
  by the second eigenmode to the third eigenmode. Predicted to be 45–55°C for insulin at pH 2.

### HYPOTHESIS C2-H2: Measured D_misfold/D_fold Ratio of PrP Predicts Bimodal Eigenvalue Spectrum via Zwanzig-Kramers Bridge

**Quality Gate score**: 7.3/10 | **Verdict**: CONDITIONAL_PASS
**Original MAGELLAN confidence**: 7/10

**Core mechanism (three levels)**:

Level 1 — Empirical anchor: Yu et al. (2015, PNAS 112:8308, PMID 26109573) measured
diffusion coefficients for PrP dimers under 10 pN force using single-molecule FRET.
They found D_misfold/D_fold = 1:1000 (misfolding landscape is 1000x rougher than
folding landscape). This is the only published direct measurement of this ratio for
an amyloidogenic protein.

Level 2 — Mathematical bridge: The Zwanzig (1988, PNAS 85:2029) roughness formula
predicts that if D_misfold/D_fold = 10^-3, then epsilon_misfold = kT × sqrt(ln(1000))
= kT × sqrt(6.91) ≈ 2.63 kT at 310K. This falls in the 2.8–3.8 kT range that the
hypothesis predicts creates bimodal eigenvalue spectra in protein MSMs (based on Sarle's
BC > 0.555 criterion applied to the spacing between eigenvalues 2 and 3).

Level 3 — Population-level: The bimodal eigenvalue spectrum (if confirmed) predicts
that amyloidogenic proteins have a clear spectral gap that non-amyloidogenic proteins
lack. This should correlate with aggregation-prone sequences identified by TANGO
(Fernandez-Escamilla et al. 2004, Nat. Biotechnol. 22:1302) and concentration-dependent
nucleation kinetics (Cohen et al. 2013, PNAS 110:9882 — secondary nucleation for Abeta42).

**Specific testable predictions**:
1. MSMs for amyloidogenic proteins (Abeta42, alpha-syn, IAPP, PrP) show Sarle BC > 0.555
   in eigenvalue spacing; non-amyloidogenic proteins (lysozyme, myoglobin) show BC < 0.555
2. epsilon_misfold derived from the bimodal spectrum falls in 2.8–3.8 kT range
3. Proteins with M_eff > 1.5 (effective number of slow modes) have higher ThT fluorescence
   at 24h than those with M_eff < 1.5
4. Concentration-dependent lag time follows: tau_lag ∝ [protein]^(-1/2) × exp(M_eff/kT)
5. TANGO score and M_eff show different proteins in their top-5 lists (orthogonal predictors)

**Cited papers**:
- Yu et al. 2015, PNAS 112:8308 (PMID 26109573): Force spectroscopy D_misfold/D_fold
  for PrP dimers. Status: VERIFIED by Quality Gate.
- Zwanzig 1988, PNAS 85:2029: Roughness formula D_rough = D_smooth × exp(-epsilon^2/(kT)^2).
  Status: Classic reference, foundational.
- Fernandez-Escamilla et al. 2004, Nat. Biotechnol. 22:1302: TANGO aggregation predictor.
  Status: VERIFIED by Quality Gate.
- Cohen et al. 2013, PNAS 110:9882: Abeta42 secondary nucleation kinetics (note: hypothesis
  originally cited wrong year/volume "2012, 109:9761" — actual is 2013, 110:9882).
  Status: Content real, metadata error.

**Known limitations (from internal review)**:
- Yu et al. measured PrP DIMERS under 10 pN applied force, not monomers in free diffusion.
  The D ratio is real but measured under non-physiological conditions.
- Extension from force spectroscopy to zero-force solution-phase MSM is uncertain — the
  roughness amplitude epsilon may differ substantially between force and force-free regimes.
- D_fold for intrinsically disordered proteins (IDPs like Abeta42) is conceptually ambiguous
  since they lack a well-defined folded state.
- The "orthogonal information" claim vs TANGO requires demonstration; M_eff might just
  recapitulate hydrophobicity in a more complex way.

**Specific verification tasks for GPT**:
1. Search for any published work connecting force spectroscopy D ratios to MSM eigenspectra
2. Verify the Zwanzig arithmetic: given D_misfold/D_fold = 10^-3, compute epsilon in kT units
3. Search for papers applying Sarle's BC to protein MSM eigenvalue distributions
4. Look for counter-evidence: do non-amyloidogenic proteins also show MSM eigenspectrum
   bimodality? Are there amyloidogenic proteins with smooth landscapes?
5. Check whether the force-to-free-diffusion extrapolation has any published precedent

### HYPOTHESIS C2-H1: A* State Population Is the Protein Mpemba Overlap Coefficient

**Quality Gate score**: 6.6/10 | **Verdict**: CONDITIONAL_PASS
**Original MAGELLAN confidence**: 6/10

**Core mechanism**:

The Chakraborty et al. 2020 (PNAS 117:19926-19937, PMID 32732434) model identified
"excited" (A*) conformational states of amyloid-forming proteins that have high aggregation
propensity. For Abeta42, approximately 3% of the equilibrium population is in A* states;
for Abeta40, approximately 0.3% — a 10-fold difference that explains their dramatically
different aggregation rates. These A* states were identified using a coarse-grained
SOP-IDP (Self-Organized Polymer for IDPs) computational model.

The Mpemba spectral theory (Lu & Raz 2017, PNAS 114:5083, PMID 28461467) defines a
Mpemba overlap coefficient as the projection of the initial probability distribution onto
the second eigenmode (slowest relaxation mode) of the system's transition matrix.

The hypothesis: the A* state population of a protein IS the physical realization of its
Mpemba overlap coefficient. Specifically:
- D_KL(P_A* ‖ P_eq) — the Kullback-Leibler divergence between the A* excited-state
  distribution and the equilibrium Boltzmann distribution — serves as a scalar unification
- For Abeta42 vs Abeta40: D_KL ratio ≥ 1.5 (testable prediction)
- Spearman correlation between A* population (%) and MSM-derived |c₂| (Mpemba coefficient)
  should exceed ρ > 0.8 across 8+ proteins
- At least 80% of the spectral weight should concentrate in 2 slow eigenmodes for
  amyloidogenic proteins (spectral concentration prediction)

**Cited papers**:
- Chakraborty et al. 2020, PNAS 117:19926-19937 (PMID 32732434): A* excited states in
  Abeta42/Abeta40. Status: VERIFIED (wrong page numbers cited originally: 117:16817;
  correct is 117:19926-19937).
- Lu & Raz 2017, PNAS 114:5083 (PMID 28461467): Mpemba effect spectral theory, eigenmode
  overlap coefficient. Status: VERIFIED.
- Klich et al. 2019, PRX 9:021060: Eigenmode framework for Mpemba effect.
  Status: VERIFIED.
- Avanzini et al. 2026, PRX 16:011065: Resource-theoretic framing of Mpemba monotones.
  Status: UNVERIFIABLE — cannot be confirmed by independent search. The hypothesis's
  "resource-theoretic" framing rests on this unconfirmed citation.

**Known limitations (from internal review)**:
- Avanzini 2026 is unverifiable — if this citation is fabricated, the resource-theoretic
  framing of D_KL as a Mpemba monotone loses its formal grounding (though the D_KL
  connection to eigenmode overlap survives via Lu & Raz 2017 directly)
- Logical tension: Mpemba effect = fast equilibration to equilibrium; amyloid aggregation
  = kinetic trapping AWAY from equilibrium. These are mathematically opposite processes.
- Chakraborty A* states come from coarse-grained SOP-IDP model — extension to all-atom
  MSMs is non-trivial
- D_KL = δF/kT equivalence holds only at equilibrium; IDPs are far from equilibrium
- The 1.5-fold D_KL ratio threshold between Abeta42/Abeta40 might not survive MSM
  construction uncertainty

**Specific verification tasks for GPT**:
1. Search for Avanzini et al. 2026, PRX 16:011065 to determine if it exists
2. Search for any papers connecting D_KL divergence (or information-theoretic measures)
   to Mpemba effect eigenmode theory
3. Search for papers connecting A* excited states (Chakraborty model) to any spectral
   or eigenmode framework
4. Look for evidence that Mpemba relaxation and kinetic trapping are compatible
   mathematical frameworks (this is the core logical tension)
5. Search for resource-theoretic treatments of protein folding/misfolding

### HYPOTHESIS C2-H3: Cooling-Rate-Dependent Fibril Polymorph Selection via Eigenmode Branching

**Quality Gate score**: 7.0/10 | **Verdict**: CONDITIONAL_PASS
**Original MAGELLAN confidence**: 7/10

**Core mechanism**:

Different cooling rates from 65°C to 37°C select different fibril polymorphs (tested with
insulin at pH 2) because thermal history determines which slow eigenmode is populated
during the transition from unfolded to aggregation-prone states. Specifically:

- At high temperature (65°C), the protein samples both the second eigenmode (c₂) and
  third eigenmode (c₃) of its MSM transition matrix
- As temperature drops, the relative populations of these eigenmodes evolve
- There exists a crossover temperature T_cross (predicted: 45–55°C for insulin at pH 2)
  where |c₂(T)| = |c₃(T)| — the two eigenmodes have equal overlap with the current
  population distribution
- Rapid cooling (quench): passes through T_cross too quickly → predominantly populates
  whichever eigenmode was dominant just above T_cross → one polymorph type
- Slow cooling: allows equilibration at each temperature → different eigenmode populations
  → different polymorph type
- Intermediate cooling rate: transitions at T_cross → mixed polymorphs

**Cited papers** (all verified):
- Jimenez et al. 2002, PNAS 99:9196: Insulin fibril polymorphs documented.
  Status: VERIFIED.
- Nielsen et al. 2001, Biochemistry 40:6036: Insulin fibrillation kinetics at pH 2.
  Status: VERIFIED.
- Klich et al. 2019, PRX 9:021060: Eigenmode overlap framework for Mpemba-type effects.
  Status: VERIFIED.

**Additional context**: Petkova et al. 2005 (PNAS) showed that Abeta42 under quiescent
vs agitated growth produces different polymorphs — attributed to shear forces, not
eigenmode overlap. This is the closest prior work to C2-H3; the eigenmode explanation
for polymorph selection is genuinely new.

**Specific testable predictions**:
1. Three-arm experiment (rapid quench / slow cool / intermediate rate) produces distinct
   fibril polymorphs at the two extreme rates
2. T_cross falls within ±5°C of the MSM-predicted 45–55°C range
3. FTIR and ssNMR show RMSD > 3Å between rapid-quench and slow-cool polymorphs
4. Cryo-EM confirms structural distinction between the two polymorph types
5. Intermediate cooling rate produces heterogeneous mixture of both polymorph types

**Known limitations (from internal review)**:
- Insulin at pH 2 is biologically artificial (physiological insulin is pH 7.4 at 37°C).
  Results may not translate to disease-relevant in vivo conditions.
- Two-eigenmode model likely oversimplifies the dense eigenvalue spectrum of a real protein
- Bootstrap uncertainty on T_cross has not been estimated
- Stochastic polymorph selection means n ≥ 10 per arm is required for statistical power
- Title/content inconsistency in the original hypothesis (title referenced Abeta42 but
  experimental system described is insulin at pH 2)

**Specific verification tasks for GPT**:
1. Search for papers on temperature-rate-dependent fibril polymorph selection (any protein)
2. Search for Petkova et al. 2005 and related work on quiescent vs agitated polymorph selection
3. Search for any MSM or eigenmode-based explanation of polymorph selection
4. Look for counter-evidence: do cooling rates actually produce distinct polymorphs, or
   is the dominant variable agitation/shear rather than thermal rate?
5. Search for insulin pH 2 fibril polymorph characterization in 2024-2026 literature
6. Verify the T_cross calculation: for a two-state Boltzmann system with eigenvalue gap
   delta_lambda, compute when c₂(T) = c₃(T) as a function of temperature
