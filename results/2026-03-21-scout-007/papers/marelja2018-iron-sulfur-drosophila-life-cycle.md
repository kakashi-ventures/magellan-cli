**Journal:** Frontiers in Physiology

**Year:** 2018

**PMID:** 29491838

**DOI:** 10.3389/fphys.2018.00050

## Abstract Summary (from full text fetch)

Comprehensive study of Fe-S cluster biosynthesis genes and their roles in Drosophila life cycle, metabolism, and circadian function. Identifies specific genes in the ISC pathway (NFS1 homologue, IscU, CIA pathway) as circadian pacemaker components.

---

## Key Findings Relevant to Fe-S × Circadian Bridge

### Circadian Function of Specific Fe-S Genes
- **NFS1 (Drosophila IscS) RNAi in clock neurons → ARRHYTHMIA** (loss of rhythmic activity in constant darkness)
- **IscU RNAi:** arrhythmia when disrupted in clock neurons
- **Mocs3 RNAi:** 56% arrhythmic flies with tim27-Gal4 driver
- **Iba57 and Cfd1:** also implicated in circadian regulation

### Proposed Mechanism (speculative in this paper)
- "Fe-S clusters are continuously present in key enzymes during the day-night cycle"
- Rhythmic iron sensing via Fe-S respiratory enzymes may regulate circadian oscillations through metabolic feedback loops
- Authors do NOT identify specific mechanism connecting Fe-S biogenesis to clock molecular machinery

### Life Cycle Context
- Fe-S cluster and molybdenum cofactor enzymes control key metabolic steps throughout the fly life cycle
- Deficiencies cause developmental delays, reduced lifespan, and metabolic dysfunction

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **NFS1 homologue (IscS) is REQUIRED for normal circadian period** in Drosophila
- **But the mechanism is UNKNOWN** — this is the primary gap
- Whether this applies to mammals is unknown (no mammalian follow-up published)
- The arrhythmia phenotype (not just period lengthening) suggests Fe-S biogenesis is needed for clock amplitude/robustness
