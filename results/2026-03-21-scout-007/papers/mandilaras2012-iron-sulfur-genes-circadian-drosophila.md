## Abstract (reconstructed from full text fetch)

RNA interference screening study targeting iron metabolism genes in Drosophila clock neurons. Identified that ferritin 2 Light Chain Homologue (Fer2LCH) is required for circadian activity in constant darkness. Five Fe-S cluster biosynthesis genes identified with putative circadian roles: IscS homologue (CG12264), IscU homologue (CG9836), IscA1 homologue (CG8198), Iba57 homologue (CG8043), Nubp2 homologue (CG4858).

---

## Key Findings Relevant to Fe-S × Circadian Bridge

- RNAi against Drosophila IscS (NFS1 homologue), IscU, Iba57, and IscA1 resulted in visible disruptions of circadian behavior in constant darkness
- IscS RNAi showed **prolonged period**; Iba57 RNAi showed **reduced period**
- This is the FIRST study linking Fe-S cluster biosynthetic pathway to circadian pacemaker function
- Mechanism not determined — authors suggest metabolic feedback via respiratory enzymes
