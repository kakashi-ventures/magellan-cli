# Diurnal control of iron responsive element containing mRNAs through iron regulatory proteins IRP1 and IRP2 is mediated by feeding rhythms

**Authors:** Nadimpalli HP, Katsioudi G, Arpa ES, Chikhaoui L, Arpat AB, Liechti A, Palais G, Tessmer C, Hofmann I, Galy B, Gatfield D

**Journal:** Genome Biology

**Year:** 2024

**PMID:** 38773499

**DOI:** 10.1186/s13059-024-03270-2

**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11106963/

---

## Abstract Summary

IRP1 and IRP2 bind to IRE hairpins under conditions of low iron, thereby inhibiting mRNA translation or stability. Cellular iron homeostasis is regulated by these proteins that sense iron levels and metabolic cues. The study reveals HIGH-AMPLITUDE DIURNAL OSCILLATIONS in the regulation of key IRE-containing transcripts in mouse liver.

---

## Key Findings

### IRP1 vs. IRP2 Oscillation Patterns
- **IRP1/ACO1 protein levels: CONSTANT throughout the day** — does NOT oscillate
- **IRP2 protein: HIGH-AMPLITUDE OSCILLATION** — approximately 10-fold amplitude between trough (ZT4) and peak (ZT12)
- Both are present in liver

### Driver of Oscillation
- **Feeding rhythms, NOT intrinsic clock**, drive IRE-mRNA oscillations
- "Rhythmic feeding was sufficient to induce post-transcriptionally generated Tfrc mRNA oscillations in the absence of an endogenous circadian system"
- Key distinction: feeding-entrained vs. clock-driven

### Downstream Targets Most Strongly Regulated
- **Ferritin** genes (Fth1, Ftl1): translation maximal at ZT4-6, minimal around ZT12
- **Alas2** (aminolevulinate synthase 2): high-amplitude rhythm
- **Transferrin receptor (Tfrc):** oscillates in mRNA stability

### IRP1 Fe-S Cluster Occupancy
- **NOT directly measured in this study**
- Authors note: "measuring labile iron pool is a major challenge in the field"
- No significant changes in Fe(II) levels detected across time points

---

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- IRP1's FUNCTIONAL STATE (aconitase vs. IRE-binding) depends on Fe-S cluster occupancy
- IRP2 oscillates 10-fold: indicates iron homeostasis IS temporally regulated
- The fact that IRP1 protein is constant but IRP2 oscillates suggests that IRP1's Fe-S cluster status (not protein level) could be the relevant variable
- **Gap identified:** No paper has measured IRP1 Fe-S cluster occupancy over 24h in any organism

---

## Bridge Relevance

**CRITICAL BRIDGE PAPER.** Establishes that iron regulatory machinery shows diurnal oscillation in liver, coupled to feeding. IRP1's aconitase/IRE switch is directly controlled by Fe-S cluster occupancy — if Fe-S biogenesis machinery (NFS1, ISCU2, GLRX5) activity varies with time-of-day, this would directly modulate IRP1 aconitase activity and thereby iron homeostasis timing. Provides the mechanistic link: Fe-S biogenesis rate → IRP1 Fe-S occupancy → iron homeostasis timing.
