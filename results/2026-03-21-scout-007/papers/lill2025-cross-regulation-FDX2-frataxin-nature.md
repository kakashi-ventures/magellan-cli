# Cross-regulation of [2Fe-2S] cluster synthesis by ferredoxin-2 and frataxin

**Authors:** Lill R, et al. (Roland Lill group, Marburg)

**Journal:** Nature

**Year:** 2025

**DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-025-09822-1

**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-09822-1

---

## Abstract Summary (from search results — full text paywalled)

Using an in-vitro-reconstituted human Fe-S cluster assembly system, the study demonstrates that efficient [2Fe-2S] cluster synthesis requires a FINE-TUNED STOICHIOMETRIC BALANCE between FDX2 and frataxin (FXN). Any deviation from approximately equimolar FDX2:FXN ratio downregulates Fe-S cluster synthesis.

---

## Key Findings

### Antagonistic Cross-Regulation
- **FXN excess:** impairs the persulfide-reductase activity of FDX2
- **FDX2 excess:** slows down FXN-accelerated persulfide transfer to ISCU2
- Both proteins compete for overlapping binding sites on the NFS1-ISD11-ACP1 complex

### Mechanistic Details
- FDX2 directly hinders persulfide generation and transfer to ISCU2 by interacting with NFS1's persulfide-carrying mobile Cys-loop
- FXN accelerates persulfide transfer from NFS1 to ISCU2
- FDX2 reduces persulfide on ISCU2 → [2Fe-2S] cluster formation

### Therapeutic Implications for Friedreich's Ataxia (FRDA)
- In FRDA (FXN deficiency): decreasing FDX2 may be beneficial
- Knocking-down FDX2 in FRDA Drosophila model increases fly lifespan
- This is a "novel therapeutic axis" for FRDA

### Companion paper (same issue)
- Mutations in mitochondrial ferredoxin FDX2 suppress frataxin deficiency (PMID 41372402)

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **The FDX2:FXN ratio is the regulatory knob for Fe-S synthesis rate**
- If EITHER protein oscillates circadianly, Fe-S synthesis rate would oscillate
- FDX2 accepts electrons from FDXR (ferredoxin reductase) using NADPH
- **NADPH levels oscillate circadianly** (NADPH/NADP+ ratio is clock-controlled)
- Therefore: circadian NADPH oscillation → FDX2 reduction rate → Fe-S synthesis rate is a plausible indirect mechanism

---

## Bridge Relevance

**HIGHEST MECHANISTIC RELEVANCE.** This paper establishes that FDX2 and FXN form a regulatory circuit controlling Fe-S synthesis rate. The electron donor FDX2 depends on NADPH (circadianly oscillating) for its reduction. This creates a direct mechanistic route: CLOCK/BMAL1 → NAD(P)H oscillation → FDX2 electron availability → Fe-S synthesis rate → downstream Fe-S dependent processes.
