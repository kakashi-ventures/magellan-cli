# Mitochondrial glutaredoxin Grx5 functions as a central hub for cellular iron-sulfur cluster assembly

**Authors:** Ashutosh K Pandey, Jayashree Pain, Pratibha Singh, Andrew Dancis, Debkumar Pain

**Journal:** Journal of Biological Chemistry

**Year:** 2025 (Epub March 10, 2025)

**PMID:** 40074084

**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jbc.2025.108391

**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40074084/

---

## Abstract (from PubMed fetch)

Grx5 serves as a pivotal control point in iron-sulfur cluster biogenesis. Isolated wild-type mitochondria can synthesize new Fe-S clusters, but isolated wild-type cytoplasm alone cannot do so unless mixed with wild-type mitochondria. Wild-type mitochondria generate an intermediate (Fe-S)int that is exported to the cytoplasm.

---

## Key Findings

### GLRX5 as Three-Way Traffic Controller
1. **Direct transfer:** GLRX5 transfers [2Fe-2S] clusters directly to recipient mitochondrial proteins
2. **ISA pathway routing:** Transfers clusters to ISA1/ISA2 complex for [4Fe-4S] cluster generation
3. **Export routing:** Transfers clusters to ATM1/ABCB7 transporter for cytoplasmic export

### Mitochondrial vs. Cytoplasmic Assembly
- Grx5-depleted mitochondria FAIL to synthesize Fe-S clusters for organellar proteins
- Loss of Grx5 prevents production of the exported (Fe-S)int intermediate
- The function is fully rescued by importing purified Grx5 protein

### Clinical Relevance
- GLRX5 is conserved from yeast to humans
- Human GLRX5 mutations cause sideroblastic anemia and insulin-dependent diabetes
- All three routing functions appear conserved

---

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **GLRX5 holds [2Fe-2S] as a bridging cluster between two monomers** (confirmed by crystal structure: PDB human GLRX5)
- **Cluster transfer is REDOX-DEPENDENT:** requires glutathione (GSH) and appropriate redox state
- **GLRX5 redox potential:** ~-270 to -300 mV (from glutaredoxin family analogy; exact value for GLRX5 [2Fe-2S] complex not in this paper)
- **Rate-limiting:** if GLRX5 is depleted, ALL downstream Fe-S cluster recipients are affected

---

## Bridge Relevance

GLRX5 is the CENTRAL ROUTING HUB for ALL cellular Fe-S cluster distribution. Its GSH-dependent redox mechanism means that changes in mitochondrial GSH/GSSG ratio (which oscillates circadianly) could directly alter GLRX5 cluster transfer activity. No circadian content in this paper.
