# Acute inhibition of iron-sulfur cluster biosynthesis disrupts metabolic flexibility in mice

**Authors:** Marte Molenaars, Hannan Mir, Samantha W Alvarez, Lakshmi Arivazhagan, Carolina Rosselot, Di Zhan, Christopher Y Park, Adolfo Garcia-Ocana, Ann Marie Schmidt, Richard Possemato

**Journal:** bioRxiv (preprint)

**Year:** 2024

**DOI:** 10.1101/2024.08.19.608291

**URL:** https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2024.08.19.608291v1.full

---

## Abstract Summary

Suppressing NFS1 (key ISC biosynthetic enzyme) causes rapid loss of white adipose tissue and metabolic shifts toward lipid utilization in mice, without changes in activity or food intake. Effects are fully reversible upon NFS1 restoration.

---

## Key Findings

### Disrupted Fe-S Dependent Processes (upon NFS1 suppression)
- **OXPHOS function:** SDHB (Complex II) levels decreased
- **Lipoic acid synthesis:** LIAS protein reduced
- **Insulin production:** CDKAL1 (tRNA modification enzyme) downregulated in pancreatic β-cells
- **TCA cycle:** Aconitase (ACO2) affected
- **Iron sensing:** Liver-specific activation of iron starvation response via IRP regulation

### Metabolic Phenotypes
- Shift from carbohydrate to lipid oxidation (RER decreased from 0.92 to 0.8)
- 20% body weight loss within two weeks; >60% adipose tissue loss
- Elevated plasma lactate and TCA intermediates
- Glucose intolerance with insulin insufficiency
- Upregulation of mitochondrial stress markers (ATF4, ATF5, PGC1a)

### Time Course of Effects
- Days 3-4: RER begins declining; metabolic shift initiates
- Day 4: Glucose intolerance develops
- Day 10: Substantial transcriptional changes across tissues
- Week 2: Humane endpoint (20% weight loss)
- Fully reversible upon NFS1 restoration

### Age-Dependent Modulation
- Aged (1-year-old) mice: blunted weight loss, preserved glucose tolerance, normalized RER
- Suggests age-related changes in metabolic flexibility interact with Fe-S biogenesis

---

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **NFS1 is rate-limiting for ALL downstream Fe-S dependent processes** — demonstrated in vivo
- Aconitase (ACO2, mitochondrial) is Fe-S dependent and rate-limiting for TCA cycle
- **IRP1/iron sensing activated by NFS1 suppression** — direct evidence that Fe-S biogenesis rate controls IRP1 switch in vivo
- The metabolic shift (carb→lipid) has circadian implications: circadian clock controls metabolic fuel selection

---

## Bridge Relevance

In vivo proof that NFS1 is rate-limiting for multiple Fe-S dependent metabolic processes, including IRP-mediated iron sensing. The circadian clock also controls metabolic fuel selection (carb vs. lipid oscillates daily) — if NFS1 activity oscillates, this could drive circadian oscillations in metabolic substrate use via Fe-S cluster-dependent enzymes.
