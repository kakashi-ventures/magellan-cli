# d-cysteine impairs tumour growth by inhibiting cysteine desulfurase NFS1

**Authors:** Zangari J, et al. (University of Geneva group)

**Journal:** Nature Metabolism

**Year:** 2025

**PMID:** 40797101

**DOI:** 10.1038/s42255-025-01339-1

**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s42255-025-01339-1

---

## Abstract (from search result summary)

D-cysteine targets NFS1, affecting Fe-S cluster biogenesis and impairing tumour growth. D-cysteine is selectively imported into xCT/CD98-overexpressing cancer cell lines. Intracellular d-Cys specifically inhibits the mitochondrial cysteine desulfurase NFS1 by blocking sulfur mobilization due to steric constraints. The (NIA)2 complex has 12-fold lower affinity for d-Cys (Kdapp = 25.6 µM) compared with l-Cys (Kdapp = 2.15 µM).

---

## Key Findings

### Mechanism of NFS1 Inhibition
- D-cysteine is the mirror image (enantiomer) of L-cysteine
- Competes with L-cysteine for the NFS1 active site
- Steric constraints prevent sulfur mobilization from d-Cys
- Selective competitive inhibition: Kdapp (d-Cys) = 25.6 µM vs. Kdapp (l-Cys) = 2.15 µM — 12-fold selectivity window
- Cancer cell specificity via overexpression of transporters xCT/CD98

### Downstream Consequences of NFS1 Inhibition
- **Mitochondrial respiration** decreased (OXPHOS disruption)
- **Nucleotide metabolism** impaired (DPYD, other Fe-S enzymes)
- **Genome integrity** compromised (XPD, FANCJ helicases)
- Decreased oxygen consumption, DNA damage, cell cycle arrest

### In Vivo Results
- Mice with aggressive mammary tumors: significant tumor growth slowing
- No major side effects in normal tissue (due to transporter selectivity)

---

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **NFS1 Km for L-cysteine:** ~2.15 µM (Kdapp from Zangari 2025) — NOTE: literature also cites ~50 µM from earlier kinetic studies; Kdapp and Km may differ
- **NFS1 active site:** PLP-dependent, flexible Cys-loop (Cys381) transfers persulfide
- **Inhibition IC50 in cancer cells:** 16.3 ± 1.92 µM (Zhu et al. 2025 found similar)
- **Specificity:** d-Cys blocks sulfur mobilization; does NOT inactivate CBS or CSE

---

## Bridge Relevance

First pharmacological proof that partial NFS1 inhibition has systemic consequences for all Fe-S dependent processes (respiration, nucleotide synthesis, DNA repair). Demonstrates that NFS1 activity is the rate-limiting node for cellular Fe-S cluster supply. Circadian connection: none mentioned.
