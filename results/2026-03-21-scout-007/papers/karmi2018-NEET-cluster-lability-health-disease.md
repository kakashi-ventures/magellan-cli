# The unique fold and lability of the [2Fe-2S] clusters of NEET proteins mediate their key functions in health and disease

**Authors:** Ola Karmi, Henri-Baptiste Marjault, Luca Pesce, Paolo Carloni, Jose N Onuchic, Patricia A Jennings, Ron Mittler, Rachel Nechushtai

**Journal:** Journal of Biological Inorganic Chemistry

**Year:** 2018

**PMID:** 29435647

**DOI:** 10.1007/s00775-018-1538-8

**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6006223/

---

## Abstract Summary (from full text fetch)

Reviews three human NEET proteins (mitoNEET/CISD1, NAF-1/CISD2, MiNT/CISD3). Their [2Fe-2S] clusters are coordinated by 3Cys:1His (unusual — most [2Fe-2S] proteins use 4Cys). The histidine coordination confers unique lability. NEET proteins regulate iron homeostasis, ROS, apoptosis, and autophagy through their distinctive cluster chemistry.

---

## Key Findings

### pH-Dependent Cluster Lability
- Coordinating His residue is easily protonated, conferring unusual cluster lability
- **Lowering pH induces accelerated cluster loss** — half-life significantly decreased at acidic pH
- His→Cys mutation (H87C/H114C) STABILIZES cluster — demonstrates His coordination drives lability

### Redox-Coupled Proton Transfer
- Cluster reduction is COUPLED TO PROTON UPTAKE at coordinating His
- pKa range: 6.5-6.8 (physiologically relevant)
- Reduction → protonation → cluster destabilization → cluster release

### NEET Protein Functions
- **mitoNEET (CISD1):** Outer mitochondrial membrane; regulates mitochondrial Fe-S maturation
- **NAF-1 (CISD2):** ER and MAM; regulates ER-mitochondria Ca²⁺ transfer; longevity gene
- **MiNT (CISD3):** Inner mitochondrial membrane; least studied

### Cluster Transfer Properties
- NEET clusters transfer to acceptor proteins WITHOUT chaperones
- Transfer is pH and redox-dependent
- Reduced cluster is more labile → paradoxical release under reducing conditions

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **CISD2 cluster half-life:** pH-dependent; shorter at lower pH (exact values require original papers)
- **Cluster transfer rate:** Spontaneous; temperature-dependent; ~minutes timescale under physiological conditions
- **CISD2 at ER-MAM:** Regulates Ca²⁺ transfer; Ca²⁺ regulates circadian clock timing
- **No circadian content** in this paper

---

## Bridge Relevance

CISD2/NAF-1 sits at the ER-mitochondria junction (MAM) and regulates Ca²⁺ transfer. Circadian Ca²⁺ oscillations in the ER/MAM regulate clock gene expression. If CISD2 cluster lability varies with time-of-day (via pH oscillations in the mitochondrial matrix), this could create a circadian feedback loop through Ca²⁺ dynamics. This is an indirect but mechanistically plausible bridge route.
