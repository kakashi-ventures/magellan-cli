# Two-stage binding of mitochondrial ferredoxin-2 to the core iron-sulfur cluster assembly complex

**Authors:** Ralf Steinhilper, Linda Boß, Sven-A Freibert, Vinzent Schulz, Nils Krapoth, Susann Kaltwasser, Roland Lill, Bonnie J Murphy

**Journal:** Nature Communications

**Year:** 2024

**PMID:** 39632806

**DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-024-54585-4

**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-54585-4

---

## Abstract (from PubMed fetch)

Cryo-EM structures of the human FDX2-bound core ISC complex reveal two distinct conformational states of FDX2 binding. FDX2 and FXN compete for overlapping binding sites. The two-stage binding mechanism provides structural insights into how FDX2 functions as an electron donor during iron-sulfur cluster assembly.

---

## Key Findings

### Two-Stage Binding Mechanism
1. **Distal conformation:** FDX2's helix F forms electrostatic interactions with an arginine-rich patch on NFS1. Fe-S cluster of FDX2 positioned **23 Å** from the ISCU2 assembly site. Low-affinity, initial docking state.

2. **Proximal conformation:** FDX2 C-terminus rigidifies and binds more tightly to NFS1. [2Fe-2S] cluster moves to within **14 Å** of the ISCU2 assembly site. Enables efficient electron transfer for persulfide reduction.

### Competition with Frataxin
- FDX2 and FXN share overlapping binding sites on the ISC complex
- Sequential mechanism: FXN facilitates sulfur transfer → FDX2 binds for cluster maturation
- This competition creates a stoichiometric regulatory node

### Structural Details
- Resolution sufficient to visualize distinct conformational states
- Arginine patch on NFS1 is key electrostatic anchor for FDX2 initial binding
- FDX2-specific C-terminus contacts are required for proximal (active) conformation

---

## Key Parameters for Bridge Hypothesis

- **FDX2 redox potential:** ~-270 mV (estimated from adrenoxin analogy; not directly measured in this paper)
- **Electron transfer distance:** 14 Å in proximal conformation (within range for efficient electron tunneling)
- **Rate-limiting step:** Distal→proximal conformational change

---

## Bridge Relevance

Establishes that FDX2 is an ELECTRON DONOR whose binding is conformationally regulated. The FDX2/FXN competition creates a tunable regulatory circuit. If FDX2 or FXN levels change (e.g., circadianly), this competition would directly modulate [2Fe-2S] cluster synthesis rate. Circadian connection: none mentioned.
