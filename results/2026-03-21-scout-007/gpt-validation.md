- **Stehling 2013** shows that late CIA specialization exists, especially for **IRP1** via **CIA2A/FAM96A**.
- There is broad circadian literature for **daily oscillations in redox state and iron handling**, but I do **not** see an established literature explicitly naming the **CIA pathway** as a circadian gate for the cytosolic Fe-S proteome.
- I also do not see convincing evidence for a **coherent daily rhythm across ~20 CIA clients** driven by CIA flux itself.

**Synthesize**
- The **stress-responsive CIA mechanism** is known.
- The **circadian gatekeeper framing** is new.
- So this is **PARTIALLY EXPLORED**: mechanistic pieces are established, system-level circadian integration is not.

### 2. **Counter-Evidence**
- The phrase **“entire cytoplasmic Fe-S proteome”** is probably too broad. Many client proteins are long-lived; they need not be rematured every 24 h.
- Published CIAO3 sensitivity is often demonstrated under **strong perturbation** (iron chelation, oxidant stress, oxygen changes), not subtle physiological daily oscillations.
- Different Fe-S clients have different maturation routes and lifetimes; a single **CIAO3 bottleneck** may not impose coherent daily synchrony.
- Apparent activity rhythms in cytosolic Fe-S enzymes could easily come from **transcription, translation, localization, or substrate rhythms**, not Fe-S insertion.

### 3. **Mechanism Plausibility**
- **Moderate for a subset of newly synthesized or borderline-sensitive clients.**
- **Weak-to-moderate for the full-proteome version.**
- The best version of the hypothesis is: circadian LIP/ROS rhythms modulate **CIA efficiency for selected nascent cytosolic/nuclear Fe-S proteins**.
- The weakest part is the claim of coordinated daily maturation of essentially the whole client set.

### 4. **Experimental Design**
**Minimal viable targeted test**
- **Model:** Primary hepatocytes or mouse liver under restricted feeding to maximize physiological rhythms.
- **Sampling:** Every **6 h** across 24 h.
- **Primary assays:**  
  - Endogenous **CIAO3 co-IP** and immunoblot for scaffold/late components  
  - Short **^55Fe pulse labeling** before each harvest  
  - Immunoprecipitate **ABCE1** as a general CIA client and **IRP1** as a specialized comparator  
  - Measure total protein abundance to separate maturation from expression
- **Perturbations:**  
  - **DFO** or holotransferrin/ferric ammonium citrate for iron  
  - low-dose **H2O2/menadione** or **mitoTEMPO/NAC** for redox
- **Success criterion:** Time-of-day-dependent CIAO3 complex formation and **new Fe-S incorporation** into at least one general client, with flattening under iron chelation or oxidant stress.

### 5. **Final Assessment**
- **Original confidence:** 5/10  
- **Updated confidence:** 4/10  
- **Change reason:** CIAO3’s sensitivity to iron/ROS is real, but the leap to a **daily gate for the whole cytoplasmic Fe-S proteome** is too broad for current evidence.  
- **Novelty status:** PARTIALLY EXPLORED  
- **Counter-evidence:** Client half-lives differ widely; physiological daily redox/iron oscillations may be too small; many observed rhythms may be upstream of Fe-S maturation.  
- **Experimental feasibility:** MEDIUM  
- **Recommended next step:** Narrow the claim to **2–3 sentinel clients** and test **time-of-day-dependent ^55Fe incorporation** plus **CIAO3 co-IP**.

## H2.2: Frataxin-Gated Fe-S Assembly via Mitochondrial LIP in FTMT-Negative Tissues

### 1. **Novelty Verdict** — **PARTIALLY EXPLORED**
**Plan**
- “frataxin mitochondrial ferritin liver circadian iron”
- “Santambrogio 2007 FTMT liver hepatocyte”
- “FDX2 frataxin stoichiometry Fe-S assembly”
- “mitochondrial labile iron liver circadian”
- “Friedreich carrier circadian iron metabolism”

**Retrieve**
- **Santambrogio 2007** supports the important tissue fact that **FTMT is low/absent in liver hepatocytes** under basal conditions.
- Mitochondrial Fe-S assembly absolutely depends on the **FXN/NFS1/ISCU/FDX2** core machinery, and recent mechanistic work supports strong dependence on proper partner stoichiometry.
- Circadian/systemic iron rhythms are real, and the liver is a plausible tissue for testing whether those rhythms propagate to mitochondrial Fe-S flux.
- What I do **not** see established is a direct line from **plasma iron rhythm → hepatocyte mitochondrial LIP rhythm → FXN-sensitive Fe-S assembly rhythm**, nor evidence that **FTMT absence is the key amplifier**.

**Synthesize**
- The parts are grounded, but the exact amplification model is not.
- This is **PARTIALLY EXPLORED**.

### 2. **Counter-Evidence**
- **Frataxin’s exact role is still debated**: direct iron donor vs allosteric activator/chaperone. That weakens a model that depends specifically on **Fe²⁺ donation by FXN** as the gated step.
- FTMT is not the only way mitochondria avoid free iron swings; trafficking, utilization, export, and whole-cell ferritin buffering can still stabilize matrix iron.
- Liver is a central iron-handling organ; systemic serum iron oscillation may not map simply onto hepatocyte mitochondrial LIP.
- The prediction for **FA carriers** is not obviously right: partial FXN limitation might **flatten** dynamic range instead of exaggerating it.

### 3. **Mechanism Plausibility**
- **Moderate.**
- Low hepatic FTMT makes liver a sensible place to look for dynamic mitochondrial iron handling.
- The strongest part is that mitochondrial Fe-S assembly could be **more labile in FTMT-low tissue**.
- The weakest part is assigning the dominant bottleneck specifically to **FXN-gated Fe²⁺ availability** rather than to broader mitochondrial iron traffic or sulfur/electron supply.

### 4. **Experimental Design**
**Minimal viable in vivo test**
- **Model:** Adult mice under ad lib vs restricted feeding.
- **Key manipulation:** Hepatocyte-specific **AAV8-TBG-FTMT** overexpression vs AAV control.
- **Sampling:** Liver every **6 h** over 24 h.
- **Readouts:**  
  - Plasma iron, transferrin saturation, hepcidin  
  - Isolated liver mitochondrial labile iron using a validated matrix-iron assay/probe  
  - Short **^55Fe incorporation** into mitochondrial Fe-S proteins (e.g., **SDHB**, **NDUFS1**)  
  - Mitochondrial aconitase and complex I/II activities  
  - FXN/NFS1/ISCU complex abundance or occupancy
- **Optional sensitization arm:** **Fxn+/-** mice.
- **Critical test:** If FTMT absence is the amplifier, liver-specific FTMT expression should **dampen amplitude** of mito iron/Fe-S oscillation without necessarily shifting mean levels dramatically.

### 5. **Final Assessment**
- **Original confidence:** 6/10  
- **Updated confidence:** 4.5/10  
- **Change reason:** The liver/FTMT angle is interesting and testable, but the mechanism is weakened by uncertainty over **frataxin’s exact biochemical role** and by multiple alternative buffering layers for mitochondrial iron.  
- **Novelty status:** PARTIALLY EXPLORED  
- **Counter-evidence:** FXN may not be a simple iron donor gate; FTMT absence may not be sufficient to create an “unbuffered” matrix LIP; FA-carrier prediction may have the wrong sign.  
- **Experimental feasibility:** MEDIUM  
- **Recommended next step:** Test **hepatocyte-specific FTMT gain-of-function** as a damping experiment before pursuing carrier genetics.

## H2.7: Conserved Fe-S Requirement in Clock Neurons — Drosophila to Mammalian SCN

### 1. **Novelty Verdict** — **PARTIALLY EXPLORED**
**Plan**
- “Mandilaras Missirlis 2012 Fe-S circadian Drosophila clock neurons”
- “SCN NFS1 iron sulfur circadian mouse”
- “SCN mitochondrial respiration firing rate circadian”
- “conditional NFS1 knockout neurons circadian behavior”
- “SCN Fe-S biogenesis preprint”

**Retrieve**
- **Mandilaras & Missirlis 2012** is the key direct precedent: perturbing Fe-S biogenesis in **Drosophila clock neurons** disrupts circadian locomotor behavior.
- Mammalian SCN biology strongly supports the background premise that SCN neurons undergo large day/night changes in **firing rate** and depend on mitochondrial metabolism.
- I do **not** see an established mammalian literature directly testing **NFS1**, **ISCU**, **FXN**, or related ISC machinery in the **SCN**.
- So the mammalian claim remains a **reasonable but untested conservation hypothesis**.

**Synthesize**
- The fly result makes this more than a wild guess.
- The mammalian extrapolation is still not known.
- Verdict: **PARTIALLY EXPLORED**.

### 2. **Counter-Evidence**
- **Full NFS1 loss** is likely to cause broad mitochondrial collapse and eventual cell dysfunction/death; arrhythmicity could be a nonspecific lesion effect.
- SCN rhythms can persist at the molecular level even when output physiology is altered; a metabolic defect may reduce amplitude rather than abolish the core oscillator.
- SCN network compensation, including **astrocyte–neuron interactions**, may preserve some rhythmicity.
- The lack of mammalian follow-up over many years is a weak but real cautionary signal.

### 3. **Mechanism Plausibility**
- **Moderate-to-high** for the statement: “normal SCN neuronal function requires intact Fe-S biogenesis.”
- **Moderate** for the stronger prediction: “conditional NFS1 loss will abolish behavioral rhythms.”
- The main issue is specificity: Fe-S failure would hit **ETC complexes, aconitase, lipoate-dependent metabolism, and ROS control** all at once. That makes the phenotype likely, but not necessarily clock-specific.

### 4. **Experimental Design**
**Minimal viable mammalian test**
- **Model:** Adult **Nfs1 flox** mice or wild-type mice with SCN-targeted knockdown.
- **Preferred perturbation:** Bilateral SCN injection of **AAV-shNfs1** or CRISPRi for a **partial knockdown** first (50–70%), to avoid immediate lesion-like toxicity.
- **Controls:** Scramble AAV, off-target hypothalamic injection control.
- **Readouts:**  
  - Wheel-running in LD then **constant darkness**  
  - Body temperature telemetry  
  - Ex vivo **SCN PER2::LUC** slice rhythms  
  - SCN firing rhythms by MEA or patch-clamp  
  - Histology for neuron survival and gliosis  
  - Complex I/II and aconitase activity in SCN punches
- **Decision rule:** If rhythm amplitude drops **before** major neuron loss and is mirrored in **SCN slice rhythms**, that supports a bona fide Fe-S requirement for clock circuitry.

### 5. **Final Assessment**
- **Original confidence:** 6/10  
- **Updated confidence:** 5/10  
- **Change reason:** The fly precedent is real and the SCN metabolic rationale is good, but a clean mammalian test must separate **clock physiology** from **general neurotoxicity**.  
- **Novelty status:** PARTIALLY EXPLORED  
- **Counter-evidence:** Full ISC loss may simply lesion the SCN; molecular clock and network compensation may persist longer than behavior; absence of mammalian follow-up tempers confidence.  
- **Experimental feasibility:** MEDIUM  
- **Recommended next step:** Do an **adult SCN-targeted partial NFS1 knockdown** with parallel **wheel-running + PER2::LUC slice** readouts.
