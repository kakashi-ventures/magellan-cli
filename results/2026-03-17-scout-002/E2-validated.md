# Wound-Induced Topological Defects as Permanent Niches via ECM Stiffness Pinning

## VERDICT: KILLED — FACTUAL ERRORS + CONTRADICTED

**Original Score**: 7.1 composite, 6/10 confidence, 6/10 groundedness
**Post-Validation**: Novelty 3/10, Confidence 2/10, Groundedness 2/10
**Reason for KILL**: Contains a fabricated biological claim (R-spondin GPI-anchor), key prediction contradicted by published data, and defect persistence assumption falsified.

---

## Fatal Problems

### 1. R-spondin Biology is WRONG (Hallucination)
The hypothesis claims R-spondin is GPI-anchored with D ~ 1-5 μm²/s. R-spondin is actually a ~35 kDa **secreted protein** that binds ECM proteoglycans (Genome Biology 2012, Genes Dev 2014). This collapses the entire "converging flow concentrates R-spondin" mechanism (claim 4). This is a parametric hallucination — the kind the Groundedness scoring and Hallucination-as-Novelty check were designed to catch.

### 2. WIHN Occurs at SOFT Sites, Not Stiff Pinned Sites
A 2022 paper (Pharmaceutics, PMC9504897) showed new hair follicles form where tissue is **softest** (~10-14 kPa), not stiffest. If ECM stiffness pins defects, pinned defect sites would be stiff — exactly where WIHN does NOT occur. The spatial prediction is inverted.

### 3. Defects Annihilate Upon Wound Closure
A 2025 preprint on wound closure in active nematic epithelia shows defects move inward and annihilate during healing, leaving "no apparent memory of the wound." The hypothesis requires defect persistence, but the default topological physics predicts annihilation. No validated biological mechanism prevents this.

## Additional Issues
- SDF-1/CXCR4 chemotaxis is well-established for wound stem cell recruitment
- Marjolin's ulcer connection is speculative (chronic inflammation is the established cause)
- The defect-stem-cell connection at wounds is partially published (MSC condensation at defects, 2024-2025)
- Groundedness: ~35% (below 50% threshold) after correcting R-spondin error

## Salvageable Elements
A heavily revised hypothesis could propose defects at soft-stiff BOUNDARIES (not stiff sites), correct R-spondin biology, and address defect persistence. But this would be essentially a new hypothesis.
