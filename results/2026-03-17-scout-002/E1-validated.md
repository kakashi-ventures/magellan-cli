# Validated Hypothesis Card: E1
# Activity-Dependent Crypt Fission via Nematic Defect-Splitting Threshold

## VERDICT: WOUNDED — PARTIALLY EXPLORED

**Original Score**: 7.3 composite, 6/10 confidence, 5/10 groundedness
**Post-Validation**: Novelty 5/10, Confidence 4/10, Groundedness 4/10
**Status**: Survives with reduced novelty. The specific quantitative prediction (defect-splitting threshold → crypt fission) is not yet published, but the broader framework is active.

---

## Novelty: PARTIALLY EXPLORED (5/10)

The general idea that topological defects drive epithelial morphogenesis is well-established (Vafa & Mahadevan 2022, Saw 2017). A 2024 arXiv preprint (2403.05276) explicitly mentions +1 topological defects at intestinal crypt bases. However:

- Nobody has published the specific quantitative model: α_c ~ K/R² as the fission trigger
- The blebbistatin-blocks-fission-despite-Wnt prediction is genuinely discriminating
- The daughter crypt opening angle prediction has not been tested

## Counter-Evidence

### SERIOUS:
1. **Inflation-collapse mechanism** (Tallapragada 2021, Cell Stem Cell): CFTR → crypt inflation → Piezo1 → Lgr5 loss. Complete pathway with pharmacological validation. No defects needed.
2. **Differential adhesion model** (Langlands 2016, PLOS Biology): Paneth cell stiffness + β4-integrin adhesion. Also explains fission without defects.
3. **2D-to-3D gap**: Giomi 2014 threshold α_c ~ K/R² derived for flat 2D. Crypts are 3D tubular invaginations. No validation of the scaling in this geometry.

### MANAGEABLE:
4. Nematic order in intestinal epithelium unverified (shared assumption with all S2 hypotheses)
5. ISC neutral drift could explain fission stochastically

## Why It Survives

- The daughter crypt angle prediction (<30° relative to nematic director for >70% of events) is genuinely testable and discriminating
- The quantitative threshold model provides a specific numerical prediction that molecular models don't
- No one has explicitly tested whether contractility alone (without Wnt) can trigger or prevent fission
