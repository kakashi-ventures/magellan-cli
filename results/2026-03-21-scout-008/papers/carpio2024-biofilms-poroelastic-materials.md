# Paper: Biofilms as Poroelastic Materials

## Citation
- **Authors:** Carpio A, Cebrián E, Espeso DR, Vidal P
- **ArXiv:** 2401.07060
- **Year:** Originally published International Journal of Non-linear Mechanics 109, 1-8, 2019; ArXiv preprint posted January 2024
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.07060

## Abstract / Key Findings
Develops a poroelastic/biphasic mixture framework for biofilms spreading on agar surfaces. Biofilm modeled as bicomponent mixture of incompressible solid matrix (bacterial cells and polymers) and interstitial fluid carrying nutrients. Incorporates both Darcy flow (pressure-driven) and osmotic flow (concentration-gradient-driven).

## Framework Details
- Truly biphasic: separate solid and fluid phases
- Darcy law equation: J_d = -k_h ∇p (hydraulic permeability k_h is a parameter)
- Osmotic flow: J_π = β·ω·k_h·∇c_e (concentration-driven)
- Osmotic pressure via Van't Hoff equation (π = βc_e) — NOT Donnan equilibrium formulation
- Lamé constants λ, μ for solid matrix elasticity

## Critical Absence
- Does NOT cite Mow 1980 biphasic theory
- Does NOT use Donnan equilibrium formulation (only Van't Hoff)
- Does NOT define aggregate modulus as a measured parameter
- Does NOT discuss fixed charge density
- No reference to cartilage biomechanics literature
- Hydraulic permeability is a model parameter but NOT measured experimentally

## Why Selected
The closest mathematical analog to Mow's biphasic theory applied to biofilms. Yet it independently redevelops the framework without citing the 45 years of cartilage literature. This confirms the fields are not talking to each other — the biphasic framework is being reinvented for biofilms without the rich parameter database available from cartilage research.
