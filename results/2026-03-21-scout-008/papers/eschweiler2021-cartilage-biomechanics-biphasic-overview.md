# Paper: The Biomechanics of Cartilage — An Overview

## Citation
- **Authors:** Eschweiler J, Horn N, Rath B, Betsch M, Baroncini A, Tingart M, Migliorini F
- **Journal:** Life (Basel)
- **Year:** 2021
- **PMC:** PMC8065530
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8065530/

## Abstract / Key Findings
Comprehensive review of articular cartilage biomechanics covering the full biphasic and triphasic theoretical frameworks. Articular cartilage is modeled as a composite of collagen-proteoglycan solid matrix and interstitial fluid. The tissue exhibits time-dependent viscoelastic behavior due to fluid-solid interactions under load.

## Key Quantitative Parameters
- **Aggregate modulus (Ha):** 0.5–0.9 MPa (equilibrium stiffness when all fluid flow ceases)
- **Hydraulic permeability (k):** ~0.44–1.42 × 10⁻¹⁵ m⁴/Ns (range from literature)
- **Fixed charge density (FCD):** up to ~0.2 mEq/g from sulfated GAGs (chondroitin sulfate, keratan sulfate)
- **Cartilage thickness:** 1–5 mm (typical articular cartilage)

## Biphasic Theory Framework (Mow 1980)
Three phases under load:
1. Solid matrix stress (intrinsic ECM stiffness)
2. Interstitial fluid pressure (hydrostatic load support during transient loading)
3. Frictional drag between solid-fluid phases

Triphasic extension (Lai et al. 1991): adds ionic phase accounting for Donnan osmotic pressure from fixed negative charges on proteoglycans. Donnan osmotic pressure (~3 atm at physiological FCD in 0.15 M NaCl) contributes to both equilibrium swelling and compressive stiffness.

## Why Selected
Provides the quantitative parameter baseline for comparison with biofilm literature. Aggregate modulus (0.5–0.9 MPa), hydraulic permeability (~10⁻¹⁵ m⁴/Ns), and FCD (~0.2 mEq/g) are the key parameters unmeasured in biofilms. The triphasic framework is the "destination" model to which biofilm mechanics should be compared.
