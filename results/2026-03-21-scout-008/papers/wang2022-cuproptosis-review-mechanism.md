## Abstract
Commentary on Tsvetkov et al. 2022. Reviews the mechanism by which excess intracellular copper induces cuproptosis via DLAT oligomerization and proteotoxic stress, distinct from all known cell death pathways.

## Key Findings

### Mechanism Summary
- Cu(II) is reduced to Cu(I) in mitochondria — the Cu2+/Cu+ redox couple is central
- Cu(I) binds lipoylated DLAT → disulfide-dependent DLAT oligomerization
- Simultaneously: FDX1-mediated loss of Fe-S cluster proteins
- Combined proteotoxic + metabolic stress → cell death

### Copper Speciation Details
- Cu+ (monovalent) is the active cuproptosis-inducing species
- Cu2+ reduction to Cu+ by FDX1 is the gating step
- Cells with high OXPHOS have more lipoylated proteins → more sensitive
- Cells in glycolysis mode have fewer lipoylated proteins → more resistant

### Therapeutic Implications
- FDX1 expression level predicts cuproptosis sensitivity
- DLAT lipoylation level is a secondary biomarker
- Elesclomol + copper combination exploits this pathway

## Relevance to Session 008
Provides concise summary of the Cu2+/Cu+ speciation transition as the critical gating step. In hydrothermal geochemistry, this same Cu2+→Cu+ transition is governed by Eh-pH conditions — a quantitative framework absent from cuproptosis biology.
