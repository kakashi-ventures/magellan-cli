## Abstract (reconstructed from search results)

This study identifies 34 CuZnSOD sequences in bacteria that closely resemble the eukaryotic form. The majority of these sequences were retrieved by environmental sampling of aquatic environments — including several examples recovered from marine hydrothermal vents. The paper traces the evolutionary development of copper handling from bacterial ancestors to eukaryotic copper chaperone systems.

## Key Findings

1. **Hydrothermal vent bacteria have CuZnSOD**: Among the 34 bacterial CuZnSOD-like proteins, several were recovered from marine hydrothermal vent environments — indicating that bacteria thriving in Cu-rich vent environments have specialized copper-handling proteins.

2. **Ferredoxin-fold copper domain**: The unification of a bacterial CuZnSOD with a ferredoxin-fold MXCXXC copper-binding domain within a single polypeptide created the advanced copper delivery system for CuZnSODs. This ferredoxin-fold domain is the evolutionary ancestor of the human copper chaperone CCS.

3. **Eukaryogenesis copper challenge**: The development of this ferredoxin-Cu delivery system facilitated evolution of large and compartmentalized eukaryotic cells following endosymbiotic eukaryogenesis — suggesting that copper management was a key selective pressure in eukaryote evolution.

4. **MXCXXC motif conservation**: The MXCXXC copper-binding motif is shared between bacterial vent proteins and human copper chaperones (ATOX1, CCS, COX17), indicating deep evolutionary conservation originating in Cu-rich environments.

## Relevance to Cross-Field Connection

- **Hydrothermal vent origin of copper chaperones**: The finding that CuZnSOD ancestors exist in hydrothermal vent bacteria directly connects the vent geochemical environment to the evolution of biological copper handling systems — the same systems that are now the targets/effectors of cuproptosis
- **Ferredoxin-fold + copper binding**: The MXCXXC domain fused to a ferredoxin-fold is structurally adjacent to FDX1's biology — ferredoxins and copper chaperones co-evolved in bacteria from Cu-rich environments (vents)
- This paper provides evidence that the ferredoxin-copper axis (FDX1 reducing Cu²⁺ in cuproptosis) may be an ancient function that originated when ferredoxin-like proteins encountered Cu²⁺ at hydrothermal vents — precisely the evolutionary echo hypothesis
