# Paper: Biphasic Creep and Stress Relaxation of Articular Cartilage in Compression: Theory and Experiments

## Citation
- **Authors:** Mow VC, Kuei SC, Lai WM, Armstrong CG
- **Journal:** Journal of Biomechanical Engineering
- **Year:** 1980
- **Volume/Issue:** 102, 73-84
- **DOI:** Foundational paper — widely cited, >3000 citations
- **URL:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/15817558

## Abstract / Key Findings
THE founding paper of biphasic theory for cartilage. Models articular cartilage as intrinsically incompressible solid-fluid mixture:
- Solid phase: collagen-proteoglycan network (porous, permeable)
- Fluid phase: interstitial fluid (incompressible, moves through pores)

Governing equations derived from mixture theory:
- Momentum balance for solid: div(σ) - K(v_s - v_f) = 0
- Momentum balance for fluid: -grad(p) - K(v_f - v_s) = 0
- Constitutive: σ = λ(div u)I + 2μe (solid matrix, Lamé constants)
- Darcy: q = -k·grad(p) (hydraulic permeability k)

## Key Parameters
- Aggregate modulus H_a = λ + 2μ (confined compression equilibrium)
- Hydraulic permeability k (determines fluid-flow rate)
- Both H_a and k measured from creep/relaxation curves
- Typical cartilage: H_a = 0.5–0.9 MPa, k = 10⁻¹⁴–10⁻¹⁶ m⁴/Ns

## Historical Significance
Extended by Lai et al. 1991 to triphasic theory including ionic phase + Donnan osmotic pressure. The H_a, k, FCD parameter space has been exhaustively mapped for articular, meniscal, and nucleus pulposus cartilage.

## Why Selected
The origin of the framework proposed to be applied to biofilms. The exact governing equations (mixture theory, Darcy flow, aggregate modulus) appear in biofilm poroelastic models (Carpio 2019) but without citing Mow and without experimental measurement of H_a and k in biofilms.
