# Paper: Pel is a Cationic Exopolysaccharide that Cross-Links Extracellular DNA in the P. aeruginosa Biofilm Matrix

## Citation
- **Authors:** Colvin KM, Irie Y, Tart CS, Urbano R, Whitney JC, Ryder C, et al.
- **Journal:** PNAS
- **Year:** 2012 (widely cited; also referenced in 2015 PNAS follow-up)
- **DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.1503058112
- **URL:** https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1503058112

## Abstract / Key Findings
Pel polysaccharide is cationic (positively charged), composed of acetylgalactosamine and acetylglucosamine. It cross-links extracellular DNA in the biofilm matrix via electrostatic interactions (ionic crosslinks). Key finding: Pel provides structural reinforcement through IONIC not covalent crosslinks.

## Charge State of P. aeruginosa Polysaccharides
- **Pel:** CATIONIC (amino sugars) — positive charge
- **Psl:** NEUTRAL (mannose/glucose/rhamnose repeats) — no charge
- **Alginate:** ANIONIC (guluronate/mannuronate carboxylate groups) — negative charge

## Why This Matters for Donnan/Biphasic Analysis
Charge heterogeneity within a single biofilm:
- Only alginate creates Donnan-type fixed charge effects (comparable to cartilage GAGs)
- Psl-dominant biofilms should show minimal osmotic swelling (confirmed experimentally)
- Pel creates CATIONIC zones that could counteract anionic eDNA/alginate effects
- Net fixed charge density of mixed-matrix biofilm is highly variable and unmeasured

This complexity is EXACTLY what the cartilage biphasic/triphasic framework was designed to handle — the FCD parameter captures the net balance of fixed charges.

## Why Selected
Defines the charge heterogeneity of the biofilm matrix that makes quantitative Donnan analysis both necessary and complex. The three polysaccharides span cationic, neutral, and anionic — a parameter space the cartilage field would characterize via FCD and relate to mechanical outcomes.
