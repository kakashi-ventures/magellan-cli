## Abstract
Tested hyperthermophilic vent archaea against heavy metals (zinc, cobalt, copper) with and without sulfide. Found that sulfide dramatically reduces metal toxicity by forming dissolved metal-sulfide complexes and precipitates, creating a natural chemical buffer.

## Key Findings

### Copper Toxicity Window
- Free Cu2+ lethal to vent archaea at ~1 μM concentrations
- Addition of sulfide (forming CuHS+ and CuS precipitates) extends tolerance to >1,000 μM
- Sulfide creates a "copper bioavailability window" — the speciation-defined zone where copper is available but not lethal

### Speciation-Controlled Bioavailability
- CuHS+ (soluble complex): bioavailable but reduced toxicity
- CuS (covellite, precipitate): essentially unavailable, non-toxic
- Free Cu2+: toxic at low concentrations
- Metal-sulfide complexes "act as a natural metal buffer that remains stable and oxidation resistant"

### Ecological Implications
- Vent archaea survival is NOT purely genetic adaptation — it is chemistry-enabled
- The Eh-pH composition of local vent fluid determines which copper species dominate
- Organisms living at different redox potentials in the vent gradient encounter different copper species

### Analogies to Cuproptosis Biology
- Hydrothermal vent archaea: sulfide complexation buffers Cu bioavailability
- Mammalian cells: metallothionein (MT) and glutathione buffer intracellular Cu bioavailability
- Vent archaea: CuHS+ → CuS precipitation removes Cu from solution
- Cancer cells with BACH1-MT axis: MT sequesters Cu to prevent cuproptosis (2025 Cancer Cell paper)
- The same thermodynamic principles (Cu speciation controlling toxicity) operate in both systems

## Relevance to Session 008
KEY BRIDGE CONCEPT: Demonstrates that copper speciation (not total copper concentration) controls toxicity in a sulfide-rich environment. The parallel to cuproptosis is direct: in both cases, it is the fraction of copper in a specific chemical form (free Cu2+ vs complexed Cu) that determines cell death. Hydrothermal geochemistry has a quantitative framework (Pourbaix/Eh-pH) for predicting this; cuproptosis biology does not.
