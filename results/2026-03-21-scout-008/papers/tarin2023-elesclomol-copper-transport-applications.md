## Abstract
Comprehensive review of elesclomol from discovery through novel applications (cancer, tuberculosis, COVID-19, copper metabolism disorders). Covers synthesis, mechanism, and clinical translation status.

## Key Findings

### Copper Transport Mechanism (Detailed)
- ELC binds Cu(II) extracellularly in 1:1 ratio
- Forms membrane-permeable ELC-Cu(II) complex
- Conditional stability constant: 24-fold higher than TRIEN chelator
- Selective mitochondrial delivery confirmed by subcellular fractionation
- FDX1 mediates reduction: ELC-Cu(II) + FDX1[2Fe-2S]_red → ELC + Cu(I) + FDX1[2Fe-2S]_ox

### Novel Applications
1. Cancer: cuproptosis-dependent, particularly OXPHOS-dependent tumors
2. Tuberculosis: synergistic with rifampicin (different mechanism — oxidative stress)
3. COVID-19: HSP70-dependent mechanism
4. Menkes disease: provides bioavailable Cu to copper-deficient cells
5. Cytochrome c oxidase deficiency: restores Complex IV assembly

### Thermodynamic Data
- Stability constant for ELC-Cu(II) indicates strong binding under physiological conditions
- No published E° for ELC-Cu2+/ELC-Cu+ couple in biological context
- No Eh-pH diagram published for ELC-Cu system

### Gap Identified
- The speciation of Cu within the ELC-Cu complex under different pH/Eh conditions is unknown
- Whether pH changes in the ischemic tumor microenvironment (pH 6.5-7.0) affect Cu speciation and ELC efficacy has not been studied
- Geochemical speciation modeling has never been applied to predict ELC-Cu bioavailability

## Relevance to Session 008
Identifies the key untested variable: the thermodynamic stability of ELC-Cu as a function of pH and Eh. The tumor microenvironment Eh (~-0 to -100 mV) and pH (6.5-7.0) differ from normal tissue — these differences in speciation have never been mapped using Pourbaix analysis. This is the therapeutic gap that geochemical speciation frameworks could fill.
