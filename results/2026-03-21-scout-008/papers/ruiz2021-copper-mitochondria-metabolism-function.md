## Abstract
Reviews copper's role in mitochondrial function, particularly in Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase) and SOD1. Covers copper transport to mitochondria (COX17, SCO1/2), copper's redox cycling, and how copper imbalance affects mitochondrial dynamics and bioenergetics.

## Key Findings

### Mitochondrial Copper Chemistry
- Complex IV contains 3 Cu ions: CuA center (2 Cu) + CuB center (1 Cu)
- CuA: primary acceptor of electrons from cytochrome c, E° ~ +245 mV vs NHE
- CuB: paired with heme a3, participates in O2 reduction to H2O
- Both centers cycle Cu2+ ↔ Cu+ during normal catalysis

### Redox Chemistry in Mitochondria
- Mitochondrial matrix Eh: approximately -250 to -300 mV (highly reducing)
- Mitochondrial intermembrane space Eh: less reducing (~-200 mV)
- At these Eh values, the Cu2+/Cu+ couple (E° = +159 mV vs NHE) would strongly favor Cu+
- Note: This analysis is implicit — the paper does not use Eh-pH/Pourbaix framework explicitly

### Copper Delivery to Mitochondria
- COX17 shuttles Cu+ from cytoplasm to IMS
- SCO1/SCO2 metalate CuA in Complex IV
- SLC25A3 transports copper from IMS to matrix
- CuL (low-molecular-weight ligand) as mitochondrial matrix copper pool

### Stress Response
- Excess copper: induces mitophagy, shifts balance toward mitochondrial fragmentation
- Deficiency: induces fusion (protective)
- ROS generation: Cu+ participates in Fenton/Haber-Weiss reactions

### Critical Gap Identified
- The paper describes copper redox chemistry functionally but never applies Eh-pH analysis
- The mitochondrial matrix is a well-defined Eh-pH environment (~pH 7.5-8.0, Eh ~-280 mV)
- Where does this plot on a Cu Pourbaix diagram? What species predominate? Not answered.

## Relevance to Session 008
Establishes that mitochondria have a well-defined redox potential (Eh ~ -250 to -300 mV, pH 7.5-8.0). These values define a specific region in a Cu Pourbaix diagram. No paper has plotted "mitochondrial matrix" as a point on the Cu Pourbaix diagram to predict which copper species are thermodynamically stable under physiological vs cuproptosis-inducing conditions.
