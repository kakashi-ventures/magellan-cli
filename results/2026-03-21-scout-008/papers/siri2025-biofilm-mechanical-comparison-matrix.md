# Paper: Mechanical Comparison of Biofilms with Altered Matrix Composition

## Citation
- **Authors:** Siri et al.
- **Journal:** ACS Biomaterials Science & Engineering
- **Year:** 2025
- **Volume/Issue:** 11(7), 4523–4536
- **PMC:** PMC12264770
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12264770/

## Abstract / Key Findings
Measures mechanical properties of E. coli biofilms with different matrix compositions using combined shear-rheology and microindentation. Specifically compares pEtN-modified cellulose vs. curli amyloid fibers.

## Mechanical Parameters Measured
- Storage modulus (G'): 16–51 kPa depending on ECM composition
- Loss modulus (G")
- Reduced elastic modulus (E_r) via microindentation (50 μm diamond indenter)
- Yield stress and flow stress
- Plasticity during holding time
- Adhesion strength

## Key Absence
- No reference to biphasic theory, Mow 1980, cartilage biomechanics
- No measurement of hydraulic permeability, aggregate modulus, or fixed charge density
- No Donnan equilibrium framework
- Does NOT compare with Pseudomonas (Pel/Psl/alginate) — focuses on E. coli curli/cellulose

## Why Selected
State-of-the-art 2025 biofilm mechanical characterization. The parameters measured (G', G") are viscoelastic moduli, NOT the biphasic parameters (H_a, k, FCD) that characterize cartilage. Confirms the gap: biofilm mechanics uses viscoelastic framework while cartilage uses poroelastic-triphasic framework, despite shared underlying physics.
