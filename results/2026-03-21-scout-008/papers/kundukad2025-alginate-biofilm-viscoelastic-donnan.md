# Paper: Alginate exopolymer significantly modulates the viscoelastic properties and resilience of bacterial biofilms

## Citation
- **Authors:** Kundukad B, Rice SA, Doyle PS, Kjelleberg S
- **Journal:** NPJ Biofilms and Microbiomes
- **Year:** 2025
- **Volume/Issue:** 11, 98
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41522-025-00718-6
- **PMC:** PMC12149293
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41522-025-00718-6

## Abstract / Key Findings
This study demonstrates that alginate overproduction in P. aeruginosa (ΔmucA mutant) produces biofilms with counterintuitive mechanical behavior: swelling to 130% of original size while simultaneously stiffening (elastic modulus increases from 19 ± 14 Pa to 138 ± 75 Pa). Wild-type biofilms (Psl-dominant, neutral polysaccharide) show minimal swelling and decreased elasticity post-treatment.

## Mechanical Parameters Measured
- Elastic storage modulus (G') at 10 s⁻¹: WT = 57 ± 45 Pa; mucoid = 19-138 Pa depending on treatment
- Loss modulus (G") via particle-tracking microrheology
- Swelling ratio: mucoid biofilm swells to 130% of original projected area

## Key Mechanistic Framework
**Donnan equilibrium is explicitly invoked** to explain swelling-induced stiffening. The paper states: "alginate's polyelectrolyte nature drives swelling through the Donnan effect, enhancing matrix stability." This is analogous to the triphasic cartilage framework where fixed charge density drives osmotic swelling pressure.
