## Abstract
Radiotherapy induces cuproptosis in cancer cells independent of apoptosis/ferroptosis. Mechanism: radiotherapy upregulates CTR1 and depletes mitochondrial glutathione → elevated mitochondrial Cu → cuproptosis. Radioresistance occurs via BACH1-metallothionein (MT1E/X) axis that sequesters Cu.

## Key Findings

### New Cuproptosis Mechanism (2025)
- Radiotherapy elevates mitochondrial Cu via CTR1 upregulation
- Radiotherapy depletes mitochondrial glutathione (GSH) — a Cu chelator
- Net effect: more free Cu in mitochondria → cuproptosis
- This is FDX1-dependent (confirmed by FDX1 knockout rescue)

### Resistance Mechanism
- BACH1 (transcription factor) downregulation → MT1E/X upregulation
- Metallothioneins (MT) sequester Cu (analogous to covellite sequestering Cu in hydrothermal systems)
- MT-bound Cu is not available for FDX1-mediated reduction and DLAT binding
- The Cu speciation state (free vs MT-bound) determines life/death outcome

### Therapeutic Synergy
- Elesclomol + radiotherapy: near-complete tumor regression in PDX models
- Elesclomol overcomes MT-mediated Cu sequestration
- FDX1-dependent confirmed by rescue experiments

### Speciation Analogy (not made in paper, identified here)
- MT sequesters Cu+ via thiolate ligation (20 Cys residues per MT molecule)
- Covellite (CuS) sequesters Cu in hydrothermal vent systems via sulfide coordination
- Both create "inactive" Cu pools unavailable for toxicity
- The thermodynamic stability constants for MT-Cu and CuS complexes are comparable (both log K > 15)
- This parallel has NOT been explored in the cuproptosis literature
