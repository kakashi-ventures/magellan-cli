# FDX1-dependent and independent mechanisms of elesclomol-mediated intracellular copper delivery

**Authors**: Not specified in abstract retrieval
**Journal**: PNAS (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences)
**Year**: 2023
**DOI**: 10.1073/pnas.2216722120
**URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2216722120
**Status**: Peer-reviewed

## Abstract (reconstructed from search results)

Elesclomol (ES) is a copper ionophore that delivers Cu²⁺ to the mitochondrial matrix, triggering cuproptosis. This paper characterizes both FDX1-dependent and FDX1-independent mechanisms by which ES delivers copper to cells. The study demonstrates that ES-Cu²⁺ (elesclomol-copper complex) has much higher electron affinity for FDX1 than free Cu²⁺, and that FDX1 preferentially binds ES-Cu²⁺ over free Cu²⁺ or over the homologous FDX2. ES binds Cu²⁺ extracellularly with extremely high affinity (Ka = 10^17.1 at physiological pH).

## Key Findings

1. **ES-Cu²⁺ affinity for FDX1**: The elesclomol-Cu²⁺ complex is a far better electron acceptor from FDX1 than free Cu²⁺ ions. FDX1 preferentially reduces ES-Cu²⁺ over free copper.

2. **FDX1 vs FDX2 specificity**: FDX1 has much higher binding affinity for ES-Cu²⁺ than FDX2. This selectivity is structurally encoded (consistent with D136/D139 findings from the 2025 DMS paper).

3. **ES binds Cu²⁺ extracellularly**: Ka = 10^17.1 — extremely tight. ES scavenges ambient Cu²⁺ at physiological concentrations and delivers it to mitochondria.

4. **FDX1-independent pathway exists**: Some copper delivery to mitochondria by ES occurs even in FDX1-null cells, suggesting an FDX1-independent route (possibly via CTR1 after partial reduction, or direct ES-Cu delivery to respiratory chain components).

5. **Speciation matters**: The redox state of Cu (²⁺ vs ⁺) determines which pathway is activated and the efficiency of cuproptosis induction.
