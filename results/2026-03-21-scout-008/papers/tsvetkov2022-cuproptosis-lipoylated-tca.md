# Copper induces cell death by targeting lipoylated TCA cycle proteins

**Authors**: Tsvetkov, P., Coy, S., Petrova, B., Dreyfuss, M., et al.
**Journal**: Science
**Year**: 2022
**DOI**: 10.1126/science.abf0529
**URL**: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abf0529
**Status**: Peer-reviewed

## Abstract (reconstructed from multiple sources)

This paper defines cuproptosis as a distinct form of copper-induced regulated cell death. Copper ionophores (elesclomol, disulfiram) selectively kill cells dependent on mitochondrial respiration but not glycolysis. The mechanism involves direct binding of copper to lipoylated components of the TCA cycle — primarily dihydrolipoamide S-acetyltransferase (DLAT), the E2 subunit of pyruvate dehydrogenase — causing DLAT oligomerization and aggregation. This triggers loss of Fe-S cluster proteins and proteotoxic stress leading to cell death. FDX1 (ferredoxin 1) was identified as the upstream regulator: it reduces Cu²⁺ to the more reactive Cu⁺ form, and it also promotes protein lipoylation. Deletion of FDX1 or lipoylation enzymes (LIAS, LIPT1, DLD) confers resistance to copper-induced death.

## Key Findings

1. **Cuproptosis requires mitochondrial respiration**: Cells switched to glycolysis (no OXPHOS) are resistant. This links copper toxicity strictly to mitochondrial metabolism.

2. **FDX1 is the master regulator**: FDX1 is both the Cu²⁺→Cu⁺ reductase AND the upstream regulator of protein lipoylation. Loss of FDX1 blocks both functions and confers resistance.

3. **DLAT is the primary Cu target**: Copper binds directly to the lipoyl moiety of DLAT. KD for Cu binding to free lipoic acid: ~10⁻¹⁷ (extraordinarily tight). Lipoylation of DLAT is necessary; delipoylated DLAT does not oligomerize in response to copper.

4. **Fe-S cluster proteins are secondary victims**: After DLAT aggregation, Fe-S cluster proteins (including FDX1 itself) are destabilized and lost. This creates a self-amplifying loop.

5. **Genetic screen identified the pathway**: Genome-wide CRISPR screen identified FDX1, LIAS, LIPT1, LIPT2, DLD, DLAT, PDHA1, PDHB as core resistance genes — all in the lipoylation pathway.

## Relevance to Cross-Field Connection

- FDX1 contains a [2Fe-2S] iron-sulfur cluster — structurally ancestral to hydrothermal vent FeS minerals (greigite, pyrite)
- The lipoyl dithiolane ring of DLAT (the primary Cu binding site) is chemically analogous to the HS⁻/S²⁻ ligands that coordinate Cu⁺ in hydrothermal sulfide minerals
- The KD ~10⁻¹⁷ for Cu-lipoate binding is anomalously tight — consistent with evolutionary pressure in high-Cu environments (hydrothermal vents)
- The strict mitochondrial respiration requirement connects to the ancient origin of mitochondria as bacterial endosymbionts that may have evolved in Fe-S/Cu-rich environments
