# Paper: Material Properties of Biofilms — Key Methods for Understanding Permeability and Mechanics

## Citation
- **Authors:** Wilking JN, Angelini TE, Seminara A, Brenner MP, Weitz DA
- **Journal:** MRS Bulletin
- **Year:** 2011 (widely cited review)
- **PMC:** PMC4504244
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4504244/

## Abstract / Key Findings
Review of experimental methods for measuring biofilm permeability and mechanics. Covers:
- Microsensors for concentration gradients
- FRAP, FCS fluorescence methods for diffusion
- NMR for non-invasive measurements
- Bulk rheology (parallel plate, cone-plate)
- Passive microrheology (particle tracking)
- Active microrheology (AFM, optical tweezers, magnetic)

## Critical Gap Identified
Paper explicitly notes biofilms "require study in native contexts" and lack "a general theoretical framework" connecting permeability and mechanics to physiological function.

## What Is NOT Mentioned
- Biphasic theory (Mow 1980)
- Cartilage biomechanics as a model system
- Aggregate modulus concept
- Hydraulic permeability in the biphasic/Darcy sense (only diffusion/advection permeability measured)
- Donnan equilibrium or fixed charge density
- Osmotic swelling pressure

## Measurable Parameters in 2011
Diffusion coefficients, storage/loss moduli (G', G"), creep compliance, adhesive strength, yield stress — NOT aggregate modulus H_a or hydraulic permeability k in the confined-compression sense.

## Why Selected
Demonstrates that even the 2011 state-of-the-art review of biofilm mechanics does not reference the cartilage biomechanics literature. The fields were already parallel but not cross-citing — a gap that has persisted to 2025.
