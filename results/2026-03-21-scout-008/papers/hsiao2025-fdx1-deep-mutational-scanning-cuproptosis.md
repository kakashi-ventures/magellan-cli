## Abstract
Deep mutational scanning of all FDX1 residues to identify structural determinants of cuproptosis and lipoylation activity. Identifies critical electrostatic residues mediating protein-protein interactions required for Cu2+→Cu+ reduction.

## Key Findings

### Critical Residues
- D136 and D139 on FDX1's third alpha helix (α-helix 3) are essential
- These are solvent-exposed, charged residues
- Charge-reversal mutations (D→K or D→R) abolish cuproptosis AND lipoylation
- Critically: these mutants retain full FDX1 enzymatic activity in vitro
- Conclusion: D136/D139 mediate protein-protein interactions, not catalytic function

### FDX1 Dual Role
1. Reduces Cu(II) → Cu(I) (ionophore substrate reduction)
2. Promotes LIAS activity → lipoylation of DLAT, DLST, etc.
Both functions require D136/D139 surface residues

### New Mechanistic Finding
- DLD (dihydrolipoamide dehydrogenase) identified as alternative upstream reductase of FDX1
- DLD can functionally replace FDXR (ferredoxin reductase) in cellular contexts
- FDX1 operates in a multi-protein complex (FDX1-LIAS-DLD-DLAT)

### Implications for Copper Speciation
- The Cu(II)→Cu(I) reduction occurs within a protein complex context
- Reduction potential must be compatible with FDX1's [2Fe-2S] cluster E° (~-270 mV vs NHE)
- This is within the hydrothermal vent mixed-fluid redox range
- First structural map of the cuproptosis execution complex

## Relevance to Session 008
Most recent FDX1 structural data (2025). The FDX1 [2Fe-2S] cluster reduction potential (-270 mV) places the Cu2+→Cu+ reduction in a specific Eh window that can be mapped onto a Pourbaix diagram. This is the first paper providing quantitative electrochemical context for the FDX1-mediated step, though it doesn't explicitly connect to geochemical frameworks.
