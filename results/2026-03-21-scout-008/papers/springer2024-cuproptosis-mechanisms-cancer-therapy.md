## Abstract
Comprehensive review of cuproptosis mechanisms and cancer therapy implications. Covers copper ionophores (elesclomol, disulfiram), FDX1/DLAT pathway, Fe-S cluster disruption, and therapeutic development.

## Key Findings

### Mechanism Overview
1. Cu2+ enters cell via CTR1 (reduced to Cu+ at membrane)
2. Cu+ enters mitochondria via SLC25A3/CuL pool
3. FDX1 reduces Cu2+ (from ionophore-bound form) → Cu+
4. Cu+ binds lipoylated DLAT → oligomerization
5. Simultaneous: Cu+ inhibits Fe-S cluster synthesis
6. Result: proteotoxic stress + metabolic disruption → cell death

### Open Questions (Explicitly Stated)
- "The exact mechanism of these genetic encoders and cuproptosis is still being explored"
- "The regulatory mechanisms underlying cuproptosis must be determined"
- Missing: specific biomarkers for clinical application
- Missing: mechanistic links between copper toxicity and protein lipoylation at atomic level

### Unexplored Areas
- Copper speciation beyond simple Cu2+/Cu+ distinction — no discussion of:
  - Copper-thiolate complexes within mitochondria
  - pH effects on copper species
  - Role of mitochondrial Eh in determining Cu speciation
  - No geochemical analogy or thermodynamic modeling
- The paper addresses only the Cu2+/Cu+ redox couple, no higher oxidation states

### Cancer Context
- FDX1 expression correlates with tumor metabolic state
- Elesclomol clinical trials for AML, myelodysplastic syndromes
- Combination with radiotherapy (2025 Cancer Cell): additive cuproptosis

## Relevance to Session 008
Explicitly identifies "exact mechanism" and "speciation beyond Cu2+/Cu+" as gaps. The absence of thermodynamic/speciation modeling is noted implicitly by the review's focus on binary Cu2+ vs Cu+ description. This is precisely where geochemical speciation frameworks (Pourbaix, Eh-pH, ligand stability constants) would provide quantitative depth.
