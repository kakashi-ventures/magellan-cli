## Abstract
The authors identify a novel form of copper-induced cell death termed "cuproptosis" that is mechanistically distinct from apoptosis, ferroptosis, necroptosis, and pyroptosis. Using a genome-wide CRISPR screen, FDX1 was identified as the top hit required for copper ionophore (elesclomol)-induced toxicity.

## Key Findings

### Core Mechanism
- Copper directly binds lipoylated TCA cycle proteins (DLAT, DLST, GCSH, DBT)
- FDX1 (ferredoxin 1) is an upstream regulator of protein lipoylation
- FDX1 reduces Cu2+ to Cu+ — this reduction is required for cuproptosis
- Cu+ binds the lipoyl moiety on DLAT, inducing disulfide-dependent oligomerization
- DLAT oligomerization causes proteotoxic stress → cell death

### Quantitative Data
- Cells dependent on mitochondrial respiration are ~1,000-fold more sensitive to copper ionophores vs glycolysis-dependent cells
- FDX1 knockout: complete loss of protein lipoylation, full resistance to copper ionophores
- LIAS or FDX1 deletion blocks lipoylation → blocks cuproptosis

### Molecular Details
- Lipoylated proteins: DLAT (PDH complex E2), DLST (alpha-KGDH complex E2), DBT (BCKDH E2), GCSH (glycine cleavage H protein)
- Fe-S cluster synthesis is inhibited by excess Cu+ (secondary effect via FDX1)
- HSP70 upregulation is a downstream marker of proteotoxic stress

### Copper Speciation Detail
- Elesclomol binds Cu2+ extracellularly → delivers Cu2+ to mitochondria
- FDX1 reduces Cu2+ → Cu+ inside mitochondria (redox step)
- Cu+ (not Cu2+) is the species that binds lipoyl moieties
- This Cu2+/Cu+ redox transition is thermodynamically critical but unstudied using geochemical frameworks

## Relevance to Session 008
CRITICAL PAPER. Establishes the core cuproptosis mechanism. The Cu2+→Cu+ reduction step is analogous to Cu2+→Cu+ transitions in hydrothermal vent fluid mixing zones (oxidizing seawater + reducing vent fluid). The lipoyl dithiolane group (DLAT) is structurally and chemically analogous to inorganic sulfide ligands that complex Cu+ in vent systems.
