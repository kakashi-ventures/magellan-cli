## Field A: Cuproptosis Mechanism (10 papers)

### 1.1 Tsvetkov et al. 2022 — THE FOUNDATIONAL PAPER
**Title**: Copper induces cell death by targeting lipoylated TCA cycle proteins
**Journal**: Science | **DOI**: 10.1126/science.abf0529 | **PMID**: 35298263
**Key findings**:
- Defined cuproptosis as distinct copper-dependent cell death
- FDX1 reduces Cu²⁺ → Cu⁺; required for lipoylation via LIAS pathway
- Cu⁺ binds lipoylated proteins (DLAT, DLST, GCSH, DBT) via dithiolane ring
- **Cu binding to free lipoic acid: KD = 10⁻¹⁷ M** (extraordinarily tight)
- DLAT oligomerization → insoluble aggregates → proteotoxic stress → cell death
- Fe-S cluster proteins lost coincident with cuproptosis

### 1.2 Hsiao et al. 2025 — FDX1 Structural Determinants
**Title**: Deep Mutational Scanning of FDX1 Identifies Key Structural Determinants of Lipoylation and Cuproptosis
**Journal**: Nature Communications | **DOI**: 10.1038/s41467-025-67869-0
**Key finding**: Deep mutational scan separates FDX1's Fe-S cluster functions from its cuproptosis functions.

### 1.3 Guthrie et al. 2023 — Elesclomol FDX1-Dependent and Independent Mechanisms
**Title**: FDX1-dependent and independent mechanisms of elesclomol-mediated intracellular copper delivery
**Journal**: PNAS | **DOI**: 10.1073/pnas.2216722120 | **PMID**: 36848556
**Key findings**: FDX1 in mitochondrial matrix reduces elesclomol-bound Cu²⁺ → Cu⁺. FDX1-independent pathway exists outside mitochondria. ES-Cu²⁺ complex has greater electron acceptance from FDX1 than free Cu²⁺.

### 1.4 Kuang et al. 2025 — EPR Evidence for Direct Electron Transfer
**Title**: EPR Insights into Direct Electron Transfer Between FDX1 and Elesclomol-Cu²⁺ Complex in Cuproptosis
**Journal**: Chemistry – A European Journal | **DOI**: 10.1002/chem.202501145
**Key finding**: Low-temperature EPR validates direct ET from reduced FDX1 to elesclomol-Cu²⁺. FDX1 has higher binding affinity for elesclomol-Cu²⁺ than FDX2.

### 1.5 Hendricks et al. 2021 — Lipoyl Synthase (LIAS) Reconstitution
**Title**: Characterization and Reconstitution of Human Lipoyl Synthase (LIAS)
**Journal**: Int. J. Mol. Sci. | **DOI**: 10.3390/ijms22041598
**Key finding**: LIAS is an Fe-S radical SAM enzyme using two [4Fe-4S] clusters to synthesize lipoic acid — the moiety copper binds during cuproptosis.

### 1.6 Lu et al. 2026 — Mitochondrial Uncoupling and Cuproptosis
**Title**: Mitochondrial uncoupling sensitizes gastric cancer cells to elesclomol-induced cuproptosis
**Journal**: Free Radical Biology and Medicine | **PMID**: 41407052
**Key finding**: Mitochondrial membrane potential (Δψm) sensitizes to cuproptosis; respiration is prerequisite.

### 1.7 Li et al. 2023 — DLAT in Cancer
**Title**: MELK promotes HCC carcinogenesis through modulating cuproptosis-related gene DLAT
**Journal**: Cell Death & Disease | **PMID**: 37949877
**Key finding**: DLAT is E2 subunit of pyruvate dehydrogenase; dysregulation modulates mitochondrial function and cuproptosis sensitivity.

### 1.8 Liu et al. 2026 — FDX1/LIAS in Diabetic Kidney Disease
**Title**: Integrated Multi-omics Reveal FDX1/LIAS-Mediated Cuproptosis in Diabetic Kidney Disease
**Journal**: Biological Trace Element Research | **PMID**: 41100003
**Key finding**: FDX1 and LIAS are co-expressed and co-regulated in vivo, confirming the FDX1→LIAS→lipoylation axis.

### 1.9 Wang et al. 2025 — Elesclomol Review
**Title**: An up-To-Date Review of Elesclomol and Its Nano-Formulations in Cancer Therapy
**Journal**: Cancer Reports | **PMID**: 40195280
**Key finding**: Elesclomol coordinates Cu²⁺ via thiohydrazide groups; FDX1-mediated reduction is central mechanism.

### 1.10 Saito group 2020 — H₂S + Copper Neurotoxicity
**PMID**: 32315022
**Key finding**: H₂S donor (NaHS) + CuSO₄ dramatically increases Cu cytotoxicity. CuS nanoparticles re-release Cu when oxidized by H₂O₂. **Directly relevant to sulfide-copper bridge hypothesis.**

## Field C: Hydrothermal Vent Copper Geochemistry (8 papers)

### 2.1 Beverskog & Puigdomenech 1997 — Foundational Pourbaix Reference
**Title**: Revised Pourbaix Diagrams for Copper at 25 to 300°C
**Journal**: J. Electrochemical Society | **DOI**: 10.1149/1.1838036
**Key findings**:
- Reference Pourbaix diagrams across 25–300°C
- **Cu²⁺/Cu⁺ boundary at pH 7: Eh ≈ +0.15 V vs SHE**
- Full hydrolysis series of Cu(I) and Cu(II)
- Dissolved species predominance at 10⁻³ to 10⁻⁹ mol/kg

### 2.2 Luther et al. 2001 — Chemical Speciation Drives Vent Ecology
**Title**: Chemical speciation drives hydrothermal vent ecology
**Journal**: Nature 410:813–816 | **PMID**: 11298448
**Key finding**: In situ electrochemical measurements show Eh-pH speciation is the primary driver of vent community structure.

### 2.3 Samin et al. 2024 — Copper Isotopes in Chalcopyrite Chimneys
**Title**: Preservation of Hydrothermal Fluid Cu Isotope Signatures in Chalcopyrite-Rich Chimneys
**Journal**: G-Cubed | **DOI**: 10.1029/2023GC011349
**Key finding**: δ⁶⁵Cu increases across chimney wall as Cu⁺ (chalcopyrite interior) → Cu²⁺ (covellite exterior). Demonstrates Cu oxidation state transition across redox gradient.

### 2.4 Sander & Koschinsky 2011 — Organic Complexation of Vent Copper
**Title**: Metal flux from hydrothermal vents increased by organic complexation
**Journal**: Nature Geoscience 4:145–150
**Key findings**: Organic Cu-binding ligands up to 4000 nM in vent areas. **Conditional stability constants: log K'Cu'L = 12.48–13.46.** Thiols including glutathione form Cu(I) complexes at pH 7.8.

### 2.5 Luther group 2006 — Organic Complexation Deep-Sea
**Title**: Organic complexation of copper in deep-sea hydrothermal vent systems
**Journal**: Environmental Chemistry (CSIRO) | **DOI**: 10.1071/EN06086
**Key finding**: Cu-thiol conditional stability constants 12.3–14.1. Thiols competitive with sulfide at high [S²⁻].

### 2.6 Stüeken et al. 2020 — Vent Copper and Organic Ligands
**Title**: Hydrothermal vents and organic ligands sustained the Precambrian copper budget
**Journal**: Geochemical Perspectives Letters
**Key finding**: Cu largely organically bound throughout Proterozoic. Thiol/organic ligand speciation is geologically ancient.

### 2.7 2024 — Cu-Resistant Bacteria from Hydrothermal Fields
**Journal**: Frontiers in Microbiology | **DOI**: 10.3389/fmicb.2024.1390451
**Key findings**: Bacteria resistant to 6–10 mM Cu from vents. Cu in hydrothermal sulfides: 9.6–228 g/kg.

### 2.8 Cambra group 2012 — Stable Cu(II)/Cu(I) Intermediates in Thermus thermophilus
**Journal**: JACS | **DOI**: 10.1021/ja307276z | **PMID**: 22946616
**Key finding**: PCuAC copper chaperone delivers Cu(I) to CuA site; Cu²⁺/Cu⁺ interconversion mechanistically central in thermophilic copper homeostasis — parallel to FDX1 in cuproptosis.

## Bridge / Gap Papers (5 key findings)

### Gap 3.1: Intracellular Copper Redox State — THE KEY GAP
**Source**: PMC: 8421569 + multiple reviews
- Intracellular copper exists in "an immense variety of static forms involving multiple oxidation states" — but **no quantitative Eh/pH analysis has ever been published**
- Mitochondrial copper: 30–50 ng Cu/mg mitochondrial protein
- The copper ligand (CuL) in mitochondrial matrix has **unknown molecular identity**
- Cu²⁺/Cu⁺ ratio under cuproptotic conditions: **unmeasured**

### Gap 3.2: Mitochondrial Redox Potential Values
- Mitochondrial matrix Eh (from NADH/NAD⁺): **−290 to −320 mV vs SHE**
- Mitochondrial matrix pH: **7.8–8.0**
- At these values, Beverskog-Puigdomenech diagram predicts **Cu⁺ as thermodynamically stable species**
- Cu²⁺/Cu⁺ boundary at pH 7.8 is approximately −50 to +50 mV — well above matrix Eh of −300 mV

### Gap 3.3: H₂S and Copper — Parallel Geochemical and Cellular Chemistry
- H₂S directly binds Cu²⁺ at cytochrome c oxidase CuB site
- CuS nanoparticles re-release Cu upon H₂O₂ oxidation
- Vent H₂S creates CuS species; biological H₂S also modulates Cu speciation

### Gap 3.4: Dithiolane Ring — Shared Ligand Chemistry
- Lipoic acid's 1,2-dithiolane ring participates in Cu complexation
- Identical ligand chemistry to thiol complexes in vent organic ligand pools
- α-lipoic acid investigated as Wilson's disease copper chelator

### Gap 3.5: Vent Chimney ↔ Mitochondrial Membrane Gradient Parallel
- Vent chimney Eh: −500 mV (vent side) to +500 mV (seawater side)
- Mitochondrial membrane: ΔpH ~1 unit, ΔΨ ~−180 mV
- Steep Eh/pH gradients across thin barriers in both systems

## Critical Thermodynamic Insight for Generator

At mitochondrial matrix conditions (Eh = −300 mV, pH 7.8), the Beverskog-Puigdomenech Pourbaix diagram predicts **Cu⁺ as the thermodynamically stable species**. This means:

1. **Elesclomol-delivered Cu²⁺ MUST be reduced to Cu⁺** — this is thermodynamically driven, not merely kinetically facilitated by FDX1
2. **Lipoyl moiety acts as a Cu⁺ trap** — dithiolane rings bind Cu⁺ with KD ~10⁻¹⁷ M
3. **Vent chimney parallel**: chalcopyrite (CuFeS₂, Cu⁺) is stable in reduced vent fluids; covellite (CuS, Cu²⁺) forms at oxidizing seawater interface — exactly mirroring Cu⁺→Cu²⁺ transition across mitochondrial redox gradient

**This thermodynamic isomorphism between vent chimney redox gradients and mitochondrial redox gradients is the core unrecognized parallel. No published paper has made this connection.**
