## Target 2: Mitochondrial Hormesis Threshold Switching × Cellular Aging Hallmarks
Field A: Mitochondrial hormesis mechanisms (beneficial low-dose mitochondrial stress, ROS signaling, adaptive responses)
Field C: Mitochondrial dysfunction as aging hallmark (age-related decline in mitochondrial function, oxidative damage theory)

Why these should connect: Aging research identifies mitochondrial dysfunction as a fundamental hallmark of aging, emphasizing progressive decline and oxidative damage. Simultaneously, hormesis research demonstrates that low-dose mitochondrial stress extends lifespan through adaptive responses. This creates a fundamental paradox: the same mitochondrial stress that causes aging can also prevent it, suggesting critical dose-response thresholds that determine whether mitochondrial stress is beneficial or harmful.
