## Context

These hypotheses were generated by an AI scientific discovery pipeline (Claude Opus). They
explore whether the Thermodynamic Uncertainty Relation (TUR) — a bound from non-equilibrium
statistical physics — sets fundamental precision limits on bacterial cell size homeostasis.

**Key concepts you'll need:**
- **TUR (Thermodynamic Uncertainty Relation)**: For any molecular process dissipating entropy Σ,
  the coefficient of variation (CV = std/mean) of a related counting observable satisfies
  CV² ≥ 2kT/Σ. More dissipation → tighter precision floor.
- **Adder model**: E. coli adds a fixed volume per generation between birth and division,
  regardless of birth size. The precision of this "adding" (CV_added) is ~10%.
- **DnaA**: Master replication initiator protein. 11 DnaA-ATP molecules (N_eff = 11) bind
  cooperatively at oriC to trigger DNA replication. Each binding event consumes ~20 kBT.
- **FtsZ**: GTPase that forms the Z-ring for cell division. Hydrolyzes GTP ~8/min while
  treadmilling; ~300 molecules active simultaneously.
- **RIDA**: Regulatory Inactivation of DnaA — the Hda protein (loaded via β-clamp at oriC)
  converts DnaA-ATP to DnaA-ADP post-initiation, resetting the counter.
- **MinCDE system**: Oscillating proteins that establish the mid-cell division site.
- **ppGpp**: Stringent response alarmone that globally remodels metabolism under stress.
- **Berg-Purcell Limit (BPL)**: Diffusive sensing limit — CV² ≥ 1/(c·D·a·T) for ligand
  concentration sensing. Separate from and usually more lenient than TUR.
