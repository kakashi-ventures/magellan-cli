# Thermodynamic Uncertainty Relation in Biochemical Oscillations

**Title:** The thermodynamic uncertainty relation in biochemical oscillations
**Authors:** Robert Marsland III, Wenping Cui, Jordan M. Horowitz
**Year:** 2019
**Journal:** Journal of the Royal Society Interface, 16(154)
**DOI:** 10.1098/rsif.2019.0098
**PMC:** PMC6544898

## Abstract

Living systems regulate behavior through periodic oscillations of molecular concentrations, whose precision depends on suppressing stochastic fluctuations. The thermodynamic uncertainty relation (TUR) bounds the precision achievable at a given thermodynamic cost. The authors examine how well real biochemical oscillators approach this bound.

## Key Findings

- Real biochemical clocks (activator-inhibitor, repressilator, Brusselator, glycolysis, KaiC) perform **several orders of magnitude worse** than the theoretical TUR optimum
- The performance gap arises because biochemical oscillators have limited internal states per molecule (N_eff ≈ 2) combined with high thermodynamic force requirements
- A key design principle: systems approach the bound by increasing the number of internal states per molecule while maintaining uniform reaction rates
- TUR bound: CV² × σ̇ × τ ≥ 2kT, where CV = coefficient of variation of cycle period, σ̇ = entropy production rate, τ = mean cycle period
