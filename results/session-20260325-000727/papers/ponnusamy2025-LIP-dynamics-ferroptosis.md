# Labile Iron Pool Dynamics and Ferroptosis

**Title:** Labile iron pool dynamics do not drive ferroptosis potentiation in colorectal cancer cells
**Authors:** Ponnusamy V, Randall DR, Lee ZH, Das NK, Zhao L, Buscher K, Solanki S, Renslo AR, Hsu PP, Shah YM
**Year:** 2025
**Source:** bioRxiv preprint (July 2025); published PMC12236665
**DOI:** 10.1101/2025.07.01.662602

## Abstract

Using a reactivity-based iron probe (TRX-PURO), the authors show that the labile iron pool (LIP) does NOT measurably increase during pharmacological ferroptosis induction (GPX4 inhibition, SLC7A11 inhibition) in colorectal cancer cells, despite exogenous iron supplementation enhancing ferroptotic death.

## Key Findings

- **LIP does NOT expand** during ferroptosis induction — challenges the dominant model
- Iron homeostasis genes (DMT1, TFRC, FTH1, FPN) unchanged at 16 h post-treatment
- Exogenous iron (ferric ammonium citrate) DOES sensitize cells synergistically
- Iron remains functionally essential for ferroptosis, but endogenous LIP mobilization may not be the primary driver
- NCOA4-mediated ferritinophagy may not be the sole source of redox-active iron during ferroptosis
