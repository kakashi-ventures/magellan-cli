# Mesoscale Phase Separation of Chromatin in the Nucleus

**Title:** Mesoscale phase separation of chromatin in the nucleus
**Authors:** Gaurav Bajpai, Daria Amiad Pavlov, Dana Lorber, Talila Volk, Samuel Safran
**Year:** 2021
**Journal:** eLife
**DOI:** 10.7554/eLife.63976
**PMID:** Related PMC article confirmed

## Abstract

Live imaging of Drosophila larvae shows chromatin undergoes phase separation from the aqueous nuclear environment. Different organizational modes (peripheral, central, wetting droplet configurations) are governed by nuclear volume and chromatin-lamina interactions.

## Key Findings

- Chromatin undergoes **liquid-liquid phase separation** driven by self-attraction and chromatin-lamina binding competition
- Four distinct organizational modes: peripheral localization, conventional filling, central organization, wetting droplet
- Transitions between modes depend on nuclear volume changes
- **Polymer model** (not particle/granular physics) is used
