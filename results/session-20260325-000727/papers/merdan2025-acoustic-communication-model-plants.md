# An Acoustic Communication Model in Plants

**Title:** An Acoustic Communication Model in Plants
**Authors:** Fatih Merdan, Ozgur B. Akan
**Year:** 2025 (submitted December 2024)
**Source:** arXiv 2512.01096

## Abstract

Develops "the first end-to-end acoustic communication framework for plants," examining how plants process incoming sound waves. Simulations show a 200 Hz, 20 μPa stimulus raises cytosolic Ca²⁺ levels from 150 nM to 230±10 nM within 50 seconds, potentially triggering root bending. The authors introduce the term "quantitative phytoacoustics."

## Key Findings

- End-to-end mathematical model from acoustic stimulus to Ca²⁺ response
- Tracks biological signal processing steps: acoustic wave → membrane deformation → mechanosensitive channel activation → Ca²⁺ influx → downstream signaling
- Applications in precision agriculture and plant signaling research
- **Framework:** Biological signal processing via physiological models
