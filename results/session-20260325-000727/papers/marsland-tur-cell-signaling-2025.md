# TUR Constraints on Cell Signaling Information Transmission

**Title:** Thermodynamic uncertainty relation constraints information transmission through cell signaling systems
**Authors:** Shreyansh Verma, Vishva Saravanan R, Bhaswar Ghosh
**Year:** 2025 (preprint, bioRxiv)
**DOI:** 10.1101/2025.01.04.631284

## Abstract

Explores how TUR constrains information transmission in cellular signaling. Analyzes transcription factor (TF) nuclear localization in yeast under carbon, osmotic, and oxidative stress using experimental data, mathematical modeling, and machine learning.

## Key Findings

- Higher response amplitudes reduce fluctuations, improving signal-to-noise ratio and mutual information
- Entropy production positively correlates with mutual information: "entropy production enables reduction in intrinsic noise"
- TUR predictions confirmed: current fluctuations decrease as entropy production increases
- Mutual information bounded by log(1 + Σ) where Σ is entropy production

## What This Paper Does NOT Do

- Does NOT apply TUR to cell size homeostasis, division timing, or the adder model
- Does NOT address bacterial growth or DnaA-ATP molecular counting
- Focuses on eukaryotic (yeast) transcription factor dynamics — completely different biological context
- Information transmission framing vs. precision of a size-adding mechanism
