# Plant Ultrasound Detection: A Cost-Effective Methodology

**Title:** Plant ultrasound detection: a cost-effective method for identifying plant ultrasonic emissions
**Authors:** Luca Bonisoli, Ivano Pelicella, Laura Arru
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** Plant Signaling & Behavior
**DOI:** 10.1080/15592324.2024.2310974
**PMC:** PMC10863351

## Abstract

Presents a standardized, low-cost methodology for recording and analyzing plant ultrasonic emissions (UEs) using a single ultrasound microphone and open-source software (Audacity).

## Key Findings

- **Frequency ranges of plant UEs:** 20–100 kHz; emissions occur in four separable bands: 20–40 kHz, 40–60 kHz, 60–80 kHz, 80–100 kHz
- **Source of emissions:** Xylem cavitation — air seeding creates microscopic bubbles that vibrate adjacent tissue
- **Detection method:** External ultrasonic microphone (Dodotronic Ultramic 384K, sampling rate 384 kHz) at 10 cm distance
- Plant recordings yield significantly more detectable UEs than soil-only or empty room controls
- Stress conditions (desiccation, herbivory, physical damage, pathogen) enhance emission rate

## Limitations / What This Paper Does NOT Address

- **Detection from outside the plant, not the plant's own detection mechanism.** The study addresses how researchers detect plant emissions, not how plants detect sound from their environment.
- No filter-bank or frequency-selective signal processing applied to the plant side
- No MSL channel or mechanosensitive receptor involvement discussed in terms of acoustic processing
