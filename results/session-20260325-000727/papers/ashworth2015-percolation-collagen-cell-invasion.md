# Cell Invasion in Collagen Scaffold Architectures: Percolation Theory

**Title:** Cell Invasion in Collagen Scaffold Architectures Characterized by Percolation Theory
**Authors:** Jennifer C. Ashworth, Marco Mehr, Paul G. Buxton, Serena M. Best, Ruth E. Cameron
**Year:** 2015
**Journal:** Advanced Healthcare Materials, 4(9), 1317–1321
**DOI:** 10.1002/adhm.201500197
**PMID:** 25881025

## Abstract

X-ray microcomputed tomography combined with percolation theory characterizes collagen scaffold interconnectivity. Confocal microscopy validates the approach as highly relevant for determining cell invasion extent.

## Key Findings

- **Percolation diameters above 40 μm** required to ensure scaffold accessibility to connective tissue cells
- Scale-independent technique for predicting invasion based on pore interconnectivity
- Percolation threshold p_c defines the critical pore occupancy for cellular access
- Maximum accessible distance as a function of cell probe diameter mirrors percolation scaling

## What the Paper Does NOT Do

- Studies **generic connective tissue cells** in engineered scaffolds, NOT T cells in tumor ECM
- Tissue engineering context (scaffold design), NOT tumor immunology
- **No bond percolation model** for collagen fiber crosslinking density
- No LOX crosslinking as bond probability
- No correlation length exponents (ξ ~ |p - p_c|^{-ν})
- No immune cell exclusion or T cell-specific analysis
- No connection to extracellular matrix remodeling by tumors

## Relevance to T1 (Percolation × T cell infiltration)

This is the **only existing paper** that applies percolation theory to collagen and cell invasion. However:
- It's scaffold architecture (tissue engineering), not tumor ECM
- It's pore percolation (threshold for cells to pass through pores), not bond percolation (LOX crosslinks as bonds between collagen fibers)
- It's generic connective tissue cells, not T cells or immune cells
- No connection to tumor immunology literature

The T1 hypothesis proposes applying **bond percolation** to the collagen crosslinking network in tumor ECM, where:
- LOX-catalyzed crosslinks = bonds
- Bond probability p = LOX activity / crosslink density
- p_c = critical collagen crosslink density for percolation-blocked T cell infiltration
- ξ = correlation length of connected fiber clusters → predicts infiltration geometry

This is entirely novel and not prefigured in Ashworth 2015.
