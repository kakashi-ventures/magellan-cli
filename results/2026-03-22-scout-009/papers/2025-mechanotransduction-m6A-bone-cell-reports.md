# Metabolite-Dependent m6A Methylation Driven by Mechanotransduction-Metabolism-Epitranscriptomics Axis Promotes Bone Development and Regeneration

**Year**: 2025
**Journal**: Cell Reports
**PMID**: 40272981
**DOI**: 10.1016/j.celrep.2025 (from PubMed)
**Source**: PubMed abstract retrieval

## Abstract

Intramembranous ossification, a major bone development process, begins with the condensation of precursor cells through the timely structural adaption of extracellular matrix (ECM) catering to rapid cellular morphological changes. Inspired by this, we design a highly cell-adaptable hydrogel to recapitulate an ECM-dependent mechanotransduction-metabolism-epitranscriptomics axis in mesenchymal stromal cells (MSCs). This hydrogel significantly enhances the E-cadherin-mediated cell-cell interactions of MSCs and promotes glucose uptake and tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle activities. We further show that elevated succinate inhibits fat mass and obesity-associated protein (FTO), a N6-methyladenosine (m6A) demethylase, thereby enhancing methyltransferase-like 3 (METTL3)-driven m6A methylation. Methylated RNA immunoprecipitation sequencing (MeRIP-seq) indicates increased m6A methylation of runt-related transcription 2 (Runx2), a key osteogenic signaling factor, promoting osteogenesis of hydrogel-delivered MSCs and bone regeneration in critical-sized bone defects.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Mechanotransduction → Metabolism → Epitranscriptomics chain**: ECM mechanical cues → E-cadherin cell-cell interactions → glucose uptake ↑ → TCA cycle ↑ → **succinate elevation** → FTO (m6A demethylase) inhibition → METTL3-driven m6A hypermethylation → Runx2 mRNA stabilization → osteogenesis.

2. **Succinate as mechanotransduction-epitranscriptomics linker**: Succinate (a TCA cycle metabolite) inhibits FTO at the active site (competitive with α-KG). This is the same mechanism by which oncometabolites (2-HG, fumarate) inhibit α-KG-dependent dioxygenases.

3. **MeRIP-seq validation**: m6A hypermethylation confirmed transcriptome-wide, with Runx2 as the primary osteogenic target. Shows m6A can decode mechanical signals into fate decisions.

4. **Therapeutic proof-of-concept**: Hydrogel-delivered MSCs with enhanced mechanotransduction showed superior bone regeneration in vivo.

## Critical Cross-Domain Implications

**THIS IS THE KEY MECHANOBIOLOGY × EPITRANSCRIPTOMICS BRIDGE PAPER.**

The mechanotransduction → succinate → FTO inhibition → METTL3/m6A axis has only been demonstrated in **bone/MSCs** (osteogenic context). What happens in immune cells?

- **Gap 1**: Does mechanical stress on monocytes/macrophages (e.g., in stiff fibrotic tissues, or in bone marrow niche) similarly elevate succinate → inhibit FTO → increase m6A on immune memory gene mRNAs?
- **Gap 2**: In trained immunity, β-glucan training reprograms monocyte metabolism toward aerobic glycolysis. Does this shift also produce succinate accumulation → m6A changes that help encode the trained state?
- **Gap 3**: The succinate → FTO inhibition mechanism is the same as 2-HG (IDH1/2 mutant oncometabolite) inhibition of FTO. IDH1/2 mutations occur in AML, glioma. Do these tumors have altered m6A landscapes specifically because of FTO inhibition?
- **Gap 4**: Could the mechanotransduction → m6A axis explain why bone marrow stiffness (which increases with aging, fibrosis, MDS) impairs hematopoietic stem cell (HSC) function?

## Why Selected

First paper establishing the mechano-epitranscriptomic axis via succinate → FTO → m6A methylation. The mechanism (succinate inhibits FTO) is generalizable far beyond bone biology. The immune cell and trained immunity context has never been explored. Disjointness: DISJOINT in immune/trained immunity context.
