## Abstract

The marking of DNA, histones, and RNA is central to gene expression regulation in development and disease. Recent evidence links N6-methyladenosine (m6A), installed on RNA by the METTL3-METTL14 methyltransferase complex, to histone modifications, but the link between m6A and DNA methylation remains scarcely explored. This study shows that METTL3-METTL14 recruits the DNA methyltransferase DNMT1 to chromatin for gene-body methylation. We identify a set of genes whose expression is fine-tuned by both gene-body 5mC, which promotes transcription, and m6A, which destabilizes transcripts. We demonstrate that METTL3-METTL14-dependent 5mC and m6A are both essential for the differentiation of embryonic stem cells into embryoid bodies and that the upregulation of key differentiation genes during early differentiation depends on the dynamic balance between increased 5mC and decreased m6A. Our findings add a surprising dimension to our understanding of how epigenetics and epitranscriptomics combine to regulate gene expression and impact development.

## Key Findings

1. **METTL3-METTL14 recruits DNMT1**: The m6A writer complex (METTL3-METTL14) physically interacts with and recruits DNMT1 (DNA methyltransferase 1) to chromatin, establishing gene-body 5mC DNA methylation. This is the first direct RNA methyltransferase → DNA methyltransferase recruitment mechanism.

2. **Opposing functions on the same genes**: Gene-body 5mC (promoted by METTL3-METTL14-DNMT1) promotes transcription. m6A on the same gene's mRNA destabilizes transcripts. These two modifications create a fine-tuning rheostat: high transcription rate + reduced mRNA stability = tightly controlled protein output.

3. **ESC differentiation logic**: During early ESC differentiation: 5mC ↑ + m6A ↓ = upregulation of key differentiation genes. The balance between these two marks determines cell fate timing.

4. **Cross-layer epigenetic regulation**: Reveals a direct "epitranscriptome-epigenome" crosstalk: RNA-level modification (m6A) instructs chromatin-level modification (5mC). Unprecedented in mammalian development biology.

## Cross-Domain Significance

- **Epitranscriptomics × DNA methylation bridge**: METTL3 writing m6A on mRNA while simultaneously recruiting DNMT1 to write 5mC on the gene body represents a completely novel feedback loop between RNA and DNA modification layers.
- **Trained immunity connection (unexplored)**: In BCG-trained monocytes, does the same METTL3-DNMT1 axis regulate trained immunity gene expression? Could m6A on specific immune gene mRNAs simultaneously direct DNMT1 to those genes' promoters, creating long-lasting epigenetic memory alongside H3K18la?
- **Cancer/immune evasion**: METTL3 and DNMT1 are both overexpressed in multiple cancers. Does the METTL3-DNMT1 axis establish immunosuppressive gene body methylation patterns in tumor cells?
- **Developmental biology**: The 5mC/m6A balance as a cell fate timer is a new paradigm for how cells coordinate transcription rate and mRNA stability during rapid developmental transitions (gastrulation, organogenesis).
