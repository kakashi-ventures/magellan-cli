## Abstract

Trained immunity, a de facto innate immune memory characterized by enhanced responsiveness to future challenges, is underpinned by epigenetic and metabolic rewiring. In individuals vaccinated with Bacille Calmette-Guérin (BCG), lactate release was associated with enhanced cytokine responsiveness upon restimulation. Trained monocytes/macrophages are characterized by lactylation of histone H3 at lysine residue 18 (H3K18la), mainly at distal regulatory regions. Histone lactylation was positively associated with active chromatin and gene transcription, persisted after the elimination of the training stimulus, and was strongly associated with "trained" gene transcription in response to a secondary stimulus. Increased lactate production upon induction of trained immunity led to enhanced production of proinflammatory cytokines, a process associated with histone lactylation. Pharmacological inhibition of lactate production or histone lactylation blocked trained immunity responses, while polymorphisms of LDHA and EP300 genes modulated trained immunity. Long-term histone lactylation persisted in vivo 90 days after vaccination with BCG, highlighting H3K18la as an epigenetic mark of innate immune memory.

## Key Findings

1. **H3K18la is the epigenetic mark of trained immunity**: Monocytes/macrophages trained by BCG vaccination show lactylation of H3K18 (histone H3 lysine 18), predominantly at distal enhancer regions.

2. **Persistence**: H3K18la persists 90 days post-BCG vaccination in vivo — demonstrating long-term epigenetic imprinting from a transient metabolic stimulus.

3. **Mechanism**: Glycolytic reprogramming → elevated lactate → histone lactylation (via EP300/CBP acetyltransferases acting as lactyltransferases) → active chromatin → enhanced cytokine gene transcription.

4. **Genetic validation**: LDHA (lactate dehydrogenase) and EP300 (p300 acetyltransferase/lactyltransferase) polymorphisms modulate trained immunity strength in humans.

5. **Pharmacological validation**: Inhibiting lactate production (2-DG, oxamate) or lactylation blocks trained immunity responses.

6. **Chromatin localization**: H3K18la marks distal regulatory regions (enhancers), not promoters — suggesting it primarily acts through enhancer activation.

## Cross-Domain Significance for Session 009

- **Immunometabolism × Epigenetics bridge**: Metabolic output (lactate) directly encodes epigenetic memory via H3K18la. This is the clearest example of metabolite → histone mark → immune memory.
- **Cancer connection**: Tumor microenvironments are lactate-rich → could induce H3K18la in tumor-infiltrating macrophages → trained toward immunosuppressive M2 phenotype (see Deng et al. 2025, 28 citations).
- **Cross-kingdom gap**: Does plant defense priming (SAR via pipecolic acid → metabolic reprogramming) produce analogous histone lactylation? No papers address this.
- **m6A gap**: Does m6A mRNA methylation regulate LDHA or EP300 mRNA stability/translation, thereby controlling trained immunity duration? Zero papers on this.
- **Mechanobiology gap**: Does matrix stiffness in the bone marrow niche regulate glycolytic reprogramming of monocyte precursors, pre-programming their lactylation capacity before infection?
