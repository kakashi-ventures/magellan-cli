## Abstract

**Background**: Bladder cancer (BCa), particularly muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC), is associated with poor prognosis, partly because of immune evasion driven by M2 tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs). Understanding the regulatory mechanisms of M2 macrophage polarization via PRKN-mediated mitophagy and histone lactylation (H3K18la) is crucial for improving treatment strategies.

**Methods**: A single-cell atlas from 46 human BCa samples was constructed to identify macrophage subpopulations. Bioinformatics analysis and experimental validation, including ChIP-seq and lactylation modulation assays, were used to investigate the role of PRKN in M2 macrophage polarization and its regulation by H3K18la.

**Results**: Single-cell analysis revealed distinct macrophage subpopulations, including M1 and M2 types. PRKN was identified as a critical regulator of mitophagy in M2 macrophages, supporting their immunosuppressive function. Pseudotime analysis revealed that PRKN was upregulated during the M1-to-M2 transition. siRNA-mediated PRKN knockdown impaired M2 polarization, reducing the expression of CD206 and ARG1. ChIP-seq and histone lactylation modulation confirmed that H3K18la enhanced PRKN expression, promoting mitophagy and M2 polarization and thereby facilitating immune suppression and tumor progression.

**Conclusions**: Histone lactylation regulated PRKN-mediated mitophagy, promoting M2 macrophage polarization and contributing to immune evasion in BCa.

## Key Findings

1. **H3K18la drives immunosuppressive macrophage fate**: Tumor-derived lactate (Warburg effect) → H3K18la in TAMs → PRKN gene activation → mitophagy → M2 polarization → immune evasion.

2. **Mechanistic chain**: H3K18la (ChIP-seq validated) occupies the PRKN promoter/enhancer → PRKN protein ↑ → mitophagy clearance of damaged mitochondria → sustains M2 metabolic state.

3. **Single-cell validation**: 46 human BCa samples confirm M2 TAM subpopulation with high PRKN expression correlates with worse prognosis.

4. **Bistable immune state**: Lactylation acts as a bistable switch — once H3K18la is established by tumor lactate, the M2 state self-reinforces via PRKN/mitophagy.

## Cross-Domain Significance

- **Trained immunity vs. tumor evasion PARADOX**: BCG vaccination induces H3K18la in monocytes → trained immunity (enhanced anti-pathogen response). Tumor lactate induces H3K18la in TAMs → trained immunosuppression. THE SAME EPIGENETIC MARK (H3K18la) drives OPPOSITE immune outcomes depending on context. This is a critical anomaly.
- **Boolean logic angle**: H3K18la functions as a bistable epigenetic switch. The direction of the switch (pro-inflammatory vs. immunosuppressive) depends on which genes are lactylated — determined by chromatin accessibility context and training stimulus. Synthetic biology framing: this is a natural Boolean logic gate in immune cells.
- **Therapeutic gap**: No strategy exists to distinguish "protective H3K18la" (BCG-induced trained immunity) from "immunosuppressive H3K18la" (tumor-induced). Targeting lactylation broadly would harm both. Context-specific lactylation editing is unexplored.
