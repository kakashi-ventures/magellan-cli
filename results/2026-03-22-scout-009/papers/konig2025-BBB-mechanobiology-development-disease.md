## Abstract

The blood-brain barrier (BBB) preserves brain health through selective permeability, and its disruption is a hallmark of many neurological disorders. Mechanical stimuli such as shear stress and cyclic strain are increasingly recognised to influence BBB integrity and function, while alterations in tissue stiffness and extracellular matrix composition contribute to its breakdown during ageing and disease. Despite its importance, BBB mechanobiology remains underexplored. Here the authors highlight the central role of mechanics in BBB development, pathology, and ageing, identify key knowledge gaps, and argue that combining innovative BBB model systems with mechanical probing techniques could transform therapeutic strategies targeting brain vascular dysfunction.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Mechanical forces control BBB integrity**: Shear stress (blood flow) and cyclic strain are essential regulators of BBB tight junction protein expression (claudin-5, occludin, ZO-1). Loss of flow → BBB breakdown → neuroinflammation.

2. **Tissue stiffness changes drive BBB pathology**: ECM stiffening with aging (due to collagen crosslinking, AGE accumulation) disrupts endothelial mechanosensing → impairs BBB maintenance. Stiffness is a causal factor, not just a correlate.

3. **Mechanosensing mechanisms**: Piezo1 (mechanosensitive ion channel), integrins, and primary cilia transduce mechanical signals in brain endothelial cells. Piezo1 activation → Ca²⁺ influx → downstream transcriptional responses.

4. **Developmental BBB mechanobiology**: Blood flow onset during embryonic development is required for proper BBB formation — an entirely mechanical developmental signal for brain vascular barrier establishment.

5. **Knowledge gaps**: How BBB mechanosensing changes epigenetic landscapes in endothelial cells; whether mechanical memory (trained mechanical response) exists in brain endothelial cells; how neuroinflammation alters local tissue mechanics and creates pathological feedback loops.

## Cross-Domain Significance

- **Mechanobiology × Neuroinflammation**: BBB disruption by stiffness/flow changes → microglial activation → neuroinflammation. Mechanical signals set neuroinflammatory tone.
- **Mechanobiology × Epitranscriptomics (unexplored)**: If succinate → FTO inhibition → m6A elevation occurs in MSCs under mechanical strain, does the same occur in brain endothelial cells or neurons? Could m6A changes mediate mechanically-induced BBB gene expression changes?
- **Piezo1 × Trained immunity**: Piezo1 is also expressed in macrophages and modulates their activation. Could bone marrow niche stiffness (Piezo1-mediated) prime monocyte precursors before they enter circulation, pre-establishing trained immunity capacity?
- **Ageing × Stiffness × Neurodegeneration**: Progressive ECM stiffening with age impairs BBB mechanosensing → BBB breakdown → neuroinflammation → Alzheimer's/Parkinson's progression. Mechanical aging as a neurodegeneration driver.
