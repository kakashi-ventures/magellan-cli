# Matrix stiffening promotes chondrocyte senescence and the osteoarthritis development through downregulating HDAC3

**Authors:** Fu B, Shen J, Zou X, Sun N, Zhang Z, Liu Z, Zeng C, Liu H, Huang W
**Journal:** Bone Research
**Year:** 2024
**PMID:** 38789434
**PMC:** PMC11126418
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41413-024-00333-9
**Published:** 2024-05-24

## Abstract

Extracellular matrix (ECM) stiffening is a typical characteristic of cartilage aging, which is a quintessential feature of knee osteoarthritis (KOA). However, little is known about how ECM stiffening affects chondrocytes and other molecules downstream. This study mimicked the physiological and pathological stiffness of human cartilage using polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) substrates. It demonstrated that epigenetic Parkin regulation by histone deacetylase 3 (HDAC3) represents a new mechanosensitive mechanism by which the stiffness matrix affected chondrocyte physiology. We found that ECM stiffening accelerated cultured chondrocyte senescence in vitro, while the stiffness ECM downregulated HDAC3, prompting Parkin acetylation to activate excessive mitophagy and accelerating chondrocyte senescence and osteoarthritis (OA) in mice. Contrarily, intra-articular injection with an HDAC3-expressing adeno-associated virus restored the young phenotype of the aged chondrocytes stimulated by ECM stiffening and alleviated OA in mice.

## Key Findings

- ECM stiffening directly downregulates HDAC3 (histone deacetylase) — epigenetic enzyme mechanosensitive
- HDAC3 downregulation → Parkin acetylation → mitophagy → chondrocyte senescence
- ECM stiffness acts as a mechanosensitive upstream regulator of HDAC3 expression
- In vivo validation in mouse OA model; HDAC3 restoration rescues the ECM stiffness phenotype
- Identifies HDAC3 as a novel mechanosensitive epigenetic regulator

## Why Selected

DISEASE RELEVANCE: Establishes that ECM stiffness (Field A) directly controls the activity of a key chromatin-modifying enzyme (HDAC3 — Field C). Demonstrates this in a pathological context (osteoarthritis) with in vivo validation. Mechanically stiff ECM as an upstream controller of the histone acetylation/deacetylation balance.
