# Force-induced gene up-regulation does not follow the weak power law but depends on H3K9 demethylation

**Authors:** Sun J, Chen J, Mohagheghian E, Wang N
**Journal:** Science Advances
**Year:** 2020
**PMID:** 32270037
**PMC:** PMC7112933
**DOI:** 10.1126/sciadv.aay9095
**Published:** 2020-04-01

## Abstract

Mechanical forces play important roles in development, physiology, and diseases, but how force is transduced into gene transcription remains elusive. Here, we show that transcription of transgene or endogenous genes DHFR and PCNA is rapidly up-regulated in response to cyclic forces applied via integrins at low frequencies but not at 100 Hz. Gene up-regulation does not follow the weak power law with force frequency. Force-induced transcription up-regulation at the nuclear interior is associated with demethylation of histone H3 lysine-9 trimethylation (H3K9me3), whereas no transcription up-regulation near the nuclear periphery is associated with H3K9me3 that inhibits Pol II recruitment to the promoter site. H3K9me3 demethylation induces Pol II recruitment and increases force-induced transcription of DHFR and PCNA at the nuclear interior and activates mechano-nonresponsive gene LMNA near the nuclear periphery, whereas H3K9me3 hypermethylation has opposite effects. Our findings demonstrate that rapid up-regulation of endogenous mechanoresponsive genes depends on H3K9me3 demethylation.

## Key Findings

- Integrin-transmitted mechanical force rapidly upregulates gene transcription
- Frequency-dependence: low-frequency cyclic forces effective; 100 Hz not — force must match biological time scales
- Mechanistic pathway: force → H3K9me3 demethylation → Pol II recruitment → transcription
- Subnuclear geography matters: nuclear interior genes responsive; nuclear periphery genes resist force-induced activation due to H3K9me3
- H3K9me3 demethylation (removal of repressive mark) is the epigenetic switch

## Why Selected

KEY MECHANISTIC PAPER: Establishes that integrin-transmitted mechanical force drives a specific epigenetic modification (H3K9me3 demethylation — a repressive mark associated with closed chromatin/silenced enhancers). Directly bridges integrin signaling (Field A) to histone modification at gene regulatory regions (Field C).
