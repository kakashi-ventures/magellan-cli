# Extracellular Matrix Stiffness Regulates Cancer Stemness in Uveal Melanoma via the PIEZO1–DOT1L Axis

**Year:** 2025

## Abstract

This study aimed to investigate how mechanical signals from the extracellular matrix (ECM) regulated uveal melanoma (UM) stemness through the PIEZO1–DOT1L signaling axis. PIEZO1 expression was assessed using immunofluorescence in human UM and adjacent normal tissues. Polyacrylamide hydrogel models with tunable stiffness were used to simulate the biomechanical microenvironment in vitro. PIEZO1 was upregulated in UM tissues. In vitro, increased ECM stiffness enhanced UM stemness through PIEZO1. Functioning as a mechanosensor, PIEZO1 promoted DOT1L expression, which consequently upregulated the stemness markers NANOG and SOX2. In vivo, reduced ECM stiffness suppressed tumor growth and downregulated the PIEZO1–DOT1L axis and stemness markers.

## Key Findings

- ECM stiffness → PIEZO1 (mechanosensitive ion channel) → DOT1L (H3K79 methyltransferase) → stemness gene expression
- Complete mechanotransduction → epigenetic pathway: ECM mechanics → PIEZO1 → DOT1L → H3K79me2 → NANOG/SOX2
- In vivo validation: reduced ECM stiffness suppresses tumor stemness via this axis
- DOT1L is a histone methyltransferase that marks active gene bodies (H3K79me2)
- PIEZO1 as the mechanosensitive initiator linking matrix stiffness to a specific epigenetic writer
