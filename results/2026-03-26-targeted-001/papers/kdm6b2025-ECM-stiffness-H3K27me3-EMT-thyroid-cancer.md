# KDM6B regulates the epigenetic mechanism of epithelial-mesenchymal transition in differentiated thyroid cancer in response to extracellular matrix stiffness

**Authors:** Jiangtao Yu, Qingfeng Cao, Xiaopei Xing, Peiyu Liu, Min Wang, Xinxin Duan, Songyang Zhang

**Year:** 2025

## Abstract

This study aims to investigate the epigenetic mechanisms by which KDM6B regulates epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) in differentiated thyroid cancer cells in response to changes in extracellular matrix (ECM) stiffness. The differentiated thyroid cancer cell line K1 was used to prepare soft matrices (1 kPa), moderately stiff matrices (10 kPa), and stiff matrices (30 kPa). KDM6B was knocked down using siRNA and overexpressed using pcDNA3.1-KDM6B. The expression and activity of EMT markers and KDM6B were detected using qRT-PCR, Western blot, ChIP-qPCR, and RNA-seq. Under different ECM stiffness conditions, the mRNA and protein expression levels and enzymatic activity of KDM6B significantly increased. Increased matrix stiffness led to a significant decrease in E-cadherin and a significant increase in N-cadherin and vimentin. ChIP-qPCR analysis indicated that KDM6B binding to the promoters of EMT genes such as Snail and Twist was significantly enhanced in high-stiffness matrices and regulated their transcriptional activity through demethylation of H3K27me3.

## Key Findings

- ECM stiffness (1 kPa soft → 30 kPa stiff) directly regulates KDM6B (H3K27me3 demethylase) expression and activity
- Stiffer ECM → increased KDM6B → H3K27me3 demethylation at EMT gene promoters (Snail, Twist)
- H3K27me3 is the Polycomb repressive mark — its removal activates genes including enhancer-associated regions
- ECM stiffness → KDM6B → H3K27me3 loss → Snail/Twist promoter activation → EMT
- ChIP-qPCR confirms direct epigenetic regulation at specific gene regulatory loci
