### HYPOTHESIS H2.1: Pyocyanin-GPX4-Ferroptosis Bidirectional Axis with Quantitative Kinetic Framework
**Verdict: PASS (composite 8.15)**

CONNECTION: P. aeruginosa RhlR/QS -> Pyocyanin biosynthesis -> Host GSH depletion (t_1/2 ~ 1-2h at 10 uM PYO) -> GPX4 inactivation -> PLOOH accumulation -> Ferroptotic cell death (onset 4-8h) -> Release of lipid aldehydes (4-HNE ~50-500 uM locally) + labile iron (~10-50 uM) -> Iron captured by bacterial siderophores

MECHANISM: 4-phase bidirectional cycle:
Phase 1 (0-2h): PA reaches quorum, LasR/RhlR activates pyocyanin biosynthesis (Brint & Ohman 1995). PYO reaches 1-100 uM in CF sputum (Wilson 1988).
Phase 2 (1-4h): PYO redox cycling depletes GSH via superoxide generation + direct GST conjugation (Muller 2002). GPX4 requires 2 GSH per cycle (Ursini & Maiorino 2020); activity drops proportionally.
Phase 3 (4-8h): Without GPX4, PUFA-PE undergoes iron-catalyzed peroxidation (ACSL4/LPCAT3 pathway, Kagan 2017). Membrane fails.
Phase 4 (8+h): Released iron captured by pyoverdine (femtomolar Fe3+ affinity). 4-HNE may modify bacterial surface proteins.

KEY PREDICTION: Ferrostatin-1 reduces both host ferroptosis AND bacterial iron acquisition in co-culture.
COUNTER-EVIDENCE: FSP1/CoQ10 backup pathway may protect some cells. PYO-death may not be specifically ferroptotic. Dar et al. 2018 showed a different PA ferroptosis mechanism (LoxA).
CONFIDENCE: 7/10, GROUNDEDNESS: 8/10

### HYPOTHESIS H2.2: GPX4 as Inter-Kingdom Signal Gatekeeper with Extracellular Scavenging Budget
**Verdict: PASS (composite 7.55)**

CONNECTION: Host GPX4 activity status -> Controls PLOOH->4-HNE flux -> 4-HNE must exceed extracellular scavenging capacity to reach bacteria -> Effective only in GSH-depleted/inflamed microenvironments

MECHANISM: Quantitative scavenging budget framework:
- Gatekeeper ON (healthy tissue): GPX4 reduces >99.9% PLOOH. Extracellular GSH (2-5 uM) + albumin-SH (~600 uM) scavenge residual. Net 4-HNE to bacteria: ~0.
- Gatekeeper OFF (infection site): PYO depletes GSH. GPX4 drops to <10%. 4-HNE production increases 100-1000x. Extracellular scavengers also depleted (oxidative stress, edema dilution). Net 4-HNE exceeding scavenging: ~1-10 uM reaches bacteria.
- Critical threshold: when BOTH intracellular GPX4 AND extracellular scavenging are depleted simultaneously (PA infection, burns, ischemia).

KEY PREDICTION: GSH supplementation blocks bacterial response to ferroptotic conditioned medium.
CONFIDENCE: 6/10, GROUNDEDNESS: 7/10

### HYPOTHESIS H2.3: Dual-Pathway Ferroptosis Induction by P. aeruginosa — PYO and LoxA Synergy
**Verdict: CONDITIONAL PASS (composite 7.95)**

CONNECTION: P. aeruginosa -> Two independent ferroptosis pathways (PYO->GPX4 depletion AND LoxA->direct AA-PE oxidation) -> Synergistic host cell killing

MECHANISM: Two complementary, non-redundant pathways:
- LoxA (PA1169): Direct enzymatic oxidation of host AA-PE. Fast onset. Requires proximity. Sensitive to GPX4 counteraction (Dar et al. 2018 Science).
- PYO: Indirect GPX4 depletion via GSH consumption. Slower onset (2-8h). Long-range. REMOVES the GPX4 countermeasure, making cells vulnerable to LoxA.
- Synergy: PYO first disables GPX4 defense -> then LoxA oxidizes unprotected AA-PE -> rapid complete ferroptosis.

KEY PREDICTION: WT PA >> single mutant (phzM- or PA1169-) >> double mutant for ferroptosis induction rate.
COUNTER-EVIDENCE: LoxA expression varies across PA strains. PYO-deficient mutants still cause infections.
CONFIDENCE: 7/10, GROUNDEDNESS: 8/10
