# Paper: Ferroptosis: An Iron-Dependent Form of Nonapoptotic Cell Death

**Authors:** Dixon, S.J., Lemberg, K.M., Lamprecht, M.R., Skouta, R., Zaitsev, E.M., Gleason, C.E., Patel, D.N., Bauer, A.J., Cantley, A.M., Yang, W.S., Morrison III, B., Stockwell, B.R.
**Journal:** Cell
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2012.03.042
**PMID:** 22632970
**Published:** 2012 (original ferroptosis description)
**Status:** Peer-reviewed — founding paper of the ferroptosis field

## Abstract / Key Findings
The paper that coined the term "ferroptosis" and defined the cell death mechanism.

Key findings:

1. **Iron dependency**: Cell death induced by erastin requires iron — iron chelation (DFO, CPX) or reduction (NAC) prevents it. This establishes iron as essential.

2. **Mechanism**: Death by RAS synthetic lethality was found to require iron and to be distinct from apoptosis, necrosis, and autophagy. Ferroptosis involves iron-dependent ROS accumulation and differs morphologically (mitochondrial condensation, no nuclear fragmentation).

3. **GSH/GPX4 axis**: Erastin depletes GSH by blocking cystine import; RSL3 directly inhibits GPX4. Both lead to lipid peroxide accumulation.

4. **Morphological hallmarks**: Smaller-than-normal mitochondria with condensed cristae, ruptured outer membrane, normal nucleus.

5. **Cancer selectivity**: Oncogenic RAS mutations sensitize cells to ferroptosis — potential therapeutic window in RAS-mutant cancers.
