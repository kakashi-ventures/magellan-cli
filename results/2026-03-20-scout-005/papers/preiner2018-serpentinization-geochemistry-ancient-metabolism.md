## Abstract / Key Findings
Seminal review connecting serpentinization chemistry to the origin of ancient metabolism and modern industrial hydrogenation.

Key findings:

1. **Iron oxidation core reaction**: Serpentinization converts Fe(II)-bearing olivine + H2O → serpentine + brucite + magnetite (Fe3O4) + H2. The Fe(II)→Fe(III) oxidation is the electron source, with water as the electron acceptor.

2. **Temperature range**: Effective from 400°C down to near-ambient, with optimal catalyst conditions at 200-400°C for methane synthesis and 100-200°C for alternative pathways.

3. **Organic synthesis products**: Native metals (Fe, Ni) and metal oxides catalyze synthesis of formate, methanol, acetate, and pyruvate — exact intermediates of the acetyl-CoA pathway (most ancient CO2 fixation route).

4. **Fischer-Tropsch connection**: Magnetite (Fe3O4) is precursor to iron carbide (Fe5C2) under H2 reduction — the same catalyst as industrial Fischer-Tropsch synthesis of hydrocarbons including lipid-range compounds (C8-C18 fatty acids).

5. **Industrial parallel**: Serpentinization generates Fe3O4 + H2, the exact catalyst and reductant used in industrial CO2 hydrogenation (Haber-Bosch for N2, Sabatier for CH4).

6. **CODH conservation**: Carbon monoxide dehydrogenase (CODH), central to acetyl-CoA pathway, employs Ni0 catalysis — conserved for 4 billion years as a relic of natural catalysis at life's onset.

## Key Quantitative Data
- Temperature range for optimal serpentinization: 200-315°C (peak H2 at 200-315°C, drops outside this range)
- pH of vent fluids: 9-11 (alkaline due to hydroxide generation)
- Formate synthesis: up to 0.3 M from H2 + CO2 under hydrothermal conditions
- Acetate synthesis: up to 560 μM; pyruvate: up to 10 μM

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- Establishes that lipid-range hydrocarbons (C8-C18) are produced in serpentinizing systems via FTT chemistry
- These synthesized fatty acids could be substrates for abiotic Fenton-driven lipid peroxidation
- The Fe(II)/Fe(III) cycling is the thermodynamic core of BOTH serpentinization AND ferroptosis
- Native metal catalysts (awaruite Ni3Fe) in serpentinite produce similar compounds to cellular metabolic intermediates
