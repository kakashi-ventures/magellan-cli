# Paper: A GPX1-OSBPL8 Axis Mediates Noncanonical In Vivo Ferroptosis and Cancer Growth Suppression

**Authors:** Zhangchuan Xia, Wei Gu, et al.
**Journal:** Cell (2026)
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2026.01.009
**PMID:** 41720096
**Published:** February 19, 2026
**Status:** Peer-reviewed

## Abstract / Key Findings
This 2026 Cell paper identifies the natural mechanism behind ferroptosis, solving a decade-long puzzle about how ferroptosis is triggered endogenously (as opposed to by exogenous inducers like erastin or RSL3).

The study used CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing to screen for genes critical to ROS-induced ferroptosis. Key findings:

1. **GPX1-OSBPL8 axis**: Identifies a noncanonical ferroptosis pathway driven by reactive oxygen species (ROS)-induced phosphatidic acid (PA) peroxidation that operates without exogenous inducers.
2. **GPX1 role**: GPX1 regulates this pathway by modulating PA peroxidation, with its effects depending on OSBPL8 (an ER-membrane-associated oxysterol-binding protein).
3. **Cancer selectivity**: Unlike GPX4 (essential for all cells), GPX1 is dispensable in normal cells but essential for cancer cells due to their elevated ROS levels. Targeting GPX1 can selectively induce ferroptosis in cancer cells.
4. **Phosphatidic acid**: PA peroxidation is the key lipid substrate in this natural pathway, distinct from the canonical PE peroxidation via ACSL4-LPCAT3-LOX.

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- Demonstrates that lipid peroxidation substrates in ferroptosis extend beyond PE to include PA
- The ROS-driven (non-enzymatic?) peroxidation mechanism is more analogous to abiotic Fenton chemistry than the canonical enzymatic LOX pathway
- PA is a glycerophospholipid with diacylglycerol backbone — present in primitive membrane systems
- Supports the concept of ROS-driven lipid peroxidation as an intrinsic vulnerability of lipid membranes

**Significance**: 2026 breakthrough. First demonstration of a natural, in vivo, non-inducer-dependent ferroptosis pathway in cancer suppression.
