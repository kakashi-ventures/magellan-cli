# Paper: Advanced Oxidation Processes for Organic Contaminant Destruction Based on the Fenton Reaction and Related Chemistry

**Authors:** Pignatello, J.J., Oliveros, E., MacKay, A.
**Journal:** Critical Reviews in Environmental Science and Technology
**DOI:** 10.1080/10643380500326564
**Published:** 2006
**Status:** Peer-reviewed — comprehensive review of Fenton chemistry kinetics

## Abstract / Key Findings
Comprehensive review of Fenton and Fenton-like reaction kinetics with quantitative rate constants.

Key findings:

1. **Primary Fenton reaction rate constant** (Fe2+ + H2O2):
   - k = 40-80 M-1 s-1 (reported range across studies)
   - Temperature dependence: k4 = 4.5 × 10^8 e^(-39.5/RT) M-1 s-1
     - At 25°C: ~54 M-1 s-1
     - At 0°C: ~12 M-1 s-1
     - At 100°C: ~800 M-1 s-1 (estimated from Arrhenius)
   - Activation energy (ΔE): ~39.5 kJ/mol (from Arrhenius expression)

2. **Fe3+ + H2O2 reaction**: Several orders of magnitude slower than Fe2+ + H2O2

3. **Hydroxyl radical reactions with organics**: 10^9 - 10^10 M-1 s-1 (near diffusion-controlled)

4. **pH optimum**: pH 2.8-3.0 for maximum Fenton activity in homogeneous solution
   - At pH >4, iron precipitates as Fe(OH)3 and reaction slows
   - At neutral pH, heterogeneous catalysis by iron oxides extends activity range

5. **Heterogeneous Fenton** (iron oxide surfaces):
   - Magnetite, goethite, hematite, ferrihydrite all catalyze Fenton-like reactions
   - Ferrihydrite has HIGHEST reduction potential (+0.012 V at pH 7) versus goethite/hematite/magnetite (all ~-0.28 to -0.31 V)
   - Surface-area-normalized rates differ between minerals

6. **Alkaline pH modification**: At pH 9-11 (serpentinization conditions), homogeneous Fenton severely limited; heterogeneous catalysis by iron oxides maintains some activity but much slower.

## Key Quantitative Data
- Primary Fenton k: ~54 M-1 s-1 at 25°C
- Apparent activation energy: ~17.5 kJ/mol (from environmental engineering studies)
- Rate increase per 10°C temperature increment: approximately 1.5-2x (typical for Fenton)
- pH optimum: 2.8-3.0 (drops 10-100x at neutral pH)
- At pH 9 vs pH 3: rate reduction by 2-3 orders of magnitude

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- CRITICAL: pH gap is quantified — serpentinization fluids (pH 9-11) are 100-1000x less reactive for homogeneous Fenton than acidic conditions
- Temperature gap: Arrhenius analysis shows ~15x rate increase from 37°C to 200°C (geological temp)
- These rate constants are SHARED between both systems — same thermodynamics, different environmental conditions
- Heterogeneous catalysis by iron minerals provides a workaround for the pH limitation
