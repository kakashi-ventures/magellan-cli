# Cardiolipin Dynamics Promote Membrane Remodeling by Mitochondrial OPA1

**Authors:** Thatavarthy S, Abriata LA, Teixeira Pinto Meireles F, Zuccaro KE, Iragavarapu AG, Sullivan GM, Moss FR, Frost A, Dal Peraro M, Aydin H
**Year:** 2025 (peer-reviewed Nat Comms; preprint 2024)
**Journal:** Nature Communications
**DOI/URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-63813-4 | PMC11142133
**Status:** Peer-reviewed

---

## Abstract

Reveals how cardiolipin (CL) regulates OPA1-mediated mitochondrial inner membrane remodeling through molecular dynamics simulations, biochemical assays, and cryo-EM structural analysis.

---

## Key Findings

**CL-OPA1 Binding Mechanism:**
- Two conserved motifs govern binding: membrane-inserting loop (MIL: residues 771–781) and docking region (residues 857–861)
- CL molecules establish much stronger interactions: lipid contact residence times 5–10x longer than other phospholipids
- Charge-charge interactions between positively charged residues and negatively charged CL headgroups initiate binding
- Tryptophan residues (W771, W775, W778) insert ~10 Å into the membrane enabling hydrophobic interactions

**Phase Behavior Language:**
- Explicitly references CL's known polymorphic behavior: "CL and unsaturated phosphatidylethanolamine have the ability to organize into non-lamellar phases, particularly the hexagonal II phase"
- Does NOT quantify phase parameters or construct a phase diagram
- Acknowledges H_II phases but does not frame OPA1 activity in terms of LC phase boundary modulation

**Mechanical Properties:**
- Saturated CL species CL(16:0)4, CL(18:0)4 reduce OPA1 activity by ~70%
- Unsaturated CL species CL(18:2)4 enable highest remodeling activity
- MLCL (monolyso-cardiolipin) accumulation "alters the malleability of the membrane and restricts OPA1's ability to remodel membranes"
- Membrane curvature radii observed: 20–50 nm (consistent with ~19 nm tubule diameter)

**What the Paper Does NOT Do:**
- No reference to lyotropic liquid crystalline (LC) theory
- No packing parameter values reported
- No critical packing value threshold analysis
- No LC phase diagram constructed
- The connection between CL acyl chain unsaturation and LC phase boundary is noted mechanistically but not framed theoretically

---

## Why Selected

Best current paper on CL-OPA1 interaction mechanism. Establishes that CL's physical state (lamellar vs. non-lamellar) directly controls OPA1 remodeling activity — the functional link that an LC phase transition framework would formalize. The 5–10x selectivity and ~70% activity reduction with saturated CL are quantitative anchors for any LC-based hypothesis.
