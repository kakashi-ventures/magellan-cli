# Mitochondrial Cristae Remodeling: Mechanisms, Functions, and Pathology

**Authors:** Jianglong Yu, Yuanchun Luo, Jiacheng Lin, Zhuofan Li, Zheng Fang, He He, Chaojun Yan, Zhiyin Song
**Year:** 2025
**Journal:** iScience (ScienceDirect)
**DOI/URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2772892725000598 | PMC12615294
**Status:** Peer-reviewed

---

## Abstract

Reviews molecular control of cristae ultrastructure. Characterizes cristae as "dynamic bioenergetic compartments that remodel in response to physiological and stress cues." Key regulators: OPA1, MICOS complex, F1Fo-ATP synthase, and cardiolipin.

---

## Key Findings (Summary)

- OPA1 oligomerization narrows crista junctions and promotes ATP synthase dimerization; proteolytic processing of L-OPA1 → S-OPA1 is a regulated switch
- MICOS complex scaffolds crista junctions; Mic10 and Mic60 are core components
- ATP synthase dimerization shapes high-curvature ridges via row formation
- Cardiolipin stabilizes inner-membrane architecture; CL loss leads to cristae disorganization
- Cristae remodeling is linked to apoptosis (cytochrome c release), metabolic switching, and aging

**Liquid Crystal / LC Theory:** ABSENT. No mention of lyotropic LC phases, phase diagrams, packing parameters, Gaussian curvature modulus, or saddle-splay modulus. Review uses classical membrane biophysics terminology throughout.

**Phase Transition Language:** Absent. Uses "remodeling," "dynamics," "restructuring" — not thermodynamic phase transition vocabulary.
