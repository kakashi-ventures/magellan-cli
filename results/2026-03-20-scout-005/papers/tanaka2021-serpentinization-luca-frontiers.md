## Abstract / Key Findings
Comprehensive review of serpentinization as the geochemical cradle of LUCA (Last Universal Common Ancestor).

Key findings:

1. **Electrochemical environment**: Serpentinizing systems generate redox potentials of -435 to -830 mV — strongly reducing, sufficient to reduce CO2 to formate abiotically with appropriate catalysts.

2. **pH gradients**: Alkaline vent fluids (pH 9-11) mixing with ancient acidic seawater (~pH 5-6) creates proton gradients of sufficient magnitude for chemiosmotic ATP synthesis — a proto-ATPase mechanism.

3. **Abiotic organic synthesis pathway**:
   - H2 + CO2 → formate (awaruite, magnetite catalysts)
   - Formate → acetate, pyruvate, methane (further reduction)
   - Products mirror acetyl-CoA pathway intermediates

4. **Iron phases**: Fe0 (native iron), Fe2O3 (hematite), Fe3O4 (magnetite), FeS and FeS2 (sulfides) all present in serpentinizing environments as catalytic surfaces.

5. **Compartmentalization**: Mineral pores in serpentinite provide natural compartments analogous to cell membranes for concentrating reactants.

6. **LUCA genomic reconstruction**: LUCA's energy metabolism required only H2, CO2, N2 — matching serpentinizing system outputs.

## Key Quantitative Data
- Redox potential: -435 to -830 mV
- pH gradient: typically 6-7 units between vent fluid and ancient seawater
- Formate synthesis yield: up to 0.3 M under experimental conditions
- Acetate: up to 560 μM; Pyruvate: up to 10 μM

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- Establishes that PUFA precursors (C2-C18 fatty acids, alcohols) were present in serpentinizing environments
- Fe cycling (Fe2+/Fe3+) via magnetite and native iron is central to organic synthesis AND is the mechanism of ferroptotic lipid peroxidation
- The highly reducing conditions would have SUPPRESSED lipid peroxidation in deep vents, but could provide intermittent Fe2+ pulses when oxidizing mixing zones formed
- Key constraint: pH 9-11 is thermodynamically unfavorable for homogeneous Fenton but compatible with heterogeneous mineral surface catalysis
