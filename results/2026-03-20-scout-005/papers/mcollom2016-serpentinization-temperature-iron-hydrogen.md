# Paper: Temperature Trends for Reaction Rates, Hydrogen Generation, and Partitioning of Iron During Experimental Serpentinization of Olivine

**Authors:** McCollom, T.M., Klein, F., Robbins, M., Moskowitz, B., Berquo, T.S., Jons, N., Bach, W., Templeton, A.
**Journal:** Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.gca.2016.01.023
**Published:** 2016
**Status:** Peer-reviewed

## Abstract / Key Findings
Laboratory experimental study on serpentinization kinetics across a range of temperatures, measuring iron partitioning and H2 generation.

Key findings:

1. **Temperature range**: Experiments at 200-320°C.
2. **Iron partitioning**: Fe(II) from olivine is partitioned into serpentine and magnetite. The proportion going into magnetite (as Fe(III)) versus serpentine varies with temperature.
3. **H2 generation**: Peaks at 200-315°C; at temperatures <120°C, H2 production drops substantially because Fe(II) is sequestered into brucite rather than oxidized to magnetite.
4. **Rate constants**: Inferred serpentinization rates of olivine were 50-80x slower than older studies had reported, consistent with recent results.
5. **Products**: Chrysotile, brucite, magnetite, minor magnesite and dolomite.

## Key Quantitative Data
- Peak H2 generation temperature: 200-315°C
- Serpentinization rates revised: 50-80x slower than previously thought
- Fe(II) in olivine → Fe(III) in magnetite at 200-300°C: significant oxidation
- pH of products: alkaline (pH 9-11 range)
- Temperature threshold for significant H2 production: >120°C

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- Provides quantitative iron speciation data across temperature range
- Fe2+→Fe3+ conversion (via magnetite formation) is the geochemical analog of the ferroptosis Fe cycling
- The temperature gap (200-315°C geological vs. 37°C biological) is the key constraint to address in hypotheses
- Rate constants for iron oxidation in serpentinization allow comparison with Fenton chemistry rates in biology
