# Cardiolipin and Mitochondrial Cristae Organization

**Authors:** Nikita Ikon, Robert O. Ryan
**Year:** 2017
**Journal:** Biochimica et Biophysica Acta — Biomembranes
**DOI/URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28336315/ | PMC5426559
**Status:** Peer-reviewed

---

## Abstract (from full text retrieved)

Examines how cardiolipin (~18% of inner membrane phospholipid mass) enables cristae formation. Proposes cristae adopt "a continuously curved cylindrical bilayer capped by a dome-shaped tip" and that strain from curvature is relieved through asymmetric phospholipid distribution across monolayer leaflets.

---

## Key Findings

**Curvature Physics:**
- Framing uses curvature mechanics: cone-shaped phospholipids like CL "are tailor made to reside in, and stabilize, a curved membrane by segregating into the negatively curved monolayer leaflet"
- Crista lumen radius of curvature ~15 nm
- CL and PE segregate to the negatively curved monolayer leaflet facing the crista lumen; PC occupies the positively curved matrix-facing leaflet

**Packing Parameter Language:**
- Does NOT explicitly invoke classical Israelachvili packing parameter (P = ν/a₀lc)
- Uses functional shape language: CL's "compact anionic polar head group and four esterified fatty acyl chains results in a distinctly cone-shaped molecule"

**Phase Transitions:**
- References Schlame et al.: "tafazzin-mediated transacylase activity is lipid phase dependent, with higher activity occurring in non-bilayer phases"
- Acknowledges CL's polymorphism (lamellar, H_II) but does not frame cristae as LC phase phenomenon

**OPA1/MICOS Relationship:**
- OPA1: dynamin-like GTPase for crista membrane fusion; "responds to the bioenergetic state of mitochondria, forming oligomers that tighten cristae"
- MICOS: essential for "assembly and maintenance of crista junctions"; MIC27 binding is "cardiolipin dependent"

**Liquid Crystal Framing:** ABSENT. No reference to lyotropic LC theory, phase diagrams, or order parameters.

---

## Why Selected

Seminal review establishing the quantitative framework for CL-cristae geometry connection. Establishes what IS known (cone shape, curvature segregation, MICOS-CL dependence). Baseline for assessing what the field has NOT done (LC phase diagram, κ_G measurement, phase transition framing).
