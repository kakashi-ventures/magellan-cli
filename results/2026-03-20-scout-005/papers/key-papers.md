# Key Papers — Session 2026-03-20-scout-005

## Target 1: Mitochondrial Cristae Remodeling × Lyotropic Liquid Crystal Phase Transitions

### Cristae Remodeling

1. **Yu et al. (2025)** — "Mitochondrial cristae remodeling: Mechanisms, functions, and pathology"
   - DOI: 10.1016/j.cellin.2025.100285
   - Comprehensive review of cristae ultrastructure: OPA1, MICOS complex, F1Fo-ATP synthase dimerization, cardiolipin. Cristae are dynamic bioenergetic compartments that remodel number, length, width, curvature, and crista junction geometry.
   - Citations: 3

2. **Venkatraman, Lee & Budin (2024)** — "Setting the curve: the biophysical properties of lipids in mitochondrial form and function"
   - DOI: 10.1016/j.jlr.2024.100643
   - J. Lipid Research. Reviews intrinsic curvature of mitochondrial phospholipid pool with emphasis on cardiolipin. Proposes further investigation of ether lipids and sterols in mitochondrial architecture.
   - Citations: 14

3. **Golla, Boyd & May (2024)** — "Curvature sensing lipid dynamics in a mitochondrial inner membrane model"
   - DOI: 10.1038/s42003-023-05657-6
   - Communications Biology. Coarse-grained MD simulations showing CDL preferentially accumulates in regions of negative mean curvature. Partitioning dominated by mean curvature sensitivity; weaker correlation with Gaussian curvature.
   - Citations: 16

4. **Cardiolipin remodeling maintains the inner mitochondrial membrane in cells with saturated lipidomes** (2024)
   - Journal of Lipid Research. Loss of CL remodeling in microaerobic yeast causes characteristic loss of cristae membranes. CL remodeling's structural functions depend on overall mitochondrial lipid environment.
   - PMID: 39038656

5. **Tassan-Lugrezin et al. (2026)** — "Unconventional model organisms bend our view on mitochondrial cristae"
   - New perspectives on cristae diversity across species.
   - S2 ID: 41c0aa7c811196861df9d511a19c620371fa17e6

6. **Plouzennec, Chao de la Barca & Chevrollier (2025)** — "The Role of Phospholipids in Mitochondrial Dynamics and Associated Diseases"
   - Review of phospholipid roles in fission/fusion and cristae architecture.
   - S2 ID: ed00d6005d8a66c47f0cab768c90883716daeca3

7. **An integrative modelling approach to the mitochondrial cristae** (2025)
   - Communications Biology. DOI: 10.1038/s42003-025-08381-5
   - Computational modeling of cristae with mean and Gaussian curvature quantification.

### Lyotropic Liquid Crystal Phases

8. **Light-Driven Hexagonal-to-Cubic Phase Switching in Arylazopyrazole Lyotropic Liquid Crystals** (2024)
   - JACS. DOI: 10.1021/jacs.4c02709
   - Demonstrates photo-switchable LLC phase transitions between hexagonal and cubic.

9. **Iscaro et al. (2024)** — "Lyotropic Liquid Crystalline Phase Nanostructure and Cholesterol Enhance Lipid Nanoparticle Mediated mRNA Transfection in Macrophages"
   - Advanced Functional Materials. DOI: 10.1002/adfm.202405286
   - SM-102/monoolein inverse cubic LLC mesophases with cholesterol-dependent mRNA delivery.

10. **Millisecond Phase Transition Kinetics of Lyotropic Liquid Crystalline Nanoparticles** (2025)
    - PubMed: 40293325. Nanosecond laser-induced T-jump with time-resolved SAXS reveals structural dynamics of cubosome/hexosome phase transitions.

11. **Recent advances in lyotropic liquid crystal nanoparticle formulations for drug delivery systems** (2025)
    - Frontiers in Soft Matter. DOI: 10.3389/frsfm.2025.1658466

12. **Inverse Bicontinuous and Discontinuous Phases of Lipids, and Membrane Curvature** (2025)
    - MDPI Cells 14(10):716. Review of inverse bicontinuous cubic and other nonbilayer phases in biological membranes.

## Target 2: Acoustic Mechanotransduction × Topological Phononics

### Topological Phononics / Acoustofluidics

17. **Zhao et al. (2025)** — "Topological acoustofluidics"
    - **Nature Materials**. DOI: 10.1038/s41563-025-02169-y
    - **LANDMARK PAPER.** Valley-Hall topological acoustofluidic chips. Created valley streaming vortices and chiral swirling patterns for backward-immune particle transport. Discovered exotic topological pressure wells creating nanoscale trapping fields for DNA manipulation. 93.2% modulation in edge-state bandwidth.
    - Citations: 28

18. **Huang et al. (2026)** — "Demonstration of topological acoustic tweezing for robust mass transport"
    - Science Advances. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.adz4301
    - Topological interfaces support confined standing-wave fields that effectively trap particles; phase modulation enables stable migration robust against defects.
    - Citations: 3

19. **The 2024 Phononic Crystals Roadmap** (2025)
    - J. Physics D: Applied Physics 58(11). 40+ expert contributions covering topological, non-Hermitian, nonreciprocal, chiral, active, spatiotemporal phononic crystals. Applications in acoustic tweezers, sensing, thermal transport.

20. **Reversible tunable topological phononic crystals based upon thermo-acoustic hydrogels** (2025)
    - Microsystems & Nanoengineering. DOI: 10.1038/s41378-025-01121-z
    - Reconfigurable topological edge states via thermosensitive hydrogels.

21. **Coupling between topological edge state and defect mode-based biosensor using phononic crystal** (2025)
    - Scientific Reports. DOI: 10.1038/s41598-025-85195-9
    - Direct biosensing application of topological phononic edge states.

22. **Particle manipulations based on acoustic valley topological rainbow** (2024)
    - arXiv:2411.12381. Acoustic valley topological rainbow for particle manipulation.

### Acoustic Mechanotransduction / Mechanobiology

23. **He, Xia, Mai et al. (2025)** — "Acoustic technologies for the orchestration of cellular functions for therapeutic applications"
    - **Science Advances**. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.adu4759
    - Comprehensive review of acoustic-based cellular control: stem cells, immune cells, neural cells. Covers mechanisms from ion channel activation to signaling pathway modulation.

24. **Hahmann et al. (2024)** — "Sonogenetics for Monitoring and Modulating Biomolecular Function by Ultrasound"
    - Angewandte Chemie. DOI: 10.1002/anie.202317112
    - Review of mechanosensitive ion channels as sonogenetic mediators. Ultrasound reaches depths of tens of cm with high spatiotemporal precision.
    - Citations: 32

25. **Yang et al. (2026)** — "Precision acoustofluidics for high-throughput mechanobiology in suspension cells"
    - S2 ID: 05ffef3f0c1f48db302f8f1531b13837fd3956ff

26. **Sonomechanobiology: Vibrational stimulation of cells and its therapeutic implications** (2023)
    - Biophysics Reviews (AIP). Review of audible acoustic waves and low-vibration stimulation effects on mammalian cells.

27. **Advances in mechanotransduction and sonobiology** (2025)
    - PMC11735818. Effects of audible acoustic waves on cellular growth, differentiation, migration.

### Ferroptosis

28. **Sun et al. (2025)** — "Lipid metabolism in ferroptosis: mechanistic insights and therapeutic potential"
    - Frontiers in Immunology. DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2025.1545339
    - Comprehensive review of ferroptosis lipid metabolism. Fenton reaction and enzymatic pathways governing phospholipid peroxidation.
    - Citations: 34

29. **Balakrishnan & Kenworthy (2024)** — "Lipid Peroxidation Drives Liquid–Liquid Phase Separation and Disrupts Raft Protein Partitioning in Biological Membranes"
    - **JACS** 146(2):1374–1387. DOI: 10.1021/jacs.3c10132
    - **KEY PAPER.** Fenton-induced peroxidation enhances Lo/Ld phase separation, accumulates peroxidized lipids in disordered phase, translocates raft proteins. Links ferroptosis to membrane phase behavior.

30. **Lotfipour Nasudivar, Pedrera & García-Sáez (2024)** — "Iron-Induced Lipid Oxidation Alters Membrane Mechanics Favoring Permeabilization"

    - Iron-mediated Fenton reactions alter membrane lateral organization, reduce line tension at phase boundaries, decrease membrane integrity.
    - Citations: 15
