# Paper: Iron-Induced Lipid Oxidation Alters Membrane Mechanics Favoring Permeabilization

**Authors:** Nakano, M., et al. (multiple authorship)
**Journal:** Langmuir (ACS)
**DOI:** 10.1021/acs.langmuir.4c03294
**PMC:** PMC11603768
**Published:** 2024
**Status:** Peer-reviewed (open access)

## Abstract / Key Findings

Quantitative biophysical study of iron-induced lipid peroxidation and membrane permeabilization kinetics.

Key findings:

1. **Mechanism**: Fe2+ (from iron sulfate) + H2O2 via Fenton reaction → hydroxyl radicals → lipid peroxidation chain reaction

2. **Kinetics at physiological conditions (37°C, pH 7.2)**:
   - Permeabilization onset: ~20 minutes with 0.8 mM FeSO4 + 2 mM ascorbic acid
   - Saturation: ~80 minutes under same conditions
   - Dose-dependent: maximal permeabilization above 2.4 mM ascorbic acid

3. **Lipid susceptibility**:
   - Highly susceptible: egg phosphatidylcholine (PUFA-containing)
   - Moderately susceptible: DOPC (monounsaturated)
   - Resistant: sphingomyelin and cholesterol-enriched membranes

4. **Oxidation products**: MDA (malondialdehyde) emergence kinetically correlates with vesicle permeabilization — secondary products drive membrane disruption

5. **Mechanical consequences**: Oxidized membranes showed reduced breakthrough force (3.2 ± 0.4 nN) vs. nonoxidized (9.4 ± 0.9 nN) — 3x reduction in membrane integrity

6. **Abiotic relevance**: This study uses purely chemical conditions (no enzymes, no cells) — the closest analog to what would happen in abiotic geochemical settings

## Key Quantitative Data
- Permeabilization onset: ~20 min at 0.8 mM Fe2+, 37°C, pH 7.2
- Saturation: ~80 min
- Membrane rigidity loss: ~3x (9.4 → 3.2 nN breakthrough force)
- MDA kinetics parallel membrane permeabilization kinetics

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
