# Paper: Ferroptosis: A Regulated Cell Death Nexus Linking Metabolism, Redox Biology, and Disease

**Authors:** Stockwell, B.R., Friedmann Angeli, J.P., Bayir, H., et al.
**Journal:** Cell
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.cell.2017.09.021
**PMID:** 28985560
**PMC:** PMC5685180
**Published:** 2017 (foundational review)
**Status:** Peer-reviewed — landmark paper (>10,000 citations)

## Abstract / Key Findings
Foundational review defining ferroptosis as a distinct regulated cell death mechanism.

Key findings:

1. **Definition**: Ferroptosis is characterized by iron-dependent accumulation of lipid hydroperoxides to lethal levels. Morphologically distinct from apoptosis and necrosis.

2. **Core mechanism**:
   - Erastin inhibits cystine import (via System Xc-, SLC7A11) → GSH depletion → GPX4 inactivation
   - RSL3 directly inhibits GPX4
   - GPX4 converts toxic lipid hydroperoxides → non-toxic lipid alcohols
   - GPX4 loss → overwhelming lipid peroxidation → cell death

3. **Iron requirement**: Iron (specifically Fe2+) is required for lipid hydroperoxide accumulation. Transferrin and transferrin receptor are needed. NCOA4-mediated ferritinophagy releases iron to drive ferroptosis.

4. **PUFA-phospholipid substrates**: Primary substrates are PUFA-PLs; arachidonic acid-PE (AA-PE) and adrenic acid-PE (AdA-PE) are preferentially oxidized. ACSL4 and LPCAT3 catalyze PUFA incorporation into PE.

5. **Sensitivity modifiers**: ACSL4 overexpression sensitizes; FSP1 (CoQ antioxidant) confers resistance; DHODH provides mitochondrial protection.

6. **Disease implications**: Kidney transplant injury, neurodegeneration, atherosclerosis, cancer therapy sensitivity.

## Key Quantitative Data from Field
- Fe2+ concentration in labile iron pool: ~0.2-2 μM under normal conditions, elevated in ferroptosis
- GPX4 activity required to prevent ferroptosis correlates with cell PUFA content
- Optimal Fe3+:Fe2+ ratio for lipid peroxidation initiation: 1:1 to 7:1

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- Establishes the canonical Fe(II)/Fe(III) Fenton cycling → PLOOH → cell death mechanism in biological context
- Iron must be in the labile (chelatable) form to drive peroxidation — relevant when comparing with geochemical iron speciation
- The PUFA-PL substrate requirement is a key constraint: prokaryotes/archaea lack PUFA-PLs and are ferroptosis-resistant
