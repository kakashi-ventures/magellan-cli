# Paper: Microbial Ecology of Serpentinite-Hosted Ecosystems

**Authors:** Reveillaud, J., Rempfert, K., Bird, L.J., et al. (multiple authorship, updated 2025)
**Journal:** PMC/Current Biology (received Oct 2024, revised Jan 2025, accepted Feb 2025)
**PMC:** PMC11931622
**Published:** 2025
**Status:** Peer-reviewed (open access)

## Abstract / Key Findings

2025 comprehensive review/study of microbial ecology in serpentinite-hosted ecosystems, with important geochemical data.

Key findings:

1. **Environmental conditions**:
   - pH: 7.6-12+ (up to hyperalkaline)
   - Reduction potential (Eh): +269 to -253 mV depending on site
   - Iron concentrations: minimal (0.4-5 μM total) in fluid phase — most iron is mineral-bound
   - DIC: severely limited (<10 μM in many systems)

2. **Iron metabolism**:
   - Fe(III)-reducing and Fe(II)-oxidizing microbial populations are notably scarce despite iron mineral abundance
   - Dethiobacter alkaliphilus performs dissimilatory Fe(III) reduction using H2 and organic acids as electron donors
   - Anaerobic Fe(II) oxidation also documented

3. **Lipid composition**:
   - Glycolipids dominate (91-99% of lipidome)
   - Phospholipids <1.1% — severely depleted due to phosphate limitation
   - Betaine lipids and ceramides serve as phospholipid substitutes
   - Intact polar lipids (IPLs): 96 types identified, mostly archaeal/bacterial diethers

4. **Organic synthesis**: H2-driven reduction of CO2 produces formate, acetate, pyruvate — supporting chemolithotrophs.

5. **Key finding for bridge**: Carbon oxidation state of lipid backbones correlates positively with fluid reduction potential — organisms adjust lipid saturation based on redox conditions.

## Key Quantitative Data
- Iron in fluids: 0.4-5 μM (very low — mostly mineral-bound as magnetite/serpentine)
- pH range: 7.6 to 11.3
- Eh range: +269 to -253 mV
- Phospholipids: <1.1% of total lipidome
- Cell abundances: 10^2-10^5 cells/mL in fluids

## Relevance to Ferroptosis x Serpentinization Bridge
- CRITICAL: Phospholipid scarcity in serpentinite ecosystems due to phosphate limitation means modern PUFA-PE (canonical ferroptosis substrate) is rare in these environments
- But: primitive fatty acid membranes (non-phospholipid) ARE present and could be peroxidized
- Very low dissolved iron (0.4-5 μM) means abiotic Fenton chemistry in modern serpentinite fluids is extremely slow
- However, at mineral surfaces (magnetite, ferrihydrite), surface Fe concentrations are much higher
- Highly reducing conditions (Eh -253 mV) favor Fe(II) over Fe(III) — Fenton is Fe(II)-driven, so available Fe2+ exists
