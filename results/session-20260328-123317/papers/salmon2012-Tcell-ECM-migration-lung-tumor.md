# Within Tumors, Interactions Between T Cells and Tumor Cells Are Impeded by the Extracellular Matrix

**Authors:** Hélène Salmon et al.
**Journal:** Journal of Clinical Investigation, 2012 (key paper in field)
**PMC:** PMC3489771 (commentary/related)

## Key Findings (from published literature)
- In human lung tumor specimens, T cell migration and distribution are dictated by aligned collagen fibers surrounding tumor islets
- T cells accumulate in stroma and become trapped without reaching tumor cells
- Dense and linear matrix fibers surround tumor islets; T cells move along fibers parallel to tumor-stroma boundary (not toward tumor cells)
- Collagenase treatment ameliorates trapping and increases infiltration into malignant cell areas
- Stromal regions enriched in collagen and fibronectin fibers contain few T cells
- Motile T cells found mainly in loose ECM stromal regions; fibrotic areas devoid of lymphocytes

## Quantitative Data Available
- Live cell imaging: dynamic tracking of T cells in fresh human lung tumor slices
- T cell displacement correlates with ECM density (quantitative live imaging)
- Specific quantitative values (speeds, distances) are in figures of the original JCI paper

## Key Insight for Session 015
- Establishes the FUNDAMENTAL OBSERVATION: T cells are physically trapped by collagen fibers in the tumor stroma
- T cells move along fibers (contact guidance) not through isotropic random walk
- The boundary between loose stroma (permissive, p > p_c) and fibrotic area (restrictive, p < p_c) shows sharp T cell density transition
- This sharp spatial boundary is exactly what percolation theory would predict: a spatial phase transition in T cell infiltration probability

## Critical Gap Identified in This Paper
- Does not quantify the collagen density threshold at which trapping begins
- Does not measure T cell MSD as a function of ECM density
- Does not invoke connectivity or pore network analysis
- Gap: No paper in the field has characterized this "sharp boundary" in terms of a percolation threshold
