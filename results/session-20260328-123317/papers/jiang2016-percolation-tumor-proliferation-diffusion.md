## Abstract
Models tumor cell proliferation and diffusion in inhomogeneous host tissues using percolation theory. Tumor cells are simulated moving on percolation clusters with varying homogeneity of surrounding tissues using Monte Carlo methods.

## Key Findings
- Percolation thresholds found: 0.54 (4-neighbor lattice), 0.44 (6-neighbor lattice), 0.37 (8-neighbor lattice)
- Below threshold: tumor cells die; above threshold: tumor survives and spreads
- "Leaping metastatic diffusion" occurs via advantageous tunnels around threshold
- Critical phase transition exists between tumor dormancy and widespread invasion
- Tumor cells modeled as moving through open bonds, blocked by closed bonds
- Collagen mentioned as ECM scaffold affecting tumor invasion — but no T cell or immune cells discussed
