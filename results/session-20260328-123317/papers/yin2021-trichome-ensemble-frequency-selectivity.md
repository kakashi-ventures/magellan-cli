## Abstract
Extends the 2017 Liu et al. study from single trichomes to ensembles of trichomes on an entire leaf. Demonstrates that the distribution of trichome sizes on a leaf produces ensemble responses with frequency bands of responsiveness separated by defined band gaps.

## Key Findings
- Resonant frequencies are inversely proportional to trichome size
- Trichome size distribution on a leaf follows approximately a normal distribution
- Ensemble response spectra show "frequency bands of responsiveness separated by defined band gaps"
- Ensemble frequency range covers the sound spectrum of Pieris caterpillar feeding
- Suggests collective mechanism for plant to detect specific insect acoustic signatures
- First mode (torsional): ~6 kHz; higher modes extend to higher frequencies

## Key Engineering Analogy (Not Made Explicitly in Paper)
The ensemble behavior — multiple resonators of different sizes covering a frequency range with defined band gaps — is structurally identical to a parallel filter bank in acoustic engineering. This connection is NOT made in the paper. The T2 target proposes to formalize this isomorphism using filter-bank theory (parallel bandpass filters, each with center frequency f_n = k/L_n where L_n is trichome length) and extend it to predict MSL/MCA channel gating patterns.

## Disjointness Note
No mention of filter banks, matched filters, parallel resonators, or any acoustic engineering theory. The paper is purely mechanical/biological. The T2 bridge (formal filter-bank theory applied to trichome ensembles + MSL/MCA channel gating as demodulator) remains unexplored.
