## Abstract / Key Content

Develops e-Transmembrane device: 3D model of human intestine using EIS for label-free monitoring of host-microbe cross-talk. Key findings:

- EIS monitors **gut barrier integrity** (tight junction resistance, paracellular permeability)
- Equivalent circuit: (Rb // Cb) in series with Relectrolyte
  - Rb = barrier resistance (paracellular ionic flux)
  - Cb = barrier capacitance (transcellular pathway)
- Pro-inflammatory bacteria: ~60% Rb reduction
- Anti-inflammatory cohorts: ~20% Rb reduction with recovery
- Simplified Human Intestinal Microbiota (SIHUMI) model used

## What EIS Is NOT Used For

EIS here monitors the EPITHELIAL BARRIER response to microbiota, NOT the metabolic state of the microbiota itself. The paper explicitly acknowledges limitations in mechanistic understanding of microbial composition and metabolite content.

## Significance for T6

This is the most relevant paper for T6 — it does use EIS in the context of gut microbiome interactions. However, it measures barrier integrity as a proxy for host-microbe interaction, not direct metabolic fingerprinting of the microbial community. The bridge from EIS equivalent circuit parameters to microbial community metabolic state (dysbiosis detection, SCFA production rates, etc.) remains unestablished.

## Gap for T6

No paper uses EIS frequency sweeps to fingerprint the METABOLIC STATE of the gut microbiome itself (Randles circuit parameters = metabolic activity proxy). This is the unexplored bridge.
