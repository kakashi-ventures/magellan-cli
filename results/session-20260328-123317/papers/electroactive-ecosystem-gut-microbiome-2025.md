## Abstract / Key Content

Reviews electroactive microorganisms (EAMs) in gut context. Key findings:

- Gut-associated EAMs regulate redox balance, drive SCFA production, shape host-microbe interactions
- Corrosion microbiome principles applicable to gut EAM understanding
- EAMs linked to metabolic diseases, IBD, neurological conditions
- Electrogenicity as functional metabolic state marker

## Significance for T6

Confirms that by 2025, the gut electroactive microbiome concept is established, but the specific application of EIS equivalent circuit modeling as a metabolic fingerprinting tool for dysbiosis detection remains absent from the literature. The field has confirmed the electroactive biology but has not developed the EIS measurement framework the T6 bridge proposes.
