# Activation of lysosomal iron triggers ferroptosis in cancer

**Authors:** Xu et al.
**Journal:** Nature
**Year:** 2025
**DOI/URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08974-4
**PMID:** 40335696

## Abstract
Identifies lysosomes as the primary site where iron catalyzes lipid peroxidation leading to ferroptosis. Shows that liproxstatin-1 inhibits ferroptosis by inactivating iron in lysosomes, and that RSL3 initiates membrane lipid oxidation in lysosomes. Designed small molecule fentomycin-1 activates lysosomal iron to drive ferroptosis in cancer cells.

## Key Findings
- **Lysosomal iron as ferroptosis trigger:** Lysosomal iron catalyzes production of oxygen-centered radicals from hydroperoxides under acidic conditions.
- **Spatial localization:** Lysosomes are the initiation site, not the cytoplasm.
- **Fentomycin-1:** Targets iron-rich cancer cells (sarcoma, pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma) by activating lysosomal iron.
- **Threshold/kinetics:** No explicit kinetic parameters or iron concentration thresholds quantified.
- **Nucleation, phase transition, crystallization:** NOT mentioned. Research focuses on iron's catalytic role in lipid peroxidation chemistry, not physical state transitions of iron itself.
