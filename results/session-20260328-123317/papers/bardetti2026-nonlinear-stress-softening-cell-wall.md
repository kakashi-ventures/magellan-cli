## Abstract / Key Content

Discovers that Bacillus subtilis cell wall exhibits non-linear stress-softening exclusively in the circumferential direction. Key findings:

- Cell wall operates precisely at the critical point of the non-linear transition ("finger-trap" regime)
- Non-linearity provides negative feedback for width homeostasis: wider cells soften circumferentially → growth constrained
- Dynamic tuning of both intracellular pressure AND critical pressure of non-linearity
- The "smart material" property confers homeostasis without explicit molecular width-control machinery

## Mechanical Framework

Uses continuum mechanics, turgor pressure measurements, and AFM-based mechanical characterization. **NO fracture mechanics formalism**. The non-linear stress-softening is a pre-failure material property; fracture (crack propagation, toughness) is not addressed.

## Significance for T5

State-of-the-art 2026 paper confirms that bacterial cell wall mechanics is a richly active field — and that fracture mechanics (K_IC, G_c, Griffith criterion) has not entered this conversation at all. The frontier of PG mechanics is non-linear elasticity; fracture mechanics of the PG mesh under beta-lactam stress is entirely unexplored.
