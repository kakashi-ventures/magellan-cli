# Cell Invasion in Collagen Scaffold Architectures Characterized by Percolation Theory

**Authors:** Jennifer C. Ashworth, Marco Mehr, Paul G. Buxton, Serena M. Best, Ruth E. Cameron
**Journal:** Advanced Healthcare Materials, Vol. 4, Issue 9, pp. 1317–1321 (2015)
**DOI:** 10.1002/adhm.201500197
**PubMed:** PMID 25881025

## Abstract (as available)
"The relationship between biological scaffold interconnectivity and cell migration is an important but poorly understood factor in tissue regeneration. A scale-independent technique for characterization of collagen scaffold interconnectivity is presented, using a combination of X-ray microcomputed tomography and percolation theory. Confocal microscopy of connective tissue cells reveals this technique as highly relevant for determining the extent of cell invasion."

## Key Findings
- First study to apply percolation theory to characterize collagen scaffold pore connectivity for cell invasion
- Used X-ray microCT + percolation analysis as a "scale-independent technique" for scaffold interconnectivity
- Demonstrated that pore size and interconnectivity can be independently controlled in freeze-dried collagen scaffolds
- Identified a critical "percolation diameter" below which cell invasion sharply decreases (reported ~40 µm in some summaries)
- Cell position (invasion depth) decreases sharply in scaffolds with percolation diameter below threshold
- Cell type studied: connective tissue cells (fibroblasts/tenocytes) — NOT immune cells

## Methods
- Freeze-dried collagen scaffolds with varying architectures
- X-ray microcomputed tomography for scaffold characterization
- Percolation theory applied to quantify pore interconnectivity
- Confocal microscopy to track cell invasion

## Critical Assessment for Session 015
- **What this paper does**: Applies percolation ANALYSIS (characterization method) to collagen scaffold interconnectivity; uses percolation diameter as a structural descriptor
- **What this paper does NOT do**: Model the percolation transition itself; study immune cells; apply to tumor tissue; use active-particle percolation physics; invoke critical exponents or universality classes
- **Novelty of our target**: The session target proposes applying full percolation PHYSICS (threshold, critical exponents ξ ~ |p-p_c|^(-ν), universality, finite-size scaling) to T cell infiltration in TUMOR tissue — this is substantively different from Ashworth's use of percolation theory as a structural characterization tool for tissue engineering scaffolds
- **Gap remaining**: No paper applies percolation critical phenomena (diverging correlation length, scaling laws, universality class) to T cell infiltration dynamics in in vivo tumor ECM
