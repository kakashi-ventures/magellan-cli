# Desmoplastic Stroma Restricts T Cell Extravasation and Mediates Immune Exclusion and Immunosuppression in Solid Tumors

**Authors:** Zebin Xiao, Logan Todd, Liang Huang, Estela Noguera-Ortega, Zhen Lu, et al. (Albelda Lab, UPenn)
**Journal:** Nature Communications, 14:5110 (2023)
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-023-40850-5
**PubMed:** PMID 37607999
**PMC:** PMC10120701

## Key Findings
- FAP+ cancer-associated fibroblasts create the desmoplastic stroma that physically excludes T cells
- Depletion of FAP+ CAFs via FAP-CAR T cells eliminates structural matrix integrity
- After FAP-CAR T treatment: "marked decrease in remodeled collagen" and "rapid and profound loss of matrix" within 1–2 weeks (SHG quantified)
- FAP+ depletion enables subsequent mesothelin-CAR T cells and anti-PD-1 to work in treatment-resistant pancreatic cancer
- Mechanism: FAP+ stromal cells both restrict T cell extravasation AND prevent penetration of perivascular regions
- Two-photon microscopy of precision-cut tumor slices used for real-time T cell imaging

## Quantitative Measurements
- CAR T cell infiltration: measured as CD45.1+GFP+ cells per 0.2g tumor tissue
- SHG fiber number and SHG+ area quantified before/after treatment
- FAP-CAR T treatment: significant infiltration by day 1, increasing through day 7 vs. minimal for controls
- Collagen quantified via SHG (second-harmonic generation) for fibrillar collagen

## Exclusion Mechanism Detail
- Primary barrier: collagen-rich stromal regions surrounding tumor nests
- FAP+ cells play dual role: (1) synthesize/remodel collagen network directly, (2) restrict T cell extravasation through perivascular regions
- Removal of FAP+ cells collapses the collagen network → T cells can infiltrate

## Relevance to Session 015
- Demonstrates that the CAF-collagen complex forms a discrete BARRIER — not a gradient
- This is consistent with a percolation transition: below threshold, there is no spanning connected pathway for T cells; above threshold, there is
- FAP expression could serve as a proxy for LOX-mediated crosslinking density
- Gap: The paper does not quantify the critical collagen density at which exclusion transitions to infiltration
- Gap: No analysis of pore connectivity metrics or bond percolation parameters
