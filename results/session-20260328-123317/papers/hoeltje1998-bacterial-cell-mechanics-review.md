## Abstract / Key Content

Comprehensive review of bacterial cell wall mechanical properties. Key findings:

- Bacterial cell wall modeled as elastic shell under turgor pressure (~20 atm)
- Young's modulus of bacterial cell walls: 10-100 MPa range
- Cross-linking degree directly controls stiffness; PBP1b deletion reduces stiffness ~50%
- Beta-lactam antibiotics (via lysostaphin analog effects) reduce Young's modulus ~10-fold
- Framework used: continuum elasticity (Young's modulus, bending rigidity, Laplace's law)
- **NO Griffith criterion, stress intensity factor, or energy release rate formalism used**

## Key Finding for T5

The literature on bacterial cell wall mechanics uses continuum elasticity and shell theory, NOT fracture mechanics. The gap identified by the Scout is confirmed: Griffith fracture mechanics has not been applied to peptidoglycan in the context of beta-lactam-induced failure.

## Relevance

Provides the materials science parameters (E, turgor P, wall thickness d) needed to parameterize a Griffith-based model. The ~10 MPa stress in the cell wall (P=1 atm, w=1 µm, d=10 nm) falls in a testable range for fracture mechanics analysis.
