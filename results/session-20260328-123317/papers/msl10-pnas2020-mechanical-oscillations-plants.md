## Abstract
Demonstrates that MSL10, a plasma-membrane mechanosensitive channel localized to aerial organs, transduces oscillatory mechanical stimulations in plants. MSL10 amplifies signals in the 0.3-3 Hz range (matching wind-driven plant oscillations) and shows frequency-dependent responses.

## Key Findings
- MSL10 localizes to aerial organs naturally subject to wind-driven oscillations
- Amplifies oscillatory signals near 1 Hz; amplification range 0.3-3 Hz
- Above 3 Hz: channel activity close to static conditions (behaves as a low-pass filter)
- Plants oscillate at 1-3 Hz due to wind effects — ecologically matched frequency range
- Channel has activation, inactivation, and deactivation phases that shape frequency response
- MCA2 channel functions as a low-pass filter for acoustic/mechanical stimuli
