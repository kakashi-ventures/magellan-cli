# Modeling the iron storage protein ferritin reveals how residual ferrihydrite iron determines initial ferritin iron sequestration kinetics

**Authors:** Harrison et al.
**Journal:** PLOS ONE
**Year:** 2023
**DOI/URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article/figures?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0281401
**PMC:** PMC9901743

## Abstract
A kinetic model of ferritin iron sequestration with three steps: oxidation, nucleation, and mineralization. The model approximates the process of cytoplasmic ferrous iron internalization and subsequent mineralization into a ferrihydrite core.

## Key Findings
- **Kinetic model:** Does NOT employ classical nucleation theory. Uses empirical rate law because "little is known about the mechanism of nucleation."
- **Nucleation step:** Treated phenomenologically — no critical nucleus size, no free energy barrier calculation.
- **Mineralization kinetics:** Michaelis-Menten type with product inhibition. Saturation at 4300 Fe atoms per cage.
- **Ferroptosis connection:** NOT addressed. Paper focuses on iron sequestration to limit oxidative damage, not on cell death.
- **Dissolution kinetics:** Not modeled — only core growth from DFP intermediate.
- **Key admission:** "little is known about the mechanism of nucleation" → CNT framework not yet applied.
