## Abstract / Key Content

Develops a biophysical model of membrane response to cell wall holes in Gram-positive bacteria.

- Analytical framework: Gibbs free energy minimization (bending rigidity + stretching energy of lipid bilayer + pressure differential)
- Predicts **critical hole radius of 15-24 nm** beyond which lysis is inevitable
- Critical radius follows 1/3 power law: r_c ~ (κ/ΔP)^(1/3), where κ = bending rigidity, ΔP = turgor pressure
- Validated against Streptococcus pyogenes lysed by PlyC; 97% of observed holes exceeded threshold
- Framework is quasi-static (not dynamic crack propagation)

## Key Finding for T5

This is the CLOSEST existing paper to a Griffith-type threshold analysis for bacterial lysis. However, it uses **Gibbs free energy minimization** (membrane bending/stretching) rather than **fracture mechanics energy release rate (G_c)**. It establishes a critical threshold for hole size but does NOT:
- Apply Griffith's G = G_c criterion to PG mesh
- Calculate stress intensity factor K_I at crack tips within PG network
- Model PG crosslink defects as Griffith cracks
- Address beta-lactam-specific crosslink loss pattern

## Implication for T5 Disjointness

This paper demonstrates that energy-based threshold analysis of bacterial lysis has been done — but using membrane mechanics, not fracture mechanics of the PG layer itself. The proposed bridge (PG mesh as fractured material, crosslink defects as Griffith cracks) remains unexplored.
