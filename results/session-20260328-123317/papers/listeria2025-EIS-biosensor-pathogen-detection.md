## Abstract / Key Content

Comprehensive 2025 review of EIS biosensors for pathogen detection. Key findings:

- EIS biosensors detect specific pathogens (Listeria, Salmonella, E. coli) via impedance shifts
- Detection is based on immobilized bioreceptors (antibodies, aptamers, bacteriophages)
- Applications in food safety and clinical diagnostics
- Limit of detection: 1-3 CFU/mL achievable

## Significance for T6 Disjointness

Confirms that EIS in the microbiology domain is predominantly used for:
1. Detection of specific pathogens (discrete identification, not metabolic fingerprinting)
2. Barrier integrity monitoring (epithelial response to microbiome)
3. Biofilm characterization on surfaces (industrial)

The application of EIS as a GUT MICROBIOME METABOLIC STATE fingerprint — using frequency-dependent Randles circuit parameters to characterize community-level metabolic activity, distinguish dysbiosis, or predict disease state — is NOT described anywhere in this comprehensive 2025 review.
