# A guide to ferroptosis, the biological rust of cellular membranes

**Authors:** Veeckmans et al.
**Journal:** FEBS Journal
**Year:** 2024
**DOI/URL:** https://febs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/febs.16993

## Abstract
Comprehensive 2024 review of ferroptosis mechanisms, covering all core pathways. Covers: TFR1-mediated Fe3+ uptake, STEAP3 reduction of Fe3+ → Fe2+, DMT1 transport elevating LIP, GPX4 axis, FSP1 axis, GCHI-BH4 axis.

## Key Findings
- **Iron metabolism in ferroptosis:** LIP serves as redox engine; Fe2+ + lipid hydroperoxides → Fenton reaction → hydroxyl radicals → PUFA peroxidation.
- **LIP threshold:** "The details of how intracellular iron levels, particularly the size of the LIP, are controlled and what threshold of iron concentration is required to induce ferroptosis remain elusive."
- **Ferritin degradation:** NCOA4-mediated ferritinophagy releases Fe3+ into lysosomes; cathepsin B degrades ferritin releasing iron into LIP.
- **Phase separation:** lncFASA promotes ferroptosis via PRDX1 phase separation — a regulatory role.
- **Nucleation or CNT:** NOT mentioned anywhere. The threshold iron concentration question is an explicit open problem.
- **"Unsolved mysteries":** A referenced PLOS Biology paper identifies this as a core unsolved problem.
