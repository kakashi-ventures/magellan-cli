# Effect of biofilm physical characteristics on their susceptibility to antibiotics: impacts of low-frequency ultrasound

**Authors:** Williamson et al.
**Journal:** npj Biofilms and Microbiomes
**Year:** 2024
**DOI/URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41522-024-00544-2
**PMID:** 39160204
**Retrieved from:** PMC11333500

## Abstract
Study examining how biofilm physical characteristics (including creep compliance) affect antibiotic susceptibility, using low-frequency ultrasound (LFU) to modulate biofilm mechanics.

## Key Findings
- **Creep compliance formula used:** J_t = 3πd⁴k_BT/MSD(t) — particle-tracking microrheology formalism.
- **Power law exponent α:** Low-shear biofilms α = 0.91 (predominantly viscous), high-shear biofilms α = 0.17 (predominantly elastic). Two-orders-of-magnitude compliance difference.
- **Antibiotic diffusion:** Tobramycin diffusion coefficient increased 67-fold post-LFU (0.007×D_Aw → 0.47×D_Aw).
- **Correlation statement:** "Changes in biofilm cell viability align with measurements of creep compliance before and after LFU treatment."
- **Quantitative formula connecting J(t) to MIC:** NOT established. Correlation is observational.
- **G'/G'' crossover:** Not used.
- **Maxwell/Kelvin-Voigt models:** Not explicitly named, though power-law creep is used.

## Key Gap Relevant to T4 Bridge
This paper DOES use creep compliance measurements and DOES find correlation with antibiotic inactivation efficiency. However:
1. It does not apply the formal linear viscoelastic creep compliance framework (J(t) = J_0 + J_1(1-e^{-t/τ}) + t/η) to derive drug penetration.
2. It does not use G'/G'' crossover to predict barrier vs. permeable matrix states.
3. The correlation is phenomenological, not derived from creep theory.

This is the closest existing paper to the T4 bridge but demonstrates the gap: the formal creep compliance → MIC prediction link remains unestablished.
