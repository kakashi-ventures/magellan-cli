# Biofilm structure as a key factor in antibiotic tolerance: insights from Bacillus subtilis model systems

**Authors:** Gloag et al.
**Journal:** npj Biofilms and Microbiomes
**Year:** 2025
**DOI/URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41522-025-00864-x

## Abstract
Recent 2025 study directly correlating biofilm viscoelastic properties with antibiotic tolerance. Found that viscoelastic properties and antimicrobial tolerance to daptomycin were directly related to the presence of exopolysaccharide EpsA-O. In the absence of EpsA-O, bacteria produced weak biofilms with markedly reduced elastic and viscous moduli (G', G'') that correlated with a 3-log reduction in bacterial survival rate when challenged with daptomycin.

## Key Findings
- **G' and G'' correlation with tolerance:** Direct correlation established between storage/loss moduli and antibiotic tolerance (3-log difference in survival with vs. without EPS).
- **Model used:** G' and G'' measured but not connected via formal constitutive model (Maxwell/Kelvin-Voigt) to penetration kinetics.
- **Creep compliance:** Not the focus; uses G'/G'' measurements.
- **MIC prediction:** No quantitative formula connecting rheological parameters to MIC.
- **Mechanism:** EPS matrix mediates both mechanical stiffness and antibiotic barrier function, but the quantitative link between J(t) or G'/G'' crossover and drug concentration gradient is not established.
