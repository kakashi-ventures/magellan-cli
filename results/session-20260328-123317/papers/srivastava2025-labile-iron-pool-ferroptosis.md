# Labile iron pool dynamics do not drive ferroptosis potentiation in colorectal cancer cells

**Authors:** Srivastava et al.
**Journal:** bioRxiv (preprint) / PubMed
**Year:** 2025
**DOI/URL:** https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.07.01.662602
**PMID:** 40631145

## Abstract
Challenges the assumption that LIP expansion drives ferroptosis. Using reactivity-based probes of labile iron, the LIP did not measurably increase during ferroptosis induction with GPX4 inhibition or SLC7A11 inhibition.

## Key Findings
- **Central finding:** LIP does not expand upon pharmacologically initiated ferroptosis in colorectal cancer cells.
- **Paradox:** Exogenous iron supplementation potentiates ferroptosis even without measurable LIP increase.
- **Mechanism implication:** The relationship between LIP size and ferroptosis threshold is not as straightforward as previously assumed.
- **Threshold kinetics:** No stochastic model or nucleation framework applied to the LIP-ferroptosis threshold.
- **CNT connection:** None found.

## Key Anomaly Documented
This 2025 paper constitutes a CONTRADICTION/ANOMALY: standard models predict LIP expansion → ferroptosis, but LIP didn't measurably expand during ferroptosis induction. This is an active mechanistic debate about the threshold kinetics of iron-dependent cell death — the exact gap that CNT could potentially address.
