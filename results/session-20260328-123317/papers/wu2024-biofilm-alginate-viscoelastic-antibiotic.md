# Alginate exopolymer significantly modulates the viscoelastic properties and resilience of bacterial biofilms

**Authors:** Wu et al.
**Journal:** npj Biofilms and Microbiomes
**Year:** 2025
**DOI/URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41522-025-00718-6
**PMID:** 40490445

## Abstract
Examines how alginate EPS controls biofilm viscoelastic properties and resistance to antimicrobial treatment (NAC). Compares alginate-overproducing mucoid P. aeruginosa vs Psl-dominant wild-type.

## Key Findings
- **Alginate effect on mechanics:** Psl increases elasticity (G'), Pel increases relative viscosity (G''), alginate increases yield strain.
- **Antibiotic penetration:** Acetylation of alginate increases mesh size, directly influencing antibiotic penetration (100% penetration not achieved in most cases).
- **Multiple resistance mechanisms:** Poor antibiotic penetration, nutrient limitation, slow growth, adaptive stress responses, persister cells — viscoelasticity is one of multiple factors.
- **Creep compliance:** Not the primary analysis tool; focus is on G' and G'' changes.
- **Constitutive model:** No formal Maxwell/Kelvin-Voigt model applied to predict drug penetration.

## Relevance to T4 Bridge
2025 paper confirming the EPS rheology ↔ antibiotic penetration connection exists, but the specific mechanism (creep compliance J(t) as penetration kinetics predictor; G'/G'' crossover as barrier transition) is NOT established. The bridge concept remains unexplored.
