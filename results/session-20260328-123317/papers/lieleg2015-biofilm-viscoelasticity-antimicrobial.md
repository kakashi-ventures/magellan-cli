# Viscoelasticity of biofilms and their recalcitrance to mechanical and chemical challenges

**Authors:** Lieleg, O. et al.
**Journal:** FEMS Microbiology Reviews
**Year:** 2015
**DOI/URL:** https://academic.oup.com/femsre/article/39/2/234/635745
**PMID:** (FEMS Microbiology Reviews 39(2):234-245)
**Retrieved from:** PMC4398279

## Abstract
Biofilms display viscoelastic properties — they exhibit both solid-like elastic and fluid-like viscous characteristics. This review characterizes biofilm viscoelasticity using Maxwell elements (multiple in parallel), Kelvin-Voigt elements (spring/dashpot in parallel), and the combined Burgers model. Stress relaxation requires three to four Maxwell or Kelvin-Voigt elements to describe adequately.

## Key Findings
- **Specific models used:** Maxwell elements in parallel, Kelvin-Voigt elements, Burgers model (Maxwell + Kelvin-Voigt combined).
- **Relaxation time classification:** Fast (<5 s), intermediate (5-100 s), slow (>100 s) elements — correlating with water flow, EPS chain rearrangement, and large-scale structural reorganization.
- **Antimicrobial penetration link (quantitative):** Chlorhexidine penetration into oral biofilms was shown as "a generalized function of the relative importance of the three Maxwell elements E1, E2, E3." Penetration increased with increasing relative importance of the slow element (E3) and decreasing fast element (E1).
- **Mechanism:** Fast elements → water flow dilutes antimicrobial to ineffective concentration; slow elements → more open structure allows better antimicrobial access.
- **Creep compliance J(t):** Mentioned as a measurement mode but not explicitly linked to antibiotic efficacy quantitatively.
- **G'/G'' crossover:** NOT used to predict antibiotic barrier properties.
