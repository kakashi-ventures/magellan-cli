## Abstract
Demonstrates that inhibiting lysyl oxidase (LOX) — an enzyme that stabilizes collagen — reduces tumor stiffness and ECM content, thereby enhancing T cell infiltration and improving anti-PD-1 immunotherapy efficacy across multiple preclinical tumor models.

## Key Findings
- Tumor stiffness correlates strongly with collagen crosslinking and growth, but inversely with T cell migration
- LOX inhibition disrupts collagen fiber stabilization, reducing mechanical resistance to immune cell movement
- Combining LOX inhibition with anti-PD-1 blockade increases CD8+ T cell accumulation and delays tumor progression in pancreatic cancer models
- In vivo stiffness measurements (shear wave elastography) strongly correlated with tumor growth and ECM crosslinking, negatively correlated with T cell migration
- Second-harmonic generation imaging used to analyze fibrillar collagen architecture
