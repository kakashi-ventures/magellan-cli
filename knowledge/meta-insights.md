## Critical Screening Rules (for Generator)

1. **Thermal energy pre-screen**: For any quantum biology hypothesis, compute kT comparison BEFORE proceeding. If proposed energy << 26 meV at 300K, the mechanism must either:
   (a) Operate on sub-picosecond timescales (before thermal equilibrium)
   (b) Use thermal noise constructively (noise-assisted transport)
   (c) Be driven by external energy input (light, chemical potential)
   
2. **Citation verification**: Every [GROUNDED] claim must have a verifiable citation. If PubMed/DOI lookup fails, downgrade to [PARAMETRIC].

3. **Classical alternative check**: Before proposing quantum mechanism, verify that classical physics cannot explain the observation. If classical explanation exists and is adequate, the quantum hypothesis will be killed.

4. **Scope limitation**: Avoid universal claims. Restrict hypotheses to specific systems where assumptions hold.
