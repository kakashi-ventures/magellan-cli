# Life Sciences Discovery Guide — Primary Domain

Life sciences is the pipeline's primary discovery domain. Retrieval tools
(PubMed, KEGG, STRING), hypothesis format (molecular mechanisms/pathways),
and 60% of scoring weight (Testability + Groundedness + Mechanistic
Specificity) are structurally aligned with this domain.

## High-Potential Connection Zones (updated 2026)
- Microbiome ↔ non-gut organs (skin, lung, brain, urogenital)
- Circadian biology ↔ non-obvious diseases (cancer metabolism, neurodegeneration)
- Non-coding RNA ↔ any phenotype without known regulatory mechanism
- Metabolites as signaling molecules across organ systems
- Mitochondrial dynamics ↔ cellular senescence and aging
- Epigenetic modifications ↔ environmental exposures
- Protein misfolding ↔ materials science (amyloid-inspired structures)
- Cell-free protein synthesis optimization (cf. GPT-5 + Ginkgo results 2026)
- AI-discovered drug repurposing candidates (cf. AI Co-Scientist vorinostat for liver fibrosis)

## Key Search Patterns for Grounding
- `site:pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov [query]`
- `site:biorxiv.org [query]`
- UniProt for protein function
- KEGG for pathway analysis

## Common Pitfalls
- Correlation in epidemiological data ≠ causation
- In vitro effects often don't translate to in vivo
- Mouse models don't always predict human outcomes
- Publication bias toward positive results
- Microbiome associations confounded by diet/geography
